# 04 · Dark Elves

## Overview

The Dark Elves are among the most feared raiders in the Old World. They inhabit the continent of Naggaroth and are ruled by the iron-fisted Witch King, Malekith — a figure clad in black armour fused to a face destroyed by flame. Their entire society runs on slavery, is fuelled by hatred, and conducts its slaughter in the name of the blood-god Khaine. They sail across the oceans in Black Arks, raiding every coastline of the Old World, taking the living and leaving ash. Their existence is a nightmare that never lifts from the world's western shores.

## Origin: The Rejected One

The source of the Dark Elves is the Sundering of the Elves.

Malekith, son of the first Phoenix King Aenarion and the sorceress Morathi, was by blood the most legitimate heir to the Phoenix Throne. But the Council of Princes did not choose him — they chose Bel Shanaar. Malekith accepted publicly. He served as the highest general, campaigning everywhere to purge the remnants of Chaos. He even established diplomatic ties with the Dwarfs on behalf of the Elves. He was among the most dazzling Elven heroes of his age.

Then everything collapsed.

Malekith's mother, Morathi, was secretly a devotee of Slaanesh. She ceaselessly tried to draw her son onto the same altar. And Bel Shanaar's mounting political hostility left Malekith with fewer and fewer paths. After Bel Shanaar's death, Malekith walked into the Sacred Flame of Asuryan, seeking to burn a destiny onto his brow — and the flame judged him unworthy. He was hideously burned and nearly died in the fire.

This was the fork in all Elven history. Had he endured, let his wounds heal, he might still have been acknowledged. He did not. Burned and in agony, Malekith turned his magic upon the assembled Elven nobles, slaughtered the opposition, and launched his coup.

Civil war erupted. It lasted centuries.

In the decisive battle, Malekith attempted to unbind the magical vortex surrounding Ulthuan, intending to sink the entire continent. The plan failed. Only a corner of Ulthuan was torn away and plunged beneath the sea. Malekith gathered his followers — those who would later be called Dark Elves — aboard ships and fled west to Naggaroth, where they built a new kingdom.

## Deeds

### Society: A Spiderweb on a Blade's Edge

Naggaroth is a cold-blooded meat-grinder. In this society, mere survival is its own filter.

**Slavery** is the economic foundation. Dark Elves do not farm, mine, or haul cargo — slaves do all of that. The only source of slaves is raiding. The Dark Elf Black Ark fleets sweep the coasts of the Old World year-round: from the shores of Bretonnia to the deserts of Araby, from Empire fishing villages to the Norscan bays, every human settlement within reach of the sea is a hunting ground. Captives with some scrap of utility — skill, beauty, physical strength — may live a while longer. Ordinary prisoners are thrown straight into the mines and fields and worked to death.

**The Convent of Sorceresses** is the ideological pillar of rule. Naggaroth's magic-users possess none of the High Elves' elegant restraint — they openly practise Dark Magic (Dhar), a force that tears reality apart with the raw stuff of Chaos, more violent and more dangerous than High Magic. The political power of the Sorceresses is staggering, second only to Malekith himself, and their internal strife is bloody beyond measure. A Sorceress who shows too much strength may be summoned to a "high council" — and never seen again.

**The Temple of Khaine** is the Dark Elves' most distinctive institution. The cult of assassins devoted to the murder-god Khaine holds power in Naggaroth independent of the Witch King. They screen gifted orphans from infancy, forging them into walking blades through poison, pain-desensitisation, and death-training. A high-ranking Khainite assassin can kill an entire guard company alone, then vanish into shadow. The Witch King tolerates their existence, because these assassins are the most effective instrument for sustaining a reign of terror: any noble who entertains thoughts of usurpation may find a bloodied token on his breakfast plate the next morning.

**The Cauldrons of Blood** are the fanatical warrior branch of the Khainite cult. These warriors — women and men alike — drink the "Witchbrew," a mixture of alchemical potions and human blood, and enter a state of religious fury that does not end until death takes them. They charge into battle nearly or entirely naked, utterly indifferent to defence, seeking only to kill as many enemies as possible before dying in glory. They are the most terrifying element in Malekith's infantry line: your shield blocking their blade is irrelevant — they will simply throw themselves onto your spear-point, using their body weight to drive their knife into your throat.

**The Black Arks** carry all of this. They are not ordinary warships — they are floating fortress-cities. The keel of every Black Ark is a fragment of Ulthuan torn away during the Sundering. Those foundation-rocks, saturated in magic, were recovered by the Dark Elves and transformed through centuries of sorcerous ritual into navigable, titanic, floating bases. On board are slave pens, sorcerous towers, barracks, and harbours. A single Black Ark is a mobile invasion strongpoint. When its dark silhouette appears on the horizon, the gooseflesh is already rising on coastal folk.

### The War Against the High Elves: A Thousand-Year Blood Feud

The Dark Elves' reason for being is hatred. Hatred of the Phoenix Throne, hatred of the High Elves, hatred of every member of that Council of Princes long ago.

Since the Sundering, the Dark Elves have launched seven great invasions of Ulthuan. Each was a catastrophe, each carved a new wound into the High Elves' heart. The first several invasions nearly levelled the Outer Kingdoms of Ulthuan. The later invasions grew more insidious — combining assassin infiltration, sorcerous sabotage, and naval blockade. Malekith has tried everything.

The bitterest irony is that the killing machine the Dark Elves designed for war devours them in turn. After every failed invasion, a round of bloody purges erupts within Naggaroth. The Witch King executes a batch of "responsible" generals and Sorceresses, then elevates a new batch of crueller replacements — who will be purged after the next failure. For a full millennium, the wheel has ground across their own people.

Beyond this long grinding war, the Dark Elves have never closed the door on dealing with Chaos. Morathi's Slaaneshi cult thrives in the Naggarothi underground. Malekith maintains a surface distance from Chaos while being thoroughly riddled by his mother's penetration. They worship Khaine, tolerate Slaanesh, and occasionally trade in weapons with the Chaos Dwarfs — nominally enemies of Chaos, but in their bones sharing the same dark logic.

## Fate & Present Status

On the eve of the End Times, no one could have foreseen what was to come.

Under the pressure of the total Chaos invasion, the High Elves teetered on the brink of collapse. Tyrion — Ulthuan's greatest general — drew the Sword of Khaine, attempting to walk his grandfather Aenarion's old road and wield the curse against Chaos. Teclis — his brother, Ulthuan's mightiest mage — saw another possibility.

Teclis secretly resurrected Malekith and brought him back to Ulthuan. Before the flame of Asuryan, Teclis spoke the truth that had been hidden for a thousand years: the reason Malekith was burned the first time was not that he was unworthy — it was that the trial was not yet complete. He needed first to walk a road of darkness. To be rejected, to be burned, to live a thousand years in hatred — none of it was punishment. It was preparation.

Malekith walked into the flame a second time. This time he did not burn. His scars healed. The black armour melted away. A whole Phoenix King stepped out — no longer needing an iron shell to hold himself together.

High Elves, Dark Elves, Wood Elves — three kindreds became one. This was something his mother Morathi could not do, something not a single Phoenix King before him could do, something brought back by a man who had spent a thousand years in the dark.

The Old World was destroyed in the end. Chaos won. But the Elves, as a people, survived.

## Verdict

The Dark Elves are a fascinating population. You will find they are rotten through and through — and yet it is hard to hate them completely.

On the one hand: slavery, blood sacrifice, indiscriminate raiding — none of it can be washed clean. Pick any Dark Elf noble at random and the tally of lives on his hands probably exceeds the number of strangers an ordinary human in the Old World will meet in a lifetime. Naggaroth is a killing machine that ran for two thousand years; every gear is wet with blood.

On the other hand: the reason they exist at all is that Malekith was rejected. The Council of Princes was not without grounds to refuse him — a candidate tainted at the root with Chaos could not be trusted — but Malekith's subsequent fury was, in essence, a rejected man's revenge upon the world. His mother worshipped a dark god, the Council distrusted him, the flame pronounced him unworthy — and so he resolved to destroy everything.

And then he sustained that hatred for a thousand years.

More intriguing still is the ending. A Dark Elf story told for a thousand years reaches its final chapter — and suddenly Malekith picks up an entirely different thread. All those hatreds, massacres, power struggles, the broken and glued-together wreck of a body — all of it was waiting for this one sunrise. This is not a last-minute whitewash. The Warhammer universe has a logic deep in its foundation: the gods of this world do not hand out answers easily. Malekith spent a thousand years between life and death and earned the bitterest path to vindication in all Elven history.

None of which, of course, changes the total count of lives he has taken. It may only make you pause for one second while counting.
