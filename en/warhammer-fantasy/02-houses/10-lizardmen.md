# 10 · Lizardmen

## Overview

The Lizardmen were the first race the Old Ones ever created. They dwell in the deepest rainforests of Lustria, with pyramid-temples for cities, and the golden plaques of the Old Ones for law. For ten thousand years they have never deviated one step from the Great Plan. This race did not arise through natural evolution, but through the genetic engineering of the Old Ones: the Slann as priests, the Saurus as warriors, the Skinks as scribes and artisans — tier upon tier, each with its own function. When Chaos flooded in and the Old Ones fled in haste, only the Lizardmen remained behind, guarding cities full of plaques and star-charts, executing a mission they themselves may not entirely understand.

They are the loneliest civilisation on this world. They never engage in diplomacy. They never compromise. They never question. For ten thousand years, exactly as they did on day one, they have waited in the rainforest pyramids for the Old Ones to return — but the Old Ones have never come back.

## Origin

In the world's young age, a group of beings called the Old Ones descended from the stars. They were a higher civilisation surpassing the comprehension of any race today, masters of genetic sculpting and dimensional folding. The first thing the Old Ones did upon arriving on this planet was to build the two polar gates, drawing Chaos energy to power their technological operations.

Then they began creating life. The first creations were the Lizardmen.

The Lizardmen are not the product of natural evolution. They are a species manufactured from the spawning pools by the Old Ones, using reptilian genes as the template and precise programming. The Old Ones divided them into several functionally distinct sub-species: the Slann, of enormous size and staggering brain capacity, capable of directly manipulating the Winds of Magic, serving as the Old Ones' deputies and priests; the Saurus, cold-blooded and fearless, designed as tireless warriors; the Skinks, small-bodied but nimble-fingered and keen-minded, handling scribal work, craftsmanship, and ritual assistance; and colossal beasts — Terradons, Bastiladons, Carnosaurs — serving as heavy weapon platforms on the battlefield.

The Old Ones' goal was never fully explained to the Lizardmen. They left only a set of instructions — the Great Plan — engraved upon golden plaques and distributed among the temple-cities. What the Great Plan's ultimate purpose is, the Lizardmen themselves do not know. They know only: execute.

## Deeds

### Society

Lizardmen society is built not on culture or tradition, but on the genetic coding of the spawning pools. Before each Lizardman is even born, its role, capabilities, and mentality have already been determined. There is no such thing as "choosing a path in life" — the Old Ones never built that option in.

**The Slann** occupy the apex of society, the most direct proxies of the Old Ones. They were personally created by the first generation of the Old Ones, and in the ten thousand years since, no new Slann have ever been spawned. A single Slann's magical depth can match hundreds of human archmages; they can simultaneously manipulate multiple Winds of Magic and discern the star-chart prophecies of the Old Ones in meditation. They spend most of their time in a state of deep trance — their souls wandering among the Winds, interpreting the heavens. Centuries of slumber, one decree upon waking, then back to sleep. Lord Mazdamundi of Hexoatl can cause earthquakes and shift ocean currents while awake. Lord Kroak — the most legendary Slann of the Lizardmen — died in battle during the first Chaos incursion, but his soul refused to dissipate. To this day he sits as a mummified corpse atop the Itza pyramid, unleashing world-shattering power in moments of crisis.

**The Saurus** form the second tier, born to be warriors. Their thinking is exceedingly simple: fight, obey, guard. They crawl out of the spawning pool knowing how to wield weapons, form ranks, and identify enemies. No fear, no hesitation, no fatigue, no thought of anything beyond combat. A Saurus phalanx advances like a living wall, grinding over everything. The wounded continue hacking until their limbs break; the dying lie down in silence, soundless. Commanders are called "Oldbloods," veteran Saurus who have survived thousands of years, their scales hard as tempered steel. The Temple Guard they lead protect a single Slann for life — even if the Slann slumbers for centuries, they neither eat nor drink, and never move a single step.

**The Skinks** form the third tier, carrying the most diverse functions: scribal record-keeping, sacrificial rites, construction, beast-tending, communication. They are also responsible for javelins, blowpipes, and skirmishing on the battlefield. Among them are Chameleon Skinks, capable of vanishing from sight, specialising in assassination. Skink Priests are the core of the Lizardmen resurrection system — Lizardmen cannot breed naturally, but as long as a body remains relatively intact after death, it can be resurrected through the rituals of the Old Ones. After an army is wiped out, so long as the priests survive, it will soon be standing again.

Beneath the pyramid-cities are the great beasts created by the Old Ones: Bastiladons bearing solar-engine crystals that project incinerating beams of searing light; Carnosaurs that can swallow half a warhorse in one bite; Terradons that swoop from the sky, their Skink riders diving to deliver poison. On the battlefield, these beasts are living tanks.

This four-tiered society has run on the programmed logic of the plaques for over ten thousand years without ever falling into disorder — every individual, at the genetic level, lacks the impulse to rebel.

### Lost Cities and Pyramids

Lustria is dotted with dozens of temple-cities, each constructed precisely according to the geometric and magical network plans laid down before the Old Ones departed. At the centre of each city is an immense stepped pyramid, its apex the Slann meditation chamber, its interior stacked with golden plaques and star-chart arrays. The pyramids are linked to one another through a network of geomantic ley-lines, forming a continent-spanning magical matrix. The Slann can instantaneously transmit magical force to any city through this web.

The most important cities include: Hexoatl, the City of the Sun, where Mazdamundi lies in long sleep; Itza, the First City, the sanctuary Kroak guards in mummified eternity; Tlanxla and Xhotl, holding the eastern and western flanks of the jungle; and the already-fallen Pahuax and Sotek — reduced to ruins.

Every temple-city is a stone encyclopaedia. The four walls of every pyramid are carved with the star charts and prophecies of the Old Ones. Skink Priests trace and copy them generation after generation, without necessarily understanding their meaning. To the Lizardmen, fulfilling the Great Plan does not require understanding — only execution.

### After the Old Ones Departed

When the polar gates collapsed and Chaos flooded in, the Old Ones abandoned this planet, fleeing through the star gates and vanishing into the depths of the cosmos. To Men, Elves, and Dwarfs, the Old Ones are myth. To the Lizardmen, the Old Ones are the creators, the masters, the authors of the Great Plan. The Old Ones' departure meant the Lizardmen had lost their commanders — but retained every instruction.

For ten thousand years since, Lizardmen history has been the history of the continuous execution of the Great Plan. The Slann interpret the stars in meditation, piecing together the next step. The Saurus and Skinks eliminate anything that threatens the Old Ones' legacy.

They have no allies. Human explorers who venture into Lustria are, nine times out of ten, executed as intruders. Skaven raiding the plaques have triggered millennia of subterranean blood-war, rat-corpses piled high enough to dam rivers. Dark Elf slave-ships approaching the coast are reduced to ash by Carnosaurs and solar engines. Daemons of Chaos are their natural mortal enemies. Across the same two thousand years in which the Empire rose and fell, the Elves split and warred, and the Dwarfs slowly declined, the Lizardmen have done exactly one thing: guard the pyramids, decode the plaques, and resist Chaos.

## Fate & Present Status

The End Times were no surprise to the Lizardmen. Millennia ago, the Slann had already foreseen the apocalypse in the star charts — the final phase of the Great Plan was made for precisely this hour.

As Archaon invaded from the north, the Skaven launched their own all-out assault upon Lustria. Morrslieb plunged towards the earth, and the entire rainforest was soaked in a rain of warpstone. Temple-city after temple-city came under siege; though the Saurus legions fought ten to one, the disparity in numbers could not be overcome.

At the decisive moment, the Slann made the most important decision in their race's history. Mazdamundi led the surviving Slann in a joint ritual of unprecedented scale — offering themselves as the sacrifice, they drew the souls of the entire Lizardmen race free of the collapsing Old World and lifted them into the stars. Temple-ships rose from the pyramid summits, carrying the Lizardmen's souls into the cosmos. The majority of the Slann perished, including Mazdamundi. Kroak's spirit, in the final moment of Itza's collapse, flared with a radiance that stretched across the horizon, hurling back the Daemon host and buying the critical seconds needed for the ships to ascend.

The Old World collapsed, but the Lizardmen were not wholly extinguished. Their civilisation endured in another form.

### The Age of Sigmar

After the Mortal Realms took form, the Lizardmen's remnants became the Seraphon — the celestial Lizardmen. They are not flesh, but beings of order manifested from the memories of the Slann and azyric magic. The Slann's souls continue to meditate in the aether, their memories summoning the star-forged avatars of fallen Saurus, Skinks, and great beasts, forming celestial hosts.

The Seraphon are the oldest and least understood members of the Grand Alliance of Order. They still follow the Great Plan of the Old Ones, though the Old Ones have departed this universe entirely. When Sigmar assembled the gods to form the Pantheon of Order, the Slann made their characteristically Lizardman reply: no refusal, no delight, simply the silent dispatch of their celestial hosts — the Great Plan says to resist Chaos, and the Chaos of the Mortal Realms is still Chaos.

They still do not engage in diplomacy. They still do not explain. They still do not complain. They merely hang silently in the starry firmament, just as they did in the rainforest ten thousand years ago, continuing to execute a mission they themselves may not fully understand.

## Verdict

The Lizardmen as a race are loyal to the ultimate extreme — and tragic to the same extreme. Ten thousand years ago the Old Ones departed, leaving the Great Plan and the golden plaques, but leaving no date of return. The Lizardmen have guarded this legacy as if guarding a first principle, treating it as heaven's mandate, without complaint, without wavering. This is a loyalty so absolute that it hurts to witness.

But their tragedy lies precisely here: they are tools, and they have never known they are tools. The Old Ones created them as a craftsman forges a sword. The sword's sole meaning is to obey the will of the hand that wields it; the sword itself does not need meaning. The Lizardmen are exactly that sword — the sharpest in the whole world — and they have never once achieved self-awareness.

Men find redemption in faith. Elves, after falling, are reborn. Dwarfs resist decline through the power of the grudge. Greenskins prove their existence through violence. And the Lizardmen? Ten thousand years as day one. They do not ask, do not doubt, do not wonder why they fight, why they die, what they should do after winning. They have purely functioned, like this, for over ten thousand years.

Later ages often call the Lizardmen the "foes of Chaos" and the "shield of Order." Not without reason, but not enough. The Lizardmen are the residual image of the Old Ones' will, the lines of code of the Great Plan, the purest executors in the cosmos. They do not need to be understood — because they themselves have no need to understand.

Perhaps this was precisely the design of the Old Ones. In the eyes of the Old Ones, a race that needs no meaning is a weapon that can never be shaken.
