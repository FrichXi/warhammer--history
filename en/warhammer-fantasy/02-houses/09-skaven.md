# 09 · Skaven

## Overview

The Skaven are the ratmen, and they are the true masters of the Old World's underground empire. Their domain erects no boundary markers and plants no banners, yet it stretches wider than any surface civilisation — from the northern ice wastes to the southern jungles, from the Worlds Edge Mountains to the seabed beneath Ulthuan, their tunnels spread like a spider's web in all directions. There is no place without a rat-nest.

This race takes betrayal as its order and conspiracy as its faith. Their god is the Horned Rat, the thirteenth god of Chaos, whose status the Four refuse to acknowledge — but the Skaven are utterly convinced of it. The Council of Thirteen commands the four Great Clans and billions of rats, surviving underground for millennia on mutual suspicion and omnipresent infiltration.

## Origin

Not even the Skaven themselves can clearly explain their own origins — and this is the least "racial" thing about them. They were not created. They did not evolve. They were polluted into being.

According to legends even the Skaven cannot keep straight, beneath some unnamed human city or Dwarf hold, generation after generation of rats gnawed upon warpstone — the dark-green crystalline condensate of Chaos energy. Over generations, the corruption sank into their marrow. They began to stand upright, grip weapons in their forepaws, and their brains spawned thoughts that ought never to have existed there. Another, more ancient theory points the Skaven back to the Old Ones — a failed experiment of that prehistoric creator race, created and then abandoned beneath the earth. The attitude of the Lizardmen towards Skaven, which resembles less extermination than detoxification, seems to corroborate this ancient grievance.

Whatever the truth, the empire the Skaven built in silence beneath every civilisation's feet is far larger than any surface race realises.

## Deeds

### Society

Skaven society operates on a single core principle: whoever is more ruthless stands higher. And this "ruthlessness" is not limited to enemies — it applies even more critically to one's own kind.

**The Council of Thirteen** is the supreme governing body. Twelve seats are held by the most powerful warlords and clan chieftains, none of whom trusts any of the others. The thirteenth seat is said to be occupied by the Horned Rat himself — in practical terms, this means: anyone who tries to seize absolute power will be torn apart in an instant by the other eleven acting in concert.

**The Grey Seers** are a parallel pillar of priestly power. White-furred, short-horned, they claim to directly channel the Horned Rat's oracles and monopolise Skaven magic — a fusion of the dark Wind of Magic with warpstone, immense in power, unstable, and carrying a terrifying backlash rate. Grey Seers frequently die from spell-misfires, but not one has ever stopped on that account — because falling out of favour with the Council is even more lethal than magical backlash.

The true power of the Skaven is distributed among the **four Great Clans**:

**Clan Skryre** controls warpstone technology. Lightning cannons, flame-throwers, poison-gas globes, drilling machines, and the Screaming Bell — all warpstone-driven, all with a significant probability of detonating their own operators. But the Skaven reasoning is: blow up one more enemy, and losing three of your own is still a profit.

**Clan Pestilens** controls plague. In 1111 IC, the Black Plague they unleashed killed nearly half the Empire's population, after which their armies boiled up from below. Emperor Mandred fought a desperate counter-attack and killed the Skaven warlord, earning the title "Skavenslayer" — Clan Pestilens's greatest humiliation in its history.

**Clan Eshin** controls assassination. They once travelled far to the east and learned the arts of stealth and poison from a human assassin cult, returning to grind out an even more venomous and filthier Skaven assassin system. Any Skaven warlord sleeps with his bed surrounded by traps — you never know whether a rival has already paid Clan Eshin in warpstone.

**Clan Moulder** controls beast-breeding. They inject warpstone directly into living creatures to spawn monsters: Rat Ogres, Hell Pit Abominations, Giant Rats. Moulder's creations frequently turn around on the battlefield and trample their own side, but they win on being big enough and numerous enough.

### The Under-Empire

The Skaven subterranean realm has a formal name: the Under-Empire. Its mode of operation is completely different from any other empire. Surface nations use borders, walls, and armies to delimit their territory. The Under-Empire has none of these — it has only tunnels.

Over unknown thousands of years, the Skaven have burrowed a globe-spanning tunnel network beneath the Old World. Beneath every human city there are Skaven tunnels; the foundations of every Dwarf karak have been infiltrated by rat-runs; even beneath the isle of Ulthuan's Elves, traces of Skaven tunnels have been found. Through this subterranean web, Skaven armies can appear beneath the granaries, wells, and temples of any surface nation without the slightest warning.

But the Under-Empire is not a unified entity. The four Great Clans each hold their own territories and spheres of influence. The decrees of the Council of Thirteen, once they leave the capital city, are interpreted at will by local warlords. The Skaven's civil-war frequency is probably higher than any other race's — but every time a civil war ends, so long as a few breeding-mothers survive, a few decades later there is another massive force capable of boring through the planet's crust. The true horror of the Skaven is not that they win any given battle — it is that you can never truly exterminate them. Like a tumour, cut away one piece and three more have already grown elsewhere.

## Fate & Present Status

The End Times was the apocalypse of the Old World — and the most glorious chapter in Skaven history. As Chaos invaded from the north and Men and Elves scrambled in desperation, the Skaven seized a once-in-ten-thousand-years opportunity. The defensive lines of every surface nation loosened simultaneously; every eye was fixed on the Chaos host in the north. So the Skaven boiled out of every sewer, every mineshaft, every chasm.

Around 2525 IC, the Council of Thirteen finally issued the largest surface-attack directive in Skaven history. Clan Pestilens released plagues again. Clan Skryre pushed every doomsday weapon accumulated over centuries onto the surface. Clan Eshin assassins slipped into every castle among the refugees. Clan Moulder's monstrosities collapsed countless walls.

But the most astonishing action came from the Grey Seer Thanquol. This individual — or this rat — had spent his entire life trying to claw onto the Council of Thirteen and had failed repeatedly. He was arrogant, paranoid, and catastrophically unlucky, but his magical talent genuinely ranked among the very finest of the Grey Seers. In the final hour of the End Times, beneath the baleful light of the Chaos moon Morrslieb, Thanquol performed a summoning ritual. He used warpstone as the offering and the screaming of billions of Skaven as the conduit, pulling the Horned Rat's essence from the Realm of Chaos into the material world.

The ritual succeeded. Morrslieb began its descent towards the Old World. The Horned Rat manifested upon the moon's face — not as a symbol, but as a true, sky-rending monstrosity. The Skaven deity had finally received the recognition the four Chaos Gods had always denied him: henceforth, Chaos was not four gods — it was five.

At the moment of Morrslieb's impact, every magical node in the Old World shuddered. The final blow that crushed the Old World, however, was delivered by Archaon — the Everchosen descended into the deep tunnels beneath Middenheim and severed the last leyline holding the world together. As the Old World collapsed, the Skaven had long since burrowed into the deepest tunnels, waiting in the dark for the new world to be born.

### The Age of Sigmar

After the destruction of the Old World, the Mortal Realms precipitated from Chaos — and the Skaven were almost the only race to survive wholly intact. Other races required divine ascension, realm-migration, and racial schism just to gain a foothold in the new world. The Skaven did not. Their strategy has always been to burrow downward; burrow deep enough, and the world can collapse overhead without hitting them.

In the Age of Sigmar, the Skaven Under-Empire has expanded across all eight Mortal Realms. The Horned Rat has formally ascended as the fifth Chaos God — no longer a delusion of self-aggrandising ratmen, but a reality acknowledged among the pantheon. The Skaven outnumber the Men, Elves, and Dwarfs of Sigmar's age combined. They need no Realmgates to cross between Realms — their gnawholes are their own Realmgates.

## Verdict

The Skaven do not pursue the sacred mission of world-ending like the Chaos Warriors, nor sink into obsessions with power and immortality like the vampires, nor revel in purely violent joy like the Greenskins. The Skaven are a specimen of a twisted species finding a path to survival in extremis. Their selfishness, treachery, short-sightedness, and cruelty are, in the final analysis, the imprint left by that first mouthful of warpstone. From the moment of their birth, every Skaven knows: in this world, only you and your next litter of pups are worth caring about. Everything else can be traded, betrayed, stepped upon.

But this is also the Skaven's fundamental weakness. They have never learned to cooperate. Their empire is sustained by terror, not loyalty. Their plans always go awry during execution because of internal conflict. Every unified action of the Council of Thirteen is a miracle — and behind every miracle lie the seeds of the next civil war. The Skaven can occupy the entire subterranean world, yet can never truly conquer the surface — not because they are not strong enough, but because the moment a breach appears in the surface lines, the first thought of every Skaven warlord is not to press the advantage, but to seize the tunnels of the rival next door while he is away on campaign.

Thanquol is the distillation of Skaven fate. He was supremely gifted, appallingly unlucky, and spent his whole life climbing — then falling. Yet it was this luckless rat who, in the final hour of the End Times, completed the greatest ritual in Skaven history. The black humour in this is something the Skaven themselves will never grasp: all of their greatest achievements happen in the chaos after their plans have completely collapsed.

The ascension of the Horned Rat to the status of fifth Chaos God, viewed against the great backdrop of the Warhammer universe, is a piquant symbol. The four Chaos Gods represent violence, mutation, decay, and excess — each cutting to the heart of some extreme emotion. What the Horned Rat represents — betrayal and selfishness — is precisely the portrait of the internal relations among the Chaos gods themselves. In a sense, the Horned Rat is not merely the god of the Skaven — he is the never-acknowledged dark side of Chaos itself, finally having found his own face.
