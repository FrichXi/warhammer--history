# 05 · Chaos

## Overview

Chaos is not a race, nor a nation. It is the dark face of the universe's primal nature — older than any civilisation of the Old World, more fundamental than any god. When the Men of the Old World were still rubbing sticks together in their tribes, Chaos was already gazing down upon this continent from the uttermost north.

At Chaos's heart are the four Dark Gods: Khorne (the Blood God, the Lord of Skulls, who presides over violence and slaughter), Tzeentch (the Changer of Ways, who presides over magic, scheming, and mutation), Nurgle (the Plague Father, who presides over decay, disease, and the cycle of rot), and Slaanesh (the Prince of Excess, who presides over desire, ecstasy, and the extremes of sensation). The Four are consumed by mutual hatred and ceaseless war — yet share an eternal consensus on one point: the annihilation of Order. Every civilisation of the Old World — Men, Elves, Dwarfs — survives in the shadow that Chaos casts.

## Origin: The Coming of Chaos

The first time Chaos flooded into the Old World in force was roughly 5600 years before the Imperial Calendar.

The culprits were the Old Ones — a mysterious ancient race of unknown provenance, who constructed polar gates at the world's northern and southern extremities, drawing Chaos energy from the Realm of Chaos and burning it as fuel for their various projects. The gates operated for untold ages, and the Lizardmen they had created as servants faithfully executed a "Great Plan" whose full dimensions they did not grasp. Then, one day, the gates collapsed.

The collapse of the polar gates was not a physical explosion — it was a structural tear in reality itself. The energies of the Realm of Chaos poured into the material world like a burst dam, and the Winds of Magic turned wild. The Old Ones abandoned everything — abandoned the Lizardmen, abandoned their ongoing experiments, abandoned this world — and vanished without a trace.

The races left behind had to face the fallout alone. The Elven Phoenix King Aenarion drew the god-killing Sword of Khaine and held the first wave at the walls of Ulthuan, dying in the effort. The Dwarf ancestor Grimnir marched alone into the Chaos Wastes and was never heard from again. The corruption of the Chaos Dwarfs also began here: a Dwarf expedition isolated in the far north knelt in despair before a dark forge-god named Hashut.

The Old World's power structure was completely reshuffled. Men and Dwarfs rose. The Elves fell back to Ulthuan. All subsequent history is the story of Chaos attempting, over and over, to finish what it started.

## Deeds

### The Northern Chaos Wastes

The far north and far south of the Old World are two expanses of permanently blighted land — the Chaos Wastes.

The northern Chaos Wastes cover an area equivalent to the entire Old World, but this land is not "inhabitable wilderness." Physical law has dissolved here. Time and space twist like melting wax. A stretch of ice plain today may be a boiling lake of blood tomorrow. A bird passing overhead might take three years to disappear across the horizon. Any mortal who walks into this wasteland will suffer mutation of body and soul under the assault of the Winds: horns sprouting from the skull, a second pair of arms growing from the ribs, eyes kindling with fire, skin hardening into bronze or iron. In the Chaos Wastes, mutation is not a curse — it is the air.

Scattered across the Wastes are innumerable Chaos shrines, forgotten altars, and unnamed citadels. At the northernmost edge lies the Realm of Chaos itself — the boundary where the material world meets the Aethyr. A mortal who reaches it will see a sky burning eternally with four colours of aurora: blood-red, crystalline blue, viscous green, and deep purple.

These Wastes are also the home of the Warriors of Chaos. They do not farm, because the soil will yield no crop. They do not build houses, because Nurgle will rot the timbers to mud in a single day. Their existence has only one activity: war. Surrender to the northern Wastes, and you become part of Chaos. Resist, and you become the weapon Chaos will use against the next people behind you. There is no third road.

### Warriors of Chaos

Warriors of Chaos are not Daemons — they are mortals, or were once.

A northern tribesman, in the frenzy of slaughter, feels an unspeakable rage flood his body — henceforth his strength redoubles, he lives only for blood: Khorne has marked him. A banished shaman, desperate for the power to take revenge, feels a blue bird alight on his shoulder and whisper, "Tomorrow your enemy will die beneath white wolf claws" — and the next day it comes true; fifteen years later, deep in a labyrinth of schemes, he has murdered the one he swore to protect: Tzeentch. A mother watching her children rot one by one in a plague kneels in the mud and begs for an end to the pain — Nurgle grants her eternal "new life": she feels no more pain and becomes a living vessel of pestilence. A mercenary captain, sampling every pleasure in a thousand feasts, feels always that "just one more thing" is missing — and Slaanesh gives him that one thing: he becomes a creature forever chasing satisfaction and forever incapable of reaching it.

A mortal who gives himself to Chaos receives the "gifts" of the Dark Gods — marks, mutations, daemon-forged armour, hunger that can never be sated. The more marks he bears, the greater his power — and the less he resembles a man. The ultimate goal of a Chaos Warrior is to amass enough deeds and enough divine favour to be elevated as a **Daemon Prince** — to shed the last of mortality and become an immortal being within the Realm of Chaos. The road is nine dead for every one who walks it. Most will become Chaos Spawn along the way: a mindless mass of howling, biting flesh, discarded by all four gods.

The supreme commander of the Warriors of Chaos is the **Everchosen** — the mortal champion jointly chosen by all four Chaos Gods, whose task is to muster the full forces of Chaos and launch the final war against the world. The last Everchosen of the Old World was Archaon, who personally ended that world in 2528 IC.

### Norsca and the Kurgan

South of the Chaos Wastes and north of Kislev lies the homeland of the northern tribal peoples. The two most famous are the Norscans and the Kurgan.

The Norscans inhabit the Norscan peninsula, the northernmost coastal region of the Old World. They are deadly sailors, hunters, and raiders, ranging the coasts of every major Old World port in their dragon-prowed longships, burning and plundering. Their society observes a rough-hewn code of honour: a Norscan warrior who dies in his bed rather than on the battlefield earns a soul condemned to the meanest corner of the Chaos Wastes. Not every Norscan tribe is a fanatical devotee of Chaos — some, in peacetime, even trade furs with the Empire. But the moment a Chaos storm gathers and the Everchosen blows his war-horn, the whole of Norsca transforms overnight from "neighbour of ambiguous allegiance" into "the vanguard of annihilation." A great many of the elite troops in a Chaos Warrior host trace their origins straight to Norsca.

The Kurgan dwell on the great steppes east of Norsca, riding horses and herding, their tribal organisation exceedingly loose. They are more deeply corrupted by Chaos than the Norscans; many Kurgan tribes are already, to all intents and purposes, Chaos warbands. Multiple Everchosen in history have been born of the Kurgan steppe.

The predicament of Norsca and the Kurgan is less a "choice for Chaos" than a geographical fate. They are squeezed between the Chaos Wastes and the civilised world. Retreat means the Wastes; advance means the Empire and Kislev. The whisper of the Dark Gods is part of the north wind, poured into their ears from birth. They have certainly produced heroes — warriors who rejected corruption — but those men's grave-markers lie beneath the frozen northern soil, their names unremembered.

### Daemons of Chaos

If the Warriors of Chaos are the product of mortals surrendering to Chaos, the Daemons of Chaos are the projection of Chaos itself into the material world.

Each of the Dark Gods commands a full legion of Daemons. Khorne's Bloodthirsters stand three storeys tall, sheathed in brass armour, the earth trembling beneath every step. Tzeentch's Lords of Change are vast avian beings surrounded by pulsing sigils of sorcery; the timeline of any battle they fight may run in reverse — you see one killed before you see it arrive on the field. Nurgle's Great Unclean Ones are hunched, bloated, rotting hulks that leave a "glorious" trail of plague-flowers and maggots in their wake. Slaanesh's Keepers of Secrets are creatures of ambiguous gender whose beauty reaches a pitch that makes the stomach convulse — one extra glance and mortal minds snap.

The true terror of Daemons is this: they cannot die. A Daemon slain in the material world is merely banished back to the Realm of Chaos; its soul coalesces and returns. Killing a Daemon is sending it home — and its home is right next door to this world, ready to open the door again at any moment. In every great battle of the Old World, Daemon legions are summoned through Chaos rifts at the most critical juncture by their masters' Greater Daemons to turn the tide.

## Fate & Present Status

In 2522 IC, the Everchosen Archaon launched the Storm of Chaos, advancing south in three great prongs before being repulsed beneath the walls of Middenheim. But that invasion was only a dress rehearsal for the End Times.

A few years later, the true End Times arrived. The boundaries between the Winds dissolved. Magical energies raged out of control. The dead clawed up from their graves. The Skaven swarmed forth globally from below. Archaon mustered a Chaos host of unprecedented scale — Warriors of Chaos, Daemons of Chaos, Beastmen, the daemon-engines of the Chaos Dwarfs — spreading south like a black carpet over every city.

The Empire fell. Bretonnia fell. Dwarf strongholds were overrun one by one. The three Elven kindreds combined their forces in desperation, but it was too late. Every pocket of resistance was consumed. Archaon descended into the ancient tunnels beneath Middenheim and with his own hand severed the magical node that held the world's structure together. The Old World shattered in his grip.

But what he destroyed was not Chaos's enemy. Strictly speaking, Chaos seeks to annihilate "Order itself." The Old World was merely the most concentrated material form of Order. When the world broke, eight Mortal Realms precipitated from the wreckage. Sigmar, Nagash, and the other gods of the old world rose anew across the Realms. On the new battlefields, Archaon still commands the Chaos hosts; the new enemy is still Sigmar's Stormcast Eternals. The war of Chaos has no end.

## Verdict

Among the fourteen great houses of the Old World, Chaos is the most unique. The other thirteen are races, kingdoms, civilisations — they have borders, origins, rises and falls. Chaos has no border, no origin to trace, and can suffer no true defeat.

The most unsettling thing about Chaos is this: it does not come entirely from "out there." Inside every mortal heart lives Khorne's rage, Tzeentch's calculation, Nurgle's numbness, Slaanesh's hunger. The Dark Gods do not need to conquer everyone — they only need, at the right moment, to whisper a single sentence to a single person, and then watch his fall spread like a plague to everyone around him. Archaon was not born a world-ending monster. He was once the most devout Knight of the Empire, one revelation of his true origins away from crossing from light's end to darkness's.

The Old World died at Chaos's hands in the end. But Chaos did not win either. In the Mortal Realms, Sigmar has rallied, the war has changed skins and reopened, and the purpose remains unchanged: drag everything back into the Aethyr, reduce it to primordial Chaos without shape and without name. No one can win. No one can lose. Chaos is the only truly eternal fixture in the Warhammer universe.
