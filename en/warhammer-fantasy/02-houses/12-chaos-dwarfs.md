# 12 · Chaos Dwarfs

## Overview

The Chaos Dwarfs are the darkest branch of the Dwarf race. When the Dwarf ancestor god Grungni forged the first Dwarfs in the mountains, what came off the anvil was Order. But the origin story of the Chaos Dwarfs tells how a band of Dwarfs, in despair, welded themselves into Chaos with their own hands. They are the masters of an industrial hell, the forgers of daemon-engines, the creators of the Black Orcs. The black smoke belching from the furnace-city of Zharr-Naggrund stains the sky of the Dark Lands day and night without cease. The orthodox Dwarfs never speak of them — the pages in the Book of Grudges concerning the traitors were not forgotten. They were torn out by the High King's own hand. Not out of mercy. Out of weight.

## Origin

Everything begins with the first Chaos incursion.

Around 5500 years before the Imperial Calendar, the polar gates collapsed, and Chaos flooded across the world like a burst dam. The Dwarf strongholds in the north took the first blow. An expedition of Dwarfs — some say they were a prospecting party sent east, others that they were survivors of the collapsed northern defence line — found themselves cut off in the Dark Lands east of the Worlds Edge Mountains. They were surrounded by wasteland, active volcanoes, and warpstone-dust storms. The way home was blocked. Their shouts met no answer.

And so they made the most irreversible choice in Dwarf history: they bowed to Hashut.

Hashut is a dark being imprisoned deep in the Realm of Chaos. His form is a blazing bull wreathed in flame. He has never been counted among the Dwarf ancestor gods. Grungni does not acknowledge him. Grimnir does not acknowledge him. Valaya does not acknowledge him. But in the desperation of the Dark Lands, the Chaos Dwarfs did not need orthodoxy — they needed to survive. Hashut gave them a way: smelt black iron in Chaos-fire, drive the forges with hell-force, purchase dark magic with living sacrifice. The price was the permanent corruption of a race's soul. Every Chaos Dwarf is sold to Hashut from the moment of birth. They have no opportunity to repent, because the only path of repentance is Hashut's furnace.

They built Zharr-Naggrund — the City of Furnaces — at the heart of the Dark Lands on the Plain of Zharr. This is no traditional Dwarf stronghold. It is not a place of halls, breweries, and council chambers. It is a living black-iron factory: great towers pierce the smoke-clouds, a lava-moat boils day and night, the anvil's ring never stops for a second. A newborn who survives its first breath in this city has made the cut. Those who do not go straight into the furnace to be smelted for iron. The Chaos Dwarf empire began from this city.

## Deeds

### Society

Chaos Dwarf society is a human-flesh machine driven by terror and fire. Its hierarchy is as rigid as that of the orthodox Dwarfs, but its logic is entirely different.

At the apex stand the Sorcerer-Prophets — the high priests of Hashut and the supreme rulers. They are clergy, wizards, and arms dealers all in one. An orthodox Dwarf is absolutely incapable of wielding magic — Dwarfs are innately immune to the Winds of Magic — but Hashut's "gift" circumvents that firewall. Through living sacrifice and warpstone, the Sorcerer-Prophets can command the fires of Chaos and devastatingly destructive dark magic. The cost is the slow petrification of the body: it starts at the fingertips, crawls across the limbs, and finally the whole body turns into an immobile stone statue. Petrified Sorcerer-Prophets are carried into the deepest chambers of the Temple of Zharr to continue "living" as objects of veneration — in other words, dead and still not allowed to clock out.

Beneath the Sorcerer-Prophets are the Daemonsmiths. They are the heretics of engineering: binding Daemon souls inside iron shells to create walking furnaces and screaming artillery. Unlike the Runesmiths of the orthodox Dwarfs, they are not "inscribing" magic — they are "imprisoning" Daemons. Every Daemonsmith's career is a race against petrification. The faster ones produce more; the slower ones become another statue in the temple. Below them are the ordinary Chaos Dwarfs — warriors, overseers, craftsmen. They are crueller, more silent, and more anxious than their mountain kin. To laugh in Zharr-Naggrund is a terminal condition: you laugh, meaning you are not afraid; you are not afraid, meaning you may be the next sacrifice.

Orthodox Dwarf society is based on family, clan, and the Book of Grudges. Chaos Dwarf society is based on slaves. Tens of thousands of Orcs, Goblins, Ogres, and human captives are chained in the mines, the forges, and the construction sites, worked to death — then their skeletons are thrown into the bone-mills to be ground into powder, and the bone-powder mixed into the molten iron to "increase hardness." This is the production logic of the Chaos Dwarfs: there is no waste, only raw materials that have not yet been fully used.

Hobgoblins are the most unique tier in this slave empire. They sell themselves to the Chaos Dwarfs as overseers, buying immunity from the lash by engaging in even more enthusiastic slaughter of their fellow slaves. The Chaos Dwarfs know they are unreliable, but they are not in short supply — Hobgoblins breed faster than cockroaches.

### Military

The armies of the Chaos Dwarfs are the product catalogue of a hell-factory, every page inscribed with the same line: you can't afford it.

The core infantry is the Infernal Guard — Dwarf warriors sheathed in black heavy armour, bearing tower shields, swinging axes, firing blunderbusses. Their formation discipline concedes nothing to their mountain cousins, but they carry an extra layer of doomed intensity. Soldiers assigned to "Death Companies" may have committed a grave offence, or owe a debt too vast, or simply caught their superior's dislike. Their unifying feature: the only way to settle the account is to kill enough before dying, or to die. When they charge, these soldiers do not shout slogans — they only clench their jaws.

But the most fearsome thing about the Chaos Dwarfs is not the infantry — it is the guns.

The Hellcannon: using trapped Daemons as the raw material of its shells, every fireball that lands in the enemy ranks screams. After impact, it gets back up and keeps killing. The difference between a Hellcannon and an ordinary artillery piece is not in its yield — it is in its malice. The Chaos host made heavy use of Hellcannons during the Storm of Chaos; Archaon himself rated the merchandise highly.

Then there is the Iron Daemon — a steam-driven, tracked locomotive, bristling with multi-barrelled guns, capable of grinding through an infantry square head-on while vomiting a hail of lead. The Dreadquake Mortar — a single impact's seismic shock sufficient to crack a fortress from its foundations. The Magma Cannon, the Deathshrieker Rocket — every name was coined on the battlefield, and every one lives up to it.

The most grotesque creations are the K'daai — fire-Daemons bound directly into metal shells as their own power cores. They can move, they can burn, they can walk through an enemy formation while melting their own plating. They have no consciousness, only rage and heat. The Chaos Dwarfs themselves dare not let them linger inside the city long; they are unleashed in battle and immediately locked into cooling pools when it is over.

And then there is the Chaos Dwarfs' greatest "success" and greatest failure — the Black Orcs.

The Chaos Dwarfs attempted to breed a stronger, smarter, more obedient Orc variant to serve as a permanent slave legion. Using warpstone, dark magic, and selective breeding, they produced the Black Orcs: a head taller than ordinary Orcs, far more intelligent, combat effectiveness doubled. Then the Black Orcs rebelled. They snapped their chains, slaughtered their overseers, and burst through the gates of the City of Furnaces into the wilds. A portion of them became the most fearsome strain in the Greenskin race — Grimgor Ironhide was a Black Orc. This is the greatest achievement of Chaos Dwarf engineering and the biggest faceplant in their military history. They have never publicly mentioned the project since.

Bull Centaurs are the direct handiwork of Hashut — Chaos Dwarf infants corrupted by the bull-god in their cradles, who at adolescence undergo a mutation: the upper body remains Dwarf; the lower body transforms into a blazing iron-hooved bull. They are the most elite shock troops of Zharr-Naggrund and the personal guard of Hashut. No one knows whether they mutated willingly or were volunteered, and no one discusses it.

## Fate & Present Status

In the End Times, the Chaos Dwarfs stood, without hesitation, on Archaon's side. Their daemon-engines formed the firepower skeleton of the Chaos host. When the Old World collapsed, Zharr-Naggrund sank into the Realm of Chaos, accompanied by its never-dissipating black smoke. In the Age of Sigmar, they are reborn in the Mortal Realms as the Legion of Azgorh — still forging, still sacrificing, still belching smoke that will not clear. Hashut's business did not close. It merely changed addresses.

## Verdict

The Chaos Dwarfs are the least "tragic" of the fallen among the races of Warhammer Fantasy.

Unlike the vampires — who tasted blood they should not have tasted. Unlike the Beastmen — victims born twisted by Chaos. The Chaos Dwarfs, caught between the two roads of dying in battle and fleeing, actively chose a third: kneel. Sign a contract they knew full well was a soul-indenture, then stand up and declare victory. They survived the Dark Lands — they genuinely did survive. But what survived is no longer Dwarfs. It is a different creature wearing Dwarf memories, a Dwarf accent, Dwarf craftsmanship. It wields a hammer, but the hammer comes down on a slave's spine. It carves runes, but the runes are prison-bindings for Daemons. It can sing the ancestral drinking-songs, but the drink is human blood.

The Dwarfs of the Karaz Ankor never speak of the Chaos Dwarfs. It is not that they have forgotten. It is that any orthodox Dwarf, left alone too long in the deep mountains, might begin to hear, faintly, Hashut's whisper: "You have been abandoned. Kneel." They choose not to hear. The Chaos Dwarfs chose to hear, and knelt. The orthodox Dwarfs' hatred of the Chaos Dwarfs is so profound not only because of the betrayal, but because they see, in them, another possible version of themselves.

In the End Times, the Chaos Dwarfs stood firmly on Archaon's side. Their daemon-engines formed the firepower skeleton of the Chaos host. When the Old World collapsed, Zharr-Naggrund sank into the Realm of Chaos, accompanied by its never-dissipating black smoke. In the Age of Sigmar, they are reborn in the Mortal Realms as the Legion of Azgorh — still forging, still sacrificing, still belching smoke that will not clear. Hashut's business did not close; it merely changed addresses.
