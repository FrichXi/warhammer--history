# 06 · Greenskins

## Overview

The Greenskins are not a race — they are a natural disaster. Orcs, Goblins, Snotlings, Squigs — behind these everyday names lies a single biological fact: they are semi-fungal organisms, reproducing by spores. Kill one, the ground fills with spores; burn out a nest, and next year the same thing grows back. No one in the entire Old World has ever found a way to eradicate Greenskins at the root. In a sense, the Greenskins are the most successful life form on the planet.

## Origin: Weapons of the Old Ones

The Greenskins' origin has no definitive record, but the prevailing theory points to the Old Ones. That ancient race — which departed before Chaos first arrived — left more than one legacy on this world. The Slann of the Lizardmen were the architects and executors the Old Ones left behind; the Greenskins, in all likelihood, were the biological weapon they left.

The evidence lies in the Greenskins' brutally "user-friendly" design logic. Spore reproduction means they need no logistical tail, no nurseries, no male-female pairings. Their strength-based hierarchy means they need no political system and no ideology — one fight and the leader is instantly generated. Every Greenskin carries its entire species' genetic blueprint inside its body. Drop a spore and it grows a complete food-chain ecosystem: first Snotlings and Squigs sprout like mushrooms, then Goblins, and finally Orcs. The sequence is as tightly ordered as an automatic munitions factory.

The Chaos Dwarfs later produced the Black Orcs — a bigger, smarter, reinforced variant, originally intended as slave-soldiers. The Black Orcs revolted, battered the Chaos Dwarfs, and escaped, becoming the most terrifying sub-strain among the Greenskins.

## Origin

### Orcs and Goblins

The internal differentiation of the Greenskins is not, strictly speaking, a species division. It is more like two expressions of the same genetic blueprint.

**Orcs** are the main event — big, strong, and volatile, growing well over two metres tall, their muscle mass layered like natural plate armour, their crooked tusks capable of biting dents into iron plate. Orc thought is simple to the point of philosophy: there are only two things in the world — fighting, and getting ready to fight. Whoever's fist is biggest is boss. Whoever disagrees gets beaten until they agree. The internal politics of an Orc warband are ten thousand times simpler than the Empire: win, you're boss; lose, the boss changes. That's it.

Orc intelligence is a puzzle. They cannot manufacture precision timepieces, write poetry, or keep accounts. But they possess a beast's instinct for war — when to charge, when to flank, how to turn a pile of scrap into a siege engine — all by raw talent. An Orc smith can hammer scrap iron into a choppa; ask him what principle is at work and he'll answer, "'Cos I'm da best." And he's right — under the influence of the Greenskins' collective psychic field, that blade really is harder than ordinary iron.

**Goblins** are the skinny ones. Smaller, weaker, but with a bit more brain — and that extra bit goes almost entirely into cunning. Goblins can't beat an Orc in a fight, but they can make Orcs die for them. They excel at traps, riding Squigs as mounts, and smearing poison mushrooms on their arrows. The most famous Goblins are the Night Goblins, who dwell in caves, worship a malevolent fungus, and, when the frenzy hits, can drive an entire army into the lightless tunnels to be fed to giant spiders.

Goblin tribes almost always include a Shaman. Greenskin Shamans do not work magic through the Eight Winds — instead they draw power directly from a source we will get to shortly: the WAAAGH!. Greenskin magic is crude, unstable, and frequently detonates the caster, but before detonating it can do things that are deeply alarming.

### Gork and Mork

The Greenskins have their own gods: Gork and Mork. Two of them. No one can clearly say which is which. A Greenskin's own explanation of the two gods generally runs like this: "Gork is brutal but kunnin' — hits ya over the back of the head wiv an axe. Mork is kunnin' but brutal — stomps on yer face and asks if it hurts." Ask a different Orc and the answer might be exactly reversed.

Learned scholars have attempted the distinction: Gork is cunningly brutal (ambush you, then pulverise you); Mork is brutally cunning (pulverise you, then think of ways to pulverise you worse). But the Greenskins do not care. When they fight they shout "WAAAGH!", not the name of any god. To the Greenskins, Gork and Mork are more atmosphere than objects of worship. They exist inside every brawl, in the wildest moments of every WAAAGH! — you don't need to summon the gods, because you have already become part of them.

### The WAAAGH!

The WAAAGH! is the key to understanding the Greenskins. It is not simply a war cry — it is a collective psychic energy field. When enough Greenskins gather in one place, start fighting, and start shouting, a force condenses in the air, invisible to the eye but palpable to the Shaman. That force is the WAAAGH!.

The WAAAGH! is more perverse than any magic:
- Orcs believe red vehicles go faster — if enough Orcs believe it together, that red vehicle genuinely becomes faster.
- Orcs believe a certain boss is invincible — his skin genuinely hardens until steel blades cannot cut it.
- An Orc Shaman rams his staff into the ground and yells "BANG!" — no spell formula needed; the WAAAGH! twists the Winds of Magic into explosives for him.

The WAAAGH! is the Greenskin version of civilisation. No factories, no administration, no paperwork — all it needs is one strong enough boss to yell, and everyone surges in the direction his finger points. The bigger it gets, the stronger the WAAAGH!; the stronger the WAAAGH!, the wilder the Orcs become; the wilder the Orcs, the bigger it gets. It is a positive feedback loop. The only brake is — the fight ends, or the boss gets killed. Then the whole WAAAGH! collapses, the Greenskins start fighting each other on the spot, and wait for the next strong enough boss to appear.

### Famous Warbosses

**Gorbad Ironclaw** led the most devastating Greenskin invasion in Imperial history. In 1707 IC, his WAAAGH! swept across the Empire's south. Solland was erased from the map entirely; its Elector Count was killed in battle; its provincial banner became an Orc's foot-wrap. That war taught the Empire the bitter lesson that Greenskins are not just a mob of savages.

**Grom the Paunch** was one of the very few geniuses — if the word applies to a Goblin — to lead a WAAAGH! from a Goblin's body. His greatest achievement was sailing a fleet straight onto Ulthuan itself. It took everything the High Elves' Dragon Princes and mages had to push him back into the sea. Reading the Elven histories' account of this episode, you can feel the sheer disbelief between the lines.

**Azhag the Slaughterer** found the Iron Crown of Nagash in the northern wastes, and from that day forward a voice in his skull taught him tactics. An Orc warboss armed with Nagash's strategic mind — his WAAAGH! nearly reached the walls of Altdorf.

**Skarsnik** is the King of the Night Goblins, ruling the caverns beneath Karak Eight Peaks with a host of giant spiders and poison-dart archers. The Dwarfs have fought him for centuries and still haven't cleared him out.

But the greatest, without question, is **Grimgor Ironhide**. Grimgor was a Black Orc. Standing still, he looked like a tower of muscle that had learned to walk. His WAAAGH! was among the three largest in Orc history. During the Storm of Chaos — when the entire world was cowering in Archaon's shadow — Grimgor did something no other race dared to do: he led his WAAAGH! straight into the Chaos Wastes and fought the Chaos host in their own backyard. It is recorded that he faced the Everchosen Archaon himself in single combat and headbutted Archaon to the ground. That blow would have killed a human general twenty times over. But Archaon was, after all, the champion of all four Chaos Gods, and Grimgor ultimately fell. The histories record his final words holding no trace of regret, roughly translatable as: "Grimgor iz da best. Today... almost won."

## Fate & Present Status

The End Times, to the Greenskins, was not an apocalypse — it was just a really, really big fight. They had no stake in the fate of the world, no interest in whether Chaos would consume everything — only in whether there was something in front of them to hit. They fought everywhere, fought everyone, scattered when it was done, gathered again when opportunity called. Grimgor briefly became the living incarnation of the Wind of Beasts — Ghur — channelling the entire world's primal feral energy — but he fell in the final battles. No tragedy, no epic weight — just fight after fight and death after death.

After the Old World's destruction, the Greenskins followed into the newborn Mortal Realms. The Orcs became the Orruk Warclans — bigger, more ferocious, more organised. Goblins upgraded to Moonclan Grots, no longer mere hangers-on behind the Orcs but a force in their own right, worshipping the Bad Moon, launching mad charges on the full-moon nights. The Greenskins are doing well in the new world.

## Verdict

The Greenskins are the most pure existences in all of Warhammer Fantasy. Every other race — Men, Elves, Dwarfs — has something it wants to protect, no matter what it says. The Empire protects the Empire. The Dwarfs protect honour. The High Elves protect order. The Greenskins protect nothing, defend nothing, build nothing. They only fight.

And precisely for that reason, the Greenskins can never be truly defeated. To defeat Men you must defeat the Empire's will. To defeat Dwarfs you must erase the Book of Grudges. To defeat Elves you must destroy Ulthuan. To defeat Greenskins? You kill one warboss, the spores are in the dirt, and a little while later ten more have sprouted. The Greenskins need no will, no history, no nation. They only need to live — and for them, living is fighting.

The most heartbreaking fact is this: the Greenskins may be the happiest race on this planet. They feel no anxiety, no inner conflict, no doubt about the meaning of their existence. Fighting. That's it. Win, celebrate; lose — well, you're dead anyway, so it doesn't matter. The Chaos Gods want to buy them? Cannot buy them. Fear? Not in the genes. Despair? Nonexistent. Every second a Greenskin is alive, it is consumed in WAAAGH!.

Gork and Mork never manifest, never issue commandments, never demand temples from their faithful. But the Greenskins do not need gods to tell them what to do. They are already doing it. Every punch is a prayer. Every WAAAGH! is a mass.
