# 04 · Grimgor Ironhide

## Overview

Grimgor Ironhide, Black Orc, the greatest warlord in Greenskin history. Full stop.

"Greatest" is not about morality or achievement — Greenskins don't deal in those. He was the greatest because he took the act of "fighting" to heights no one had reached before. His WAAAGH! ranks among the three largest in Orc history. Pick any single thing he did and it would be enough for another Orc to boast about for life: he carved through the Skaven Hell Pit single-handed; he led his WAAAGH! to trample the Chaos Dwarf fortresses of the Dark Lands; and when the whole Old World cowered under Archaon's shadow, he alone took his WAAAGH! straight into the Chaos Wastes. He headbutted the Everchosen to the ground and came within a breath of winning.

In the End Times, Grimgor became the Incarnate of Ghur — the Wind of Beasts — channelling the entire world's primordial feral energy. He fell in the end, but there was nothing tragic about it. Greenskin stories have never had a word for tragedy. He just fought to the last moment and said: "Grimgor iz da best. Woz close today."

## Origin

Grimgor's origin traces back to the Chaos Dwarfs.

The Chaos Dwarfs are the darkest branch of the Dwarf race. Stranded deep in the Dark Lands, cut off in prolonged isolation, they compromised with Chaos and turned to worship Hashut, the Father of Darkness — a fallen Dwarf god. The Chaos Dwarfs live by slave labour. Their forge-cities, weapon factories, and daemon-engines all run on slaves taken from conquered peoples — above all, Orcs and Goblins.

Slaves were never enough. The Chaos Dwarfs decided to breed better ones. Using dark sorcery and warpstone, they modified and selectively bred common Orcs, creating an entirely new variant: the Black Orcs.

Black Orcs are a full size larger than common Orcs. Denser muscle, thicker bone, and sharper in the head — not the kind of sharp that does sums, but an instinctive grasp of encirclement, flanking, and ambush on the battlefield. The Chaos Dwarfs were pleased. They believed they had made the perfect slave-soldier breed.

Then the Black Orcs rebelled.

No precise record of the Black Orc uprising survives — Greenskins do not write histories. But the logic is not hard to infer: you take a species, enhance its physique, strength, and intelligence across the board, then expect it to stay a docile slave. In biological terms, that is suicide. The Black Orcs rose. Chaos Dwarf strongholds in the Dark Lands burned. The Black Orcs carved through several forge-cities, shattered the slave pens, and poured into the wilds of the Old World.

Grimgor Ironhide was a descendant of those Black Orcs. From the day he crawled out of his spore, he was bigger than his peers — bigger than his already oversized kin. Other Orcs seized territory by fighting. Grimgor seized territory by walking over — he stood there, and the other side yielded.

## Deeds

### The Unification

In under ten years, Grimgor beat every wild Greenskin tribe in the eastern Old World into submission.

His method had no finesse: walk into a tribe, rip the boss's head off, and ask the rest who objected. Usually no one raised a hand. Occasionally one did — and he ripped that one off too. Repeat until every Greenskin for hundreds of miles around the Dark Lands answered to the name Ironhide. Orcs respect strength. Grimgor's strength required no explanation. When his WAAAGH! gathered, the dust blotted the sun, the footfalls shook the earth, and you could smell rust and blood on the wind from a mountain away.

Under his banner, every kind of Greenskin — Orcs, Goblins, Black Orcs — gathered for the first time not to scatter after a fight, but as a sustained, rolling engine of destruction. Grimgor was unlike any previous Orc warlord. He was not satisfied with flattening a few villages and calling it a day. He wanted a fight big enough to be worth every spore he had.

### Sweeping the Eastern Old World

Grimgor's first real target was the makers of his own kind: the Chaos Dwarfs.

He drove his WAAAGH! north along the western fringe of the Dark Lands, flattening several Chaos Dwarf outposts. Their daemon-engines — metal constructs with bound daemon souls — were horrifying crushing machines against common Orcs. Against Grimgor, they were tin cans. The Black Orcs had been engineered by the Chaos Dwarfs to be perfect warriors; now the creations had turned and were beating their creators.

His WAAAGH! then wheeled east and crashed into Ogre territory in the Mountains of Mourn. Ogres are two to three times the size of Orcs, famed for greed and violence — the kind nobody in the mountains ever messed with. Grimgor tore an Ogre Tyrant apart with his bare hands. The remaining Ogre tribes either fled or joined his WAAAGH! — Ogres respect appetite, and Grimgor's WAAAGH! meant all-you-can-eat meat.

Civilisation in the eastern Old World was practically levelled along Grimgor's path: Chaos Dwarf citadels, Ogre strongholds, Skaven lairs — Grimgor stomped them one after another. For those years, any species living east of the World's Edge Mountains, upon hearing the distant sound of disciplined Orc war cries, knew Grimgor was coming.

### The Battle of Hell Pit

Grimgor's most legendary fight was Hell Pit.

Hell Pit is one of the Skaven's largest surface strongholds, hidden beneath the snow and ice of the northern World's Edge Mountains, packed with warpstone weapons, plague laboratories, mutated rat-ogres, and Skaven armies. The Skaven had built it up over millennia. Its defences were labyrinthine. Not even the forces of Chaos had ever cracked it.

Grimgor cracked it.

The details of the battle are fragmentary in Skaven records — understandable, since nearly every Skaven present died. The fragments that can be pieced together: Grimgor led his forces through Hell Pit's main gate and fought downward, level by level, from the uppermost sentry posts to the deepest plague breeding pools. The Skaven unleashed their most terrible creation — a Hell Pit Abomination, a colossal monstrosity stitched together from warpstone and rat carcasses. Grimgor cut the thing in half.

Skaven tactics were useless against him. The Skaven relied on numbers, ambush, warpstone gas — effective against ordinary men and Dwarfs, meaningless against Grimgor. Grimgor did not fear poison gas; Orc constitutions can withstand warpstone radiation, let alone a Black Orc's. Ambush? Grimgor's reaction speed was faster than a Skaven assassin's blade. And numbers — well, standing behind him was an entire WAAAGH!.

It is said that in the deepest reaches of Hell Pit, Grimgor came face to face with a Grey Seer. The Seer attempted to obliterate him with magic — the Skaven's vaunted Ruin magic. Grimgor tanked the first volley, walked through the warpstone fire step by step until he reached the Grey Seer, then cleaved the top of the Seer's skull off with his legendary axe.

Hell Pit was not wholly destroyed — there are too many Skaven to kill them all — but after this battle, Hell Pit was crippled and dared not launch any major surface operations for decades. Grimgor walked out covered in rat blood and was known thereafter in Greenskin legend as "da boss wot walked into hell and walked back out."

### The Everchosen and the Headbutt

During the Storm of Chaos — 2522 IC, Archaon marching his Chaos hosts south, Praag under siege, Middenheim in peril, the whole Old World choking under Chaos's iron heel — Grimgor did something no one else in the entire world dared.

He did not hold his ground waiting for Chaos to come to him. He did not ally with The Empire or the Dwarfs. He took his WAAAGH! and went north. Straight through the frozen wastes of Kislev, over the edge of the Chaos Wastes, into the home country of Chaos itself.

What are the Chaos Wastes? A place where reality and the Realm of Chaos overlap, where the very air corrodes. A human cannot last a day there without going mad. A Dwarf cannot last a month without being corrupted. Grimgor and his Greenskin host fought there for an entire season. Greenskins cannot be corrupted by Chaos — not because of any special resistance, but because Chaos needs a mind to work on. The Orc mind is too simple. The whispers of Chaos are just wind to them. Khorne trying to recruit Grimgor? Grimgor's response was to kill one of Khorne's Daemon Princes and take its horns as a trophy.

The iconic moment came outside Archaon's main encampment. Grimgor's WAAAGH! had breached the outer Chaos battle lines, and Grimgor himself went straight for the centre. Archaon — Everchosen elected by all four Chaos Gods, wielder of the Six Treasures, the man before whom half the Old World had already knelt — was forced to come out and fight.

By the accounts of those who witnessed the melee, the duel between Grimgor and Archaon was a pure collision of violence and sorcery. Archaon's Eye of Sheerian foresaw Grimgor's every blow; the Slayer of Kings struck for killing points with every stroke. Grimgor surpassed what any Chaos champion could imagine in speed and raw power. A single Black Orc, an axe the width of a door, no magic, no artefacts — nothing but strength.

The critical moment: Grimgor dipped his head, dropped his shoulder, and drove his skull into Archaon.

An Orc headbutt is a natural weapon. Grimgor's headbutt was more. The impact hit Archaon's chest and cracked several plates of his Chaos armour. Archaon — the Crown of Domination burning with the mark of Chaos, the Eye of Sheerian shrieking — was knocked to the ground by an Orc.

Old World history wobbled here, at its most delicate point. If Grimgor's blow had been a fraction heavier. If he had been holding Ghal Maraz instead of his Orc axe. If the Chaos Gods had withdrawn their favour from Archaon in that instant — then the history of the Everchosen would have ended right there. Ended by a Black Orc.

But Archaon was still the chosen champion of the four Ruinous Powers. He rose from the ground. The Slayer of Kings blazed with Chaos fire. Grimgor was defeated in the end.

Grimgor's dying response — if he died in that moment — carried no fear, no regret. According to the Orcs around him, who later roared the version out into legend, his last words were: "Grimgor iz da best! Woz real close today!"

Crudely translated into any human tongue, it sounds inadequate. In Orcish, it is the highest compliment you can pay a proper fight.

### The End Times

Grimgor did not die in the Storm of Chaos. He survived that defeat, his WAAAGH! reassembled, and he continued to roam the northern Old World.

When the End Times truly descended — the Winds of Magic collapsing, Chaos flooding in fully, every inch of the Old World in flames — something happened to Grimgor that had never happened to any Greenskin in history: he was chosen by Ghur, the Wind of Beasts, and became its Incarnate.

Ghur is the one Wind closest to the Orc essence. It is not logical, not civilised, not even magical — it is pure savagery, predator instinct, the primal impulse toward strength and survival that stirs in the unconscious depths of all living things. When the Old World was being torn apart, this force needed a vessel. Grimgor was the vessel.

Incarnate of Ghur, Grimgor was no longer a mere Black Orc warlord. He channelled the collective will of every beast, every predator, every creature on the planet that lived by violence. The Old World's primordial feral energy poured into his frame. His bulk swelled to a scale that made dragons look small. In this form, Grimgor embodied the world's last savage roar before extinction.

He fought in the final battle — not for any faction, not for Order nor Chaos. He just fought wherever there was fighting, hacking at anything that moved. His time as Incarnate was brief, because individual existence at that magnitude of power is inherently temporary. Grimgor fell in the final collision between Chaos and Order. No tragedy, no epic cadence, no last words — just fighting until he could not fight any longer.

## Fate & Present Status

Grimgor fell in the End Times, his body consumed as the Incarnate of Ghur in the final cataclysm. But his spores persist — in every Black Orc that crawls out of the earth, in every Greenskin that roars a challenge, Grimgor's legacy rolls on. Greenskins do not build memorials, but they do not need them. The spores are the memorial.

## Verdict

Grimgor Ironhide is the purest being in the entire Warhammer Fantasy universe.

Sigmar has his empire, Nagash his undead dominion, Archaon his Chaos mandate — Grimgor has nothing, and needs nothing. He does not defend anything, pursue any objective, or uphold any value. The entirety of his meaning is fighting. Not to win, not to rule, not to prove anything — fighting itself. Win and find the next fight. Lose and so be it.

This radical purity makes Grimgor a paradox no civilisation can comprehend: he is an actor who pursues no purpose, yet his agency eclipses nearly everyone who does pursue grand purposes. While Sigmar's faithful kneel in cathedrals, elven mages study ancient tomes in towers, and imperial Elector Counts scheme in palace chambers — Grimgor is chopping. Always chopping.

His iconic moment — headbutting Archaon to the ground — sticks in memory not because of power levels, but because it is the most complete "dimensionality drop" in Old World history. Archaon spent half his life studying Chaos theology, collecting the Six Treasures, unifying the northern tribes, and formulating his southern strategy. Grimgor did not need to study any of this. He only asked one question: "Who'z dat git wiv da stupid hat?" Then he charged.

The Chaos Gods could not corrupt him, not because of any special ward, but because corrupting a being with a singular will is pointless. The Orc mental structure is the brute manifestation of a philosophical "monad" that human thinkers have spent millennia trying to describe: Grimgor has no gap between "want to fight" and "don't want to fight." He has only one state — fight. In this state, all temptation is superfluous. Khorne offers power? Grimgor already has power. Tzeentch offers cunning? Grimgor has no use for cunning. Slaanesh offers pleasure? Fighting is Grimgor's only pleasure. Nurgle offers immortality? Spores are immortality.

This is the essence of Grimgor Ironhide: he is an existence that needs no answers, because his world has only one question — "Who'z next?"

Greenskins will not memorialise him. No statues, no epics, no shrines. But his spores grow in every inch of the Old World's soil. So long as one more Orc crawls out of a spore, Grimgor has not wholly vanished. He lives in every war cry, every headbutt, every inexplicable impulse in a Black Orc whelp — "I'z da best."

Grimgor iz da best. Grimgor woz real close.

That's enough.
