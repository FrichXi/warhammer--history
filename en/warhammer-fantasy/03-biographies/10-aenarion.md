# 10 · Aenarion

## Overview

Aenarion, first Phoenix King of the Elves, father of the Witch King Malekith of the Dark Elves, progenitor of the royal bloodlines of Ulthuan. He is the greatest warrior in the entirety of the Warhammer world — bar none. At the apocalyptic hour of the first great Chaos incursion, when the Old World teetered on the brink of being swallowed whole, he single-handedly turned the tide and traded his own life for the life of the entire world. But his victory came at the price of the Sword of Khaine: the blade saved him, cursed him, and cursed every generation of his bloodline for the six thousand years that followed. He is the origin of all elven glory, and the root of all elven tragedy.

## Origin

Before Chaos came, Ulthuan was the age of the elven princedoms. The elves ruled dozens of kingdoms across the continent, possessed of a civilisation rival to that of the dragons themselves. Aenarion was born to the royal house of Nagarythe, a coastal kingdom on Ulthuan's western shore. The Nagarythii were renowned for their martial ferocity, cold and proud in temperament, contemptuous of the soft inland elves who did nothing but compose poetry. In times of peace, Aenarion was merely the heir to a provincial throne. Little is recorded of his early years — for the moment of his true entry onto the stage of history was precisely the moment the world began to die.

## Deeds

### Doom Descends

Around five thousand, six hundred years before the Imperial calendar, the polar gateways built by the Old Ones collapsed without warning. The energies of the Realm of Chaos poured into the world like a breached dam. Daemons in their millions streamed forth from the north; wherever they passed, the land twisted, living things mutated, and civilisation was reduced to ash. The elves were the first to face this catastrophe. The northern coastal kingdoms of Ulthuan were virtually annihilated in the first wave. Elven armies had no means of fighting creatures that could not truly die — kill a daemon, its soul returned to the Realm of Chaos to reconstitute a body, and before long it would come screaming back. This was a war the elves were fated to lose.

Chaos swept across the whole of Ulthuan. The elven princedoms fought alone and fell one by one. Elven civilisation neared extinction. In Nagarythe, Aenarion watched with his own eyes as his family and his people were slaughtered by daemons. He was not a prince raised in peace; he was a king without a kingdom who had survived a mountain of corpses and a sea of blood.

### Into the Sacred Fire

In desperation, Aenarion came to the Shrine of Asuryan in Ulthuan's interior. The sacred flame of Asuryan, king of the elven gods, burns there eternally. Legend held that only one chosen by the god could walk into the fire and emerge alive. No elf who had tried had ever come out. Aenarion did not hesitate. He bared his chest and walked into the flames.

The fire did not consume him. Asuryan had chosen him.

Out of the sacred flame stepped Aenarion, the first Phoenix King, his body wreathed in undying divine fire. The remnant elven forces and surviving princes rallied to his banner. The Phoenix King's appearance gave the elves a unified commander for the first time — but the fire of Asuryan alone was far from enough to fight Chaos. He needed a deadlier weapon.

### Drawing the Sword of Khaine

Aenarion flew to the Blighted Isle and found the altar of Khaela Mensha Khaine, the elven god of war and murder. At the altar's centre was planted a colossal black blade — the Sword of Khaine, also called the Widowmaker. This weapon is the ultimate cursed blade in all elven legend, possessed of god-killing power, yet every hand that holds it is dragged toward destruction even as it grants invincible might. Khaine himself was the sword's first victim.

The last elf who had tried to draw the blade had his soul devoured on the spot.

Aenarion drew the Sword of Khaine.

From that moment, he became a killing engine that nothing could stop. He mounted Indraugnir, father of dragons, took up the Widowmaker, and hurled himself into the daemon hordes. A war the elves had been doomed to lose was, by one man alone, wrenched into a reversal.

### The Skies Drenched in Blood

Of Aenarion's deeds in the years that followed, only one summary suffices: he killed so many that the Chaos Gods, for the first time in their existence, tasted fear in this world. He was not fighting a defensive war — he was counter-attacking, alone. He slew four Greater Daemons, one dispatched by each of the four Ruinous Powers. He drove the daemon legions from the northern shores of Ulthuan back to the very edge of the polar Chaos Wastes. Hundreds upon hundreds of Daemon Princes, Chaos champions, and monstrous abominations fell beneath the Widowmaker. Even the most fanatical worshippers of Khorne could not bring themselves to face this elf wreathed in divine fire and wielding the black blade. His name became a word daemons forbade themselves to speak.

But the Sword of Khaine was consuming him at the same time. Every kill dragged his soul another inch into the abyss. The once-noble, cold prince of Nagarythe grew ever more savage and blood-maddened. After his first wife, the Everqueen Astarielle, was slain by daemons, he plunged wholly into fury and darkness. The Widowmaker had grown into his hand — he depended on the sword to live, and the sword fed on his killing.

It was in the intervals of battle that he rescued a beautiful and mysterious elven woman from the clutches of a daemon of Slaanesh — Morathi. She became his second wife. Subsequent history would prove that, for the destiny of the elven race, this marriage was worse than a hundred Chaos incursions.

### The Great Vortex and the Final Battle

Even as Aenarion killed until the sky turned dark, daemons could not truly die — the more he slew, the faster they poured back out from the Realm of Chaos. Caledor Dragontamer, the greatest mage of the elves, proposed a lunatic scheme: to create a colossal magical vortex at the centre of the Inner Sea of Ulthuan, one that would siphon the excess Winds of Magic out of the world and back into the Realm of Chaos. Only thus could daemons be denied the ability to maintain physical form in the mortal world.

The plan was viable, but it needed time. And time was what Aenarion would have to buy with his life.

While Caledor mustered every elven mage to initiate the Great Vortex ritual, Aenarion stood alone on the perimeter of the Isle of the Dead, facing the final, most frenzied daemon onslaught. He fought without pause for forty days and forty nights. At the ritual's last moment, after cutting down four Greater Daemons, his strength gave out and he died. His dragon Indraugnir bore his body away from the battlefield and returned the Widowmaker to its place on the altar of Khaine.

The Great Vortex was completed. The energies of Chaos were drained. Daemons lost their foothold in the material world, and the first great Chaos incursion ended. The Old World — and every race that would ever afterwards live upon it — survived.

## Fate & Present Status

Aenarion died upon the Isle of the Dead at the climax of the first Chaos incursion. His body was carried away by Indraugnir. The Sword of Khaine was returned to the Blighted Isle, waiting in silence for the next hand desperate enough to draw it.

His bloodline lived on — and through it, so did both his glory and his curse. Every Phoenix King who followed, every prince of the line of Aenarion, every tragedy stretching from the Sundering to the End Times — all trace their roots back to that moment when the first Phoenix King wrapped his fingers around the Widowmaker's hilt.

## Verdict

Aenarion is the one great figure in elven history upon whom there is no dispute. Even "great figure" does not quite capture it — he is the elves' saviour. Without him, elven civilisation would have been extinguished outright in the first Chaos incursion. There would have been no golden age of the High Elves, no rise of the dwarfs or the Empire of Man. The entire history of the Warhammer world would never have unfolded. He bought the life of the whole world with a single life — his own.

But the other face of the saviour is the curse he personally planted in the elven bloodline.

The dark power of the Sword of Khaine seeped into his blood and passed through him to every descendant. Tyrion's inherited fury, Malekith's thousand-year hatred, Morathi's endless campaign of corruption — all of it can be traced back to the moment Aenarion drew the Widowmaker. He saved the world with the sword, and the sword poisoned the world through him.

Harsher still is that the institution of the Phoenix King which he left behind carries an original sin of its own. The Phoenix King is the supreme authority among the elves, but the office was carved out by Aenarion with flesh, blood, and the Sword of Khaine — it was not the product of any natural political evolution. Every Phoenix King after him emulated his walk into the sacred fire, yet none was ever truly worthy of the throne. Malekith believed he was the rightful heir and was rejected — which triggered the Sundering. Bel Shanaar was poisoned. Tyrion, at the last, also drew the Sword of Khaine and repeated the ancestor's doom. The Phoenix Throne is a torture device set above flames; whoever sits upon it is either burned to death or devoured by the blade. There is no third outcome.

This is Aenarion's legacy: glorious beyond the reach of his descendants, crushing beyond their ability to bear. He is the beginning of everything, and the root of everything.

He traded his own life for the life of the world. The price was — every life his descendants would ever have, handed over along with it.
