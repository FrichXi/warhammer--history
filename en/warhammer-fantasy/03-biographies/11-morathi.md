# 11 · Morathi

## Overview

Morathi, the Hag Sorceress of the Dark Elves, mother of the Witch King Malekith, founder and high priestess of the Slaaneshi cult of the Pleasure Cults — the single most dangerous woman in elven history. The chronicles of the High Elves name her "the Hag." The subjects of the Druchii call her "the Witch Queen." And in the secret gatherings watched over by Slaanesh, she bears another name: the mortal mouthpiece of the god of pleasure himself.

Judged purely by survival, perhaps three beings in the entire Warhammer cosmos can match her. From the first Chaos incursion to the destruction of the Old World, across ten thousand years of elven history — how many Phoenix Kings came and went, how many generations of heroes fell — she remained alive. Not just alive: ageless, her power unbroken, her influence never truly severed. She is a dark thread running through the veins of the elven race. Dig deep enough into any of the great catastrophes in the elven historical record, and you will find her fingerprints. The dark magic of Nagash was taught by her. Malekith's Sundering was pushed by her. The thousand-year bloody court politics of Naggaroth were woven by her hand. The most terrifying thing about this woman is not how powerful she is, but that she always stands behind the curtain, always wears a smile calibrated to the exact right degree, and always leaves you with the impression — she isn't really doing evil, she's just helping her son.

## Origin

Morathi's early years have been so thoroughly wrapped in lies by her own hand that posterity can establish only one thing with certainty: before the first Chaos incursion, she was already a prophetess and sorceress in the elven colonies, and her reputation was considerable. Some accounts say she originally served Morai-Heg, the elven goddess of fate. The truth of this claim is probably known to no one but Morathi herself.

The first Chaos incursion changed everything. Daemons of Slaanesh captured her. No one knows how long she was held, nor what she experienced within Slaanesh's domain — she herself never speaks of it. The outcome, however, is this: at the height of the Chaos invasion, Morathi was rescued by the Phoenix King Aenarion himself and soon after became his wife, queen of the elves.

This sounds like a fairy tale of the hero saving the beauty — provided you ignore one fact: by the time she was rescued, Morathi was already a worshipper of Slaanesh. She did not fall later. From her very first day in the Phoenix King's court, she was the seed Chaos had planted in elven soil. Aenarion, his eyes blinded by the fury of the Sword of Khaine, went to his death never knowing what lay sleeping in the bed beside him.

Morathi bore Aenarion a son: Malekith.

## Deeds

### The First Seed: the Pleasure Cults

The first Chaos incursion ended with Aenarion's sacrifice. Chaos receded. The elves began rebuilding their civilisation. The elven princes chose Bel Shanaar as the second Phoenix King. Morathi lost the political station of queen consort — but she never truly lost power. She simply moved her arena from the throne room to the back chamber.

She founded the Pleasure Cults. On the surface, these appeared to be small circles of elven nobles devoted to hedonism and indulgence. The aristocracy of Ulthuan sneered at them but paid them little mind — elves had always prized elegance and sensuous experience; what harm was there in a banquet cult? No one imagined that every "banquet" was in truth a rite of Slaanesh, that every poured cup of wine was laced with the corruption of Chaos, that every noble who joined was mortgaging their soul to the god of pleasure without even knowing it.

Here Morathi's artistry was displayed at its most exquisite: the Pleasure Cults bore almost no outward signs. No daemonic altars, no blood-soaked sacrifices, nothing an outsider could point at and recognise as a Chaos cult. It was elven hedonism, nothing more — Slaaneshi worship in silk gowns and crystal goblets. You let your guard down precisely because it looked exactly like what elves were supposed to be.

### The Fall of Malekith

Malekith was the work of Morathi's lifetime.

The boy was born with every prerequisite for heroism: the blood of Aenarion, innate talent for war, the diplomatic skill of a master politician. In the years when he campaigned across the world as the Phoenix King's supreme general, Malekith was the most beloved leader in elven society. Many believed he should have been the second Phoenix King.

But the Council of Princes did not choose him. Bel Shanaar was raised to the throne. Malekith endured it — at first. He turned instead to the colonisation of Naggaroth, built settlements, forged alliances with the dwarfs, and served for years as the model general.

Morathi, throughout this period, did not rush. She simply kept pouring into Malekith's ear, steadily, gently, in the manner of a mother concerned for her son: Bel Shanaar is unworthy of that throne. The elven nobles have wronged the blood of Aenarion. You have been conspired against and pushed aside. Say it once and it is a complaint. Say it a hundred times and it is a grievance. Say it ten thousand times and it becomes an entire worldview.

When the conflict between Malekith and Bel Shanaar finally erupted and Malekith killed Bel Shanaar, he did something that decided the elven destiny: he walked into the sacred fire of Asuryan, seeking to prove that he was the god's acknowledged Phoenix King. The flame judged him unworthy. He was burned beyond recognition, nearly killed.

That moment was Morathi's greatest victory. If Malekith had accepted reality after the burning, waited for healing, elven history might have been rewritten. But a man fed ten thousand times on "the throne should have been yours" does not accept defeat. Malekith rose up in the fire, used sorcery to slaughter the witnesses, and launched his coup. The Sundering began.

### The Witch Queen of Naggaroth

The Sundering ended with Malekith's defeat. A portion of Ulthuan sank beneath the sea. Malekith led his followers in flight to Naggaroth and founded the kingdom of the Dark Elves.

Morathi entered Naggaroth in the dual role of queen mother and supreme sorceress. She established her own convent of sorceresses at Ghrond in the far north and made it the formal headquarters of the Pleasure Cults. In Naggaroth, the worship of Slaanesh no longer needed to skulk in shadows as it had in Ulthuan — yet Morathi still did not make it public. Why? Because the state religion of the Dark Elves was the worship of Khaine, the bloody-handed god. Malekith needed a militaristic empire that could wage war, not a carnival of Slaaneshi ecstasy. Morathi understood her son too well to cross that line.

So she played an extraordinarily deft double game: in public, she posed as a rival to the high priestess of the Khainite cult, representing an alternative power bloc in Druchii society. In secret, the Pleasure Cults continued to operate as a hidden branch *within* the cult of Khaine — worshippers believed they were serving the Bloody-Handed God, even as every killing offered their souls to Slaanesh. She ran this charade for nearly five thousand years.

Meanwhile, her relationship with Malekith became a protracted war of attrition. The mother sought to seduce her son into the embrace of Slaanesh. The son fended her off with the vigilance of a politician. Mother and son allied — putting down rebellions, prosecuting foreign wars together — and also fought each other: Morathi more than once launched her own schemes around Malekith's back; Malekith more than once purged the agents she had planted. But neither could kill the other. Not for lack of ability. For lack of will. The entire court politics of Naggaroth was, at bottom, a five-thousand-year chess game between this mother and her son.

### The Reckoning of the End Times

When the End Times came, the Pleasure Cults' true nature could at last no longer be hidden.

In the chaos surrounding Tyrion's drawing of the Sword of Khaine and his possession by the god of murder, Morathi seized the moment to summon a daemon army of Slaanesh. With Chaos sweeping all before it and the three worlds collapsing into confusion, she meant to execute the ultimate plan she had waited ten thousand years to realise: the true advent of Slaanesh, the god's rule over everything.

This time, her luck ran dry. The ritual spiralled out of control. Slaanesh did not descend under her command — he devoured *her* instead. Ten thousand years of faithful service, repaid by her god turning on her and eating her like a morsel.

This is the deepest irony in Morathi's entire story: she spent ten thousand years manipulating others, using others, treating everyone — her husband, her son, the entire elven race — as offerings upon the altar of Slaanesh. At the end, she herself was the last offering.

### The Serpent Goddess

But Morathi is not a woman who stays dead.

After the Old World was destroyed and the Mortal Realms took shape, Slaanesh — engorged on so many elven souls — fell into a kind of digestive torpor. Seizing this opportunity — exactly how she escaped, no one can say — Morathi crawled out of Slaanesh's belly. She manifested in a new form in the Realm of Shadow, Ulgu: her upper half still the flawless elven face and seductive body, her lower half transformed into a vast serpent's tail. She was no longer mortal. She was a demigod.

What she did next was even more remarkable. She gathered the elven souls that had also escaped Slaanesh — the witch-aelves — and told them an entirely new story. In this story, Morathi was not a follower of Slaanesh but a victim of Khaine. Khaine, she told them, had been swallowed by Slaanesh; Morathi had tried to save him and was swallowed in turn, barely struggling free, her body cursed into serpentine form as a result. She told the witch-aelves: the god you serve is not Slaanesh. It is Khaine.

This lie was identical to the sorcery she had worked ten thousand years before in Ulthuan — wrapping the worship of Slaanesh in the vestments of the worship of Khaine, making believers think they were doing something righteous. Only now the scale was grander: she built an entire religion, the Daughters of Khaine, with herself as their supreme goddess. Believers offered her blood and souls, and she swallowed all of it into her serpent body, swelling her divine power day by day.

Her final apotheosis was achieved not through prayer but through violence: in an exquisitely orchestrated operation, she devoured the heart of a dead god. Which heart, and how she seized it — the accounts vary wildly. The result is undisputed: Morathi-Khaine formally ascended to true godhood in the Mortal Realms, enthroned in the Druchii capital of Hagg Nar in the Realm of Shadow, maintaining surface cooperation with the Pantheon of Order while in secret perpetually gnawing away at power for herself.

## Fate & Present Status

Morathi-Khaine rules as a living goddess in the Realm of Shadow. Her cult, the Daughters of Khaine, wages a shadow war across the Mortal Realms, believing themselves to be servants of the Bloody-Handed God. She sits in Hagg Nar, ageless and undiminished, weaving the same pattern she has woven for over ten millennia — only now as a deity in her own right.

## Verdict

If you look only at Morathi's personal curriculum vitae — Phoenix Queen, queen mother of a nation, founder of a cult, ascendant to godhood — you might think her a legend. But if you pull the lens back and examine her from the perspective of the entire elven race's history, you find a horrifying fact: trace almost any dead-end road in that history back to its fork, and you will find her standing there.

The first Chaos incursion did not annihilate elven civilisation, because Aenarion bought its survival with his life. But if Morathi had not implanted the cult of Slaanesh into the nobility after the war, the rot of elven society would never have spread like a slow cancer across thousands of years. The immediate driver who tore the elven race apart in the Sundering was Malekith — but if you subtract the words Morathi poured into his ear century after century, Malekith might have been a general who accepted his lot rather than a tyrant who rebelled. Naggaroth's slavery, the Druchii's culture of blood sacrifice, the twisted symbiosis between Pleasure Cults and the cult of Khaine — all of it was built by Morathi's hand, using her signature "double method."

She is not like Archaon, who crushes everything in a frontal assault. She is not like Nagash, who openly declares his intention to dominate the cosmos. Her modus operandi is always a whisper, a banquet, a habit that looks harmlessly pleasurable — and then, dozens of centuries later, when you look back, the entire society has been hollowed out from within.

The only paradox is that her ambition was ultimately swallowed whole by a bigger god. She gave Slaanesh ten thousand years of loyal service, and her reward was to be devoured. Then she crawled back out of that same belly and, through lies and consumption, became a god herself — an ending that could not be more purely Morathi: as long as she is not quite dead, she will invent a new identity and continue doing exactly what she has always done.
