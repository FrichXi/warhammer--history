# 06 · Valkia the Bloody

## Overview

Valkia the Bloody, immortal bride of Khorne, known across the world as the Gore-Queen. Among all the northern tribes, she is the only woman to have walked into the Realm of Chaos in mortal flesh and been remade by the Blood God's own hands as a Daemon Princess. In the Old World's two-thousand-year history of Chaos, ascension to daemonhood is common enough — but female daemon princes are vanishingly rare. And among them, only one rose from a tribal girl to the very throne of the Blood God, winning divine favour through sheer, unadulterated rage. During the End Times, she swept across Naggaroth at the head of her blood-soaked host, then followed Archaon the Everchosen to the siege of Averheim, and did not take a single step back before she died.

## Origin

Valkia was born to the Schwarzvolf tribe of the north. Her father Merroc was chieftain; her mother Eris was a shield-maiden — one of the warrior women who stood in the shield-wall of the northern tribes. When her parents wed, her father was sixteen, her mother fourteen. Valkia was the first child of theirs to survive. Her father called her his "little friend" — a measure of his devotion.

When she was nine, her mother fell in battle against a rival tribe. The day after the funeral rites, her father mustered the warriors for a revenge war. Valkia told him she wanted not only to stand in her mother's place in the shield-wall, but one day to reach the Halls of the Gods and fight at their side. Her father refused at first, but after consulting the circle of elders, he allowed his daughter to fill her mother's vacant place as a shield-maiden.

Ten years old, Valkia stood in the shield-wall and held against the enemy charge with a frame barely taller than her spear. After the Schwarzvolf routed the foe, she joined the pursuit of the fleeing soldiers, sweeping several to the ground with the butt of her spear. Her father held one captive for her and let her drive the spear-point into the prisoner's heart with her own hands. She did not blink.

In the years that followed, Valkia swiftly grew into a striking woman — but equally notorious for her volcanic temper and ferocious skill with weapons. She rebuffed every suitor with curses, fists, and, when necessary, sharper means. On the battlefield, she plunged into the densest fighting, treating life and death as interchangeable. When her father remarried, she maintained several years of cordial relations with her stepmother Kata and gained two half-sisters and a half-brother. Training in arms fell to Raddek, the tribe's new War-Speaker of the Blood God. Valkia's aptitude with the spear was unrivalled; within a few years, no one in the entire North could match her.

By eighteen, Valkia had cropped her hair short, her body lean with whipcord muscle, and she almost never returned to her father's tent. She neither married nor took lovers — a matter of unending friction with her father. Not long after, her father fell ill.

During a hunt, Valkia and her father, recovered enough to ride, came upon a band of refugees. She brought the women and children back to the tribe to serve as breeding stock, then, over her father's objections, led twenty warriors to slaughter every adult male among the refugees. Merroc watched his daughter's back and understood his time was over. Valkia understood it too. She had intended to give her father a clean death, sparing him further decline. But Raddek struck first — killed Merroc to prove his loyalty to Valkia. Valkia then killed Raddek in turn, avenging her father. She did all of this before the assembled warriors, then returned to the tribe and proclaimed herself chieftain.

## Deeds

After becoming chieftain, Valkia met the first man who stirred something in her — Deron, lord of the Bloody Hand tribe. Their first encounter was a duel, with a pact that the first to draw blood lost. Deron was beaten, but he promised Valkia he would lead her deeper into the mysteries of the Blood God — in the Schwarzvolf tongue, they called Khorne "the Axe-Father." The two tribes allied. Valkia began ringing her encampment with the skulls of rival chieftains and presumptuous suitors. The ring of skulls grew.

After she conquered a minor tribe, its smith forged her a spear graven with Khorne's runes. She named it Slaupnir in the Schwarzvolf tongue. The spear became her signature — as terror-inducing as the head of Locephax, a Daemon Prince of Slaanesh, which she later nailed to her shield.

Valkia married Deron and bore twin daughters, named Eris and Bellona. But she loathed the weakness of pregnancy. She bled torrents in childbirth and emerged incandescent with fury. Deron was equally dissatisfied — he wanted sons, not daughters. Their already strained bond worsened.

During a sacrificial rite to the Blood God, Valkia ordered the tribe's God-Speaker, Fedor, to open her daughters' minds to the Chaos Gods — to Khorne above all. As the rite reached its peak, she drove a short blade into Deron's eye socket and twisted off his head.

By thirty, Valkia had reached the zenith of a mortal's life. Under her rule, the tribe's population doubled and its martial strength swelled. Her people built her a wooden throne studded with skulls. She found it uncomfortable but let them have their way.

It was at this time that Locephax, a greater daemon of Slaanesh, came to her tribe. The daemon had enthralled every person in his path with his glamour — including Valkia's two daughters. He openly toyed with the two girls in front of Valkia, then informed her he had come to recruit a new harem for his master. Valkia refused. Locephax challenged her — single combat in the blood-pit, her soul as the stake.

She took up her spear and answered. The oral epics of two tribes say the fight raged for days. As Locephax fought her, he simultaneously exerted his influence over the warriors of the tribe, turning them against her. Valkia hacked down every one of the traitors who rushed her. Khorne's fury flooded her veins. When the daemon sneered that her daughters would also become his slaves, Slaupnir's point punched through his defences. The Khornate runes on the spear blazed. Locephax was forced into his true form and had his head struck off. She nailed the daemon's skull to her shield. From that day forward, Khorne's gaze was truly upon her.

After this, Valkia announced to her tribe: she would cross the Chaos Wastes and place the head of Locephax before the Blood God's throne with her own hands. She selected over a thousand of the strongest warriors to accompany her, and marched north.

The journey was pure hell. It was the eighteenth day before the first real fight — a pack of trolls killed several scouts. Valkia led a picked band to hunt them, but the true killer was the daemon's head nailed to her shield: it gnawed at the warriors' minds and drove them to kill each other, to leap from cliffs. Then came a Beastman warherd. After a savage battle, Valkia slew its chieftain with her own hands, drank his blood, and the enemy scattered. But her army suffered grievous losses. What the War-Speaker Hepsus did not tell her was that her half-brother Edan had taken the bulk of the warriors and slipped home after the fight, leaving her with only some hundreds of wounded survivors.

At last the survivors reached a colossal green staircase of eight steps. At its summit, a gate tore the sky open. The head of Locephax issued its final warning — his kin were coming. As Valkia approached the stairs, Hepsus held everyone back. Only Cormac, a one-armed warrior, followed his queen onward. Daemon legions surged toward them like a tide. Valkia touched Cormac's shoulder, acknowledging his loyalty, and the two were swallowed.

Cormac died fighting, his axe buried in a daemon's skull. Valkia fought on alone and fell before the great gate.

Khorne let out a roar that shook the Realm of Chaos. The daemons swarming Valkia's body were vaporised in an instant. The Bloodletters descended and commanded their kin to lift Valkia's body and bear it into the Realm of Chaos.

In the Blood God's palm, Valkia was reshaped. Khorne crushed her old body. From her head he drew forth two vast horns. He remade her legs into the reverse-jointed hooves of a Bloodletter. From the muscle and skin of her spine he pulled forth great bat-like wings. When she rose again, every daemon of the Realm of Chaos knelt before her, blood-kissing the ground.

She was a Daemon Princess. She was Khorne's bride.

Upon returning to the mortal world, the first thing Valkia did was find Cormac's body on the ground where she had fallen. To her surprise, a portion of the one-armed warrior's soul still clung to the remains. She carved runes into him, poured in molten brass and fire, and transformed him into a crimson undead champion. She gifted him a great axe forged of daemon essence and forced a Khornate Juggernaut to serve as his mount.

Next, Valkia began absorbing the Khorne-worshipping tribes along her path. One of them had already been offering worship to her personally while she was still in the Realm of Chaos. Her forces swelled rapidly — men, mutants, Beastmen, Chimerae, warriors of every species gathering beneath her banner. Then she turned south and marched back to the Schwarzvolf.

Under Eris — the daughter named for Valkia's mother — the remnants of the Schwarzvolf tried to meet her. But their hundreds against Valkia's daemon army were lambs before wolves. Eris confronted her mother on the battlefield. The outcome was never in doubt, but as Valkia looked upon that unflinching young face, a flicker of pride crossed her long-dead heart. The reward for that flicker: Valkia drove her claw cleanly through her daughter's heart. A quick death.

Her half-brother Edan did not enjoy the same treatment. Valkia seized his jaw and pulled his head from his body with exquisite slowness.

Then she watched every man, woman, and child of the Schwarzvolf be slaughtered. When it was done, she gave Cormac the order: "Burn it all. Let the name of the Schwarzvolf be nothing but a faint and terrible memory."

Only the old throne remained, densely studded with skulls — among them Eris's, occupying a place of pride.

Over the centuries that followed, Valkia swept across the North and the northern Old World as the Gore-Queen. In 1396 IC, she led her army to shatter the outer defences of the Dwarf hold Karak Ghulg, and devised a torture called the Bloodraven: ribs wrenched outward to expose heart and lungs, making the dead resemble creatures with blood-drenched wings. She carved out a domain for herself in the Realm of Chaos — the volcanic fortress of Ashen Crag, her own small kingdom.

When the End Times began, Valkia led the Gorehunt warband across the Ironfrost Glacier straight for Naggaroth, catching the Dark Elves utterly off guard. She had no interest in Archaon's grand strategy — she listened only to the voice of Khorne.

In 2528 IC, she appeared before the walls of Averheim as part of Archaon's host. By then, Averheim was the last human city-state standing in the Old World. Emperor Karl Franz led the remnant of humanity in a final charge, seeking to kill the Everchosen. Valkia plunged from the sky — the Emperor was Archaon's, but she chose the Emperor's standard-bearer, Ludwig Schwarzhelm. She wanted his battle standard and his skull.

She knocked the Sword of Vengeance from Schwarzhelm's grip, but in the instant she dove to drive Slaupnir home, he swung the banner-pole across her spine. Both collapsed dead in the same moment and were immediately trampled into the blood-mud beneath the hooves of a charging Juggernaut.

## Fate & Present Status

Valkia was killed at the Siege of Averheim during the End Times, struck down in single combat with Ludwig Schwarzhelm, the Emperor's Champion. Both died together, trampled into the mud. For a bride of Khorne, there is no higher honour than death in open combat, skull to skull, without retreat.

## Verdict

Valkia is a singularity in the Chaos history of the Old World. The Chaos Gods take worshippers like grains of sand, but among all of them, only one woman rose from a tribal girl to the Blood God's own throne through pure, undiluted fury and was personally elevated to daemonhood. Her life had no Sigmarite nation-building ideals, no Nagash-like godhood ambitions, no Archaonian crisis of faith — only one trajectory of blinding purity: angrier and angrier, bloodier and bloodier, closer and closer to Khorne, until the two were one.

The killing of Locephax was the true pivot. Before that battle, she was a mortal tribal queen. After it, Khorne fixed his eyes upon her. Her journey across the Chaos Wastes to find the Blood God was less a pilgrimage than a verification: she would lay this Slaaneshi daemon's skull at Khorne's feet and prove her offering was the truest in all the world.

After her ascension, the extermination of her old tribe was the bluntest expression of Khorne's will. Khorne tolerates no betrayal, no weakness, no one abandoning a leader on the field — even a leader no longer mortal. The "clean death" she gave her daughter Eris and the "slow death" she gave her half-brother Edan were the last mortal traces she carried after ascension: respect for courage still lingered, but that trace had long gone cold.

Her death at Averheim was the most fitting full stop her life could have had. No intrigue, no retreat, no final words — a frontal assault, a shattered spine, dead in the mud. For a bride of Khorne, that is the highest glory there is.
