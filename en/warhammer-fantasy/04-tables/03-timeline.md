# A Chronicle of the Warhammer World

## The Age of the Old Ones and the Coming of Chaos

In the most ancient times, the Old Ones crossed the stars and arrived upon this world. They built the polar gates at the north and south, channelling the raw energy of Chaos to reshape the world. The Old Ones created the Lizardmen according to their design — the Slann, toad-like mage-priests to command the Winds of Magic; the Saurus, warriors; and the Skinks, artisans and scribes — all bound into a genetic directive known as the Great Plan. In the long ages that followed, the civilisation of the dragons once flourished, and the Dwarfs built a continent-spanning network of subterranean tunnels through the World's Edge Mountains, entering what later ages would call their Golden Age.

Around −5600 IC, the polar gates collapsed. The power of the four Chaos Gods — Khorne, Tzeentch, Nurgle, and Slaanesh — poured into the material world like a flood. The Old Ones fled, leaving the Lizardmen alone to execute the Great Plan. Thus began the first great Chaos incursion. Daemon legions swept the world; civilisation teetered on extinction. It was at this hour that Aenarion, hero of Ulthuan, walked into the Sacred Flame of Asuryan, drew the cursed blade of Khaine, and drove back Chaos as a mortal. After the war he wed the sorceress Morathi and sealed the god-blade back into the Flame, perishing in its fires himself. But his bloodline was henceforth wound with the curse of Khaine, shaping the destiny of the Elves for millennia to come.

## The Early Old World

With Chaos receding, the surviving races rebuilt upon the ruins. The Dwarf realm of Karaz Ankor (the Everlasting Realm of the Mountains) reached its zenith, while the ancient empire of Nehekhara rose in the southlands, its successive pharaohs raising pyramids in pursuit of immortality. Around −2750 IC, Malekith, son of the Phoenix King, rose in rebellion after being passed over as heir — he walked into the Sacred Flame of Asuryan to prove himself, was seared by the fire, and in fury led his followers into civil war. This Sundering tore Ulthuan apart. Malekith, defeated, fled to Naggaroth and founded the kingdom of the Dark Elves, vowing that one day he would reclaim the Phoenix Throne. Some eight centuries later, Dwarfs and High Elves went to war over a diplomatic insult — the shaving of a Dwarf ambassador's beard — in the War of the Beard. The high leadership of both ancient races was near-annihilated, and both entered a terminal decline.

Meanwhile, Nehekhara met its darkest chapter. Prince Nagash was captured by Dark Elves and learned forbidden magic; returning home, he murdered his brother to seize the throne, built the Black Pyramid, and invented necromancy. The kings of Nehekhara united in coalition against him and destroyed his physical form. Centuries later, Nagash's spirit gathered strength once more, raising the fortress of Nagashizzar in the Cursed Lands and unleashing the Great Ritual — a spell meant to awaken every dead being in Nehekhara simultaneously as an undead legion. Though the last pharaoh, Alcadizaar, interrupted the ritual with the Fellblade of meteoric iron, the residue of the spell continued to awaken all the Tomb Kings over the centuries that followed. The living civilisation of Nehekhara thus came to its end.

Queen Neferata of Lahmia, drawing on Nagash's indirect instruction, refashioned the Elixir of Life and created the Cup of Blood, making herself and her followers the world's first vampires. After Lahmia's fall, the nascent bloodlines scattered across the globe — Vlad von Carstein (originally Vashanesh) fled to Sylvania, seizing local dominion through marriage and becoming the seedbed of the coming vampire wars.

## The Age of Sigmar — the Founding of the Empire

Around −30 IC, Sigmar was born, son of an Unberogen tribal chieftain. As a youth he slew a giant boar with his bare hands, earning the epithet "Hammer-Bearer." After his father fell fighting Greenskins, Sigmar charged alone through the enemy lines and was acclaimed king of the tribe. Not long afterward he rescued the Dwarf High King Kurgan Ironbeard from captivity and was gifted the legendary warhammer Ghal Maraz, laying the foundation of the eternal alliance between Men and Dwarfs.

Over the next fifteen years, Sigmar — through marriage, alliance, and conquest — united the twelve principal human tribes of the central Old World. In 1 IC, an unprecedented Greenskin WAAAGH! poured from the World's Edge Mountains. The twelve tribes met them at the Battle of Black Fire Pass. Sigmar slew the Orc warlord "Bone-Crusher" with Ghal Maraz; the Greenskin host was shattered. That same year, Ar-Ulric, High Priest of the White Wolf, crowned Sigmar Emperor, and the Empire was formally founded.

Sigmar reigned for fifty years, establishing the Elector system, codifying the Imperial Law, and building a network of roads and border fortresses. Around 50 IC, he discovered that his own cousin, Geronius, had secretly fallen to the worship of Nurgle and plotted to spread a plague across the realm. Sigmar slew him with his own hand — but was wounded in the shoulder by a Nurglite blade, leaving a corruption-wound that would never heal. Several years later, he convened the Elector Counts, announced his abdication, named no successor, and walked alone into the World's Edge Mountains, hammer in hand, and vanished. For a thousand years thereafter, the Empire suffered the "Age of the Emperors" — ceaseless internecine wars and a repeatedly changing throne.

## The Middle Empire — Plague and the Vampire Wars

In 1111 IC, the Skaven unleashed the Black Death upon the Empire, then boiled out from below in their billions. Emperor Mandred rallied the remnants and slew the Skaven Warlord beneath the walls of Altdorf, earning the name "Skavenslayer" and driving the ratmen back underground. That same year, Nagash attempted to complete his apotheosis ritual, only to be grievously wounded by the Skaven Warlord Kritlik wielding the Fellblade of Black Hunger, and silenced once more.

Around 1200 IC, the Tomb Kings of Nehekhara awoke en masse. Settra the Imperishable — the King of Kings who had once conquered all Nehekhara — found his ancient realm transformed into a land of the dead amidst yellow sand and immediately launched a war of reunification, reconquering his former subordinates kingdom by kingdom. He left the most famous declaration in the Warhammer cosmos: "Settra does not serve."

In 1797 IC, Vlad von Carstein launched the First Vampire War. He led an undead host across the eastern Empire and reached the walls of Altdorf, coming near to conquering the entire realm. Grand Theogonist Wilhelm III faced Vlad upon the ramparts and slew him with a sacred hammer — but Vlad wore a resurrection ring. Ultimately Wilhelm infiltrated the enemy camp and stole the ring, and only then did Vlad truly fall. Thereafter Konrad and Mannfred launched the Second and Third Vampire Wars in succession; the undead threat from Sylvania persisted for a full century and a half.

## The Modern Era — the Storm of Chaos and the End Times

In 2301 IC, the third great Chaos incursion erupted. The Empire splintered before the Chaos hosts, until a simple friar named Magnus walked on foot through every province, calling upon the Elector Counts to set aside their quarrels and face the common foe. The allied host crushed the Chaos army at Kislev, and Magnus was crowned Emperor. With the aid of the High Elf Archmage Teclis, he established the Imperial Colleges of Magic, legalising the use of the Eight Winds among humans. The Empire entered a rejuvenation.

In 2502 IC, Karl Franz, Elector Count of Reikland, was elected Emperor. He lifted Ghal Maraz — which none had been able to wield for centuries — and was regarded as a ruler blessed by Sigmar. Yet two decades later, a greater storm descended. Archaon, the Everchosen, led the Chaos hosts south in the Storm of Chaos. The northern route cut through Kislev, the central thrust aimed at Middenheim, and the southern advance cut supply lines; the defences of the Old World hung by a thread. The Chaos army met ferocious resistance at Middenheim, and Sigmar's avatar Valten faced Archaon directly, forcing his retreat. The Old World drew a temporary breath, but doomsday was closing.

In 2525 IC, the End Times began. The Winds of Magic ran wild; the dead rose from the earth; the Skaven struck simultaneously across the globe. Arkhan the Black, with Mannfred von Carstein's aid, gathered the relics of Nagash and successfully resurrected the Lord of Undeath. Nagash completed his apotheosis — only to be dragged instantly into the Realm of Chaos by the sun god Ptra. The three Elven kindreds — High Elves, Dark Elves, and Wood Elves — united for the first time under Chaos's grinding pressure: Teclis secretly resurrected Malekith, who walked into the Flame of Asuryan and was revealed as the true chosen Phoenix King, his thousand-year road of darkness having been the forge to create a leader capable of uniting the whole race.

In 2528 IC, doomsday came. Archaon led the entire Chaos host south as every major power fell in turn. Grey Seer Thanquol presided over a ritual to pull the Great Horned Rat into the material world. The Chaos moon Morrslieb began its descent toward the earth. Archaon went deep beneath Middenheim and with his own hand severed the magical node sustaining the world. The Old World collapsed with a roar. Sigmar, possessing the body of Karl Franz, fought to the very last.

## The Age of Sigmar

After the Old World's destruction, the Mortal Realms precipitated from the residue of Chaos. Eight magical realms, corresponding to the eight Winds of Magic, took form in succession: Azyr the Realm of Heavens, Aqshy the Realm of Fire, Chamon the Realm of Metal, Ghyran the Realm of Life, Hysh the Realm of Light, Ghur the Realm of Beasts, Ulgu the Realm of Shadow, and Shyish the Realm of Death. Sigmar was transformed into the God-King ruling the Realm of Azyr, joining with other gods to form the Pantheon of Order. Mortal civilisation took root anew across the Realms — this era became known as the Age of Myth.

Chaos counterattacked swiftly, conquering nearly all the Realms during the Age of Chaos. Sigmar was forced to retreat to Azyr and seal the gates to the other Realms. Over the long ages that followed, he chose the finest souls from among the mortal heroes fallen in battle and reforged them with celestial lightning into the Stormcast Eternals — immortal thunder-warriors. On the day the Age of Sigmar officially began, the Stormcast Eternals descended from the heavens and launched the great counter-stroke, sweeping across the Realms. The Soul Wars had begun. Nagash ruled the Realm of Death, now allied with Sigmar, now breaking with him, seeking to claim every single soul for himself. Malekith was reborn as Malerion, ruling the Realm of Shadow. The Elven queen Alarielle ruled the Realm of Life, leading the sylvaneth. The races of the old world — Dwarfs became the sky-merchant Kharadron Overlords and the volcanic slayer Fyreslayers; the Lizardmen ascended as celestial Seraphon; the Skaven continued to spread beneath every Realm — all were now committed to this eternal war across eight spheres of existence.
