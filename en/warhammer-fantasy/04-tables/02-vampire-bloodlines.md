# The Vampire Bloodlines

The five bloodlines of the vampire race, ever since Neferata opened the Cup of Blood, have held dominion each in their own sphere for over three thousand years. The von Carsteins schemed for a crown, the Blood Dragons walked the path of knighthood, the Lahmians wove a hidden web, the Strigoi regressed into beasts, and the Necrarchs plumbed the depths of death. Each bloodline had its strengths, and each carried fatal flaws; all withered in the cataclysm of the End Times. What follows is a genealogy of their origins, arranged as a chronicle to trace the arcs of the bloodlines' rise and fall.

## I. The von Carstein Bloodline

The von Carsteins were the most powerful and most throne-reaching of the five bloodlines.

**Progenitor:** Vlad von Carstein, originally named Vashanesh, a prince of one of Nehekhara's ancient city-states. Around −1350 IC, Neferata hosted the Blood Feast; Vlad, Abhorash, W'soran, and Ushoran drank together from the Cup, becoming the first generation of vampires. After Lahmia's fall, Vlad crossed the World's Edge Mountains and arrived in the Imperial frontier province of Sylvania. Bleak, damp, and barren, Sylvania was the province the Empire cared least about. Vlad took a new name, wed Isabella — the mad daughter of Count Otto, locked away in a tower since childhood — and treated her with genuine devotion before turning her into a vampire as well. Together they ruled; when Otto died, Vlad succeeded by marriage and became, on parchment, a legitimate Elector Count.

**Later Generations:**
- Konrad von Carstein (?–2122 IC): Vlad's get, brutal and dim. He launched the Second Vampire War in 2097 IC with no strategy and no political programme — pure butchery, earning the Old World's universal revulsion. In 2122 IC, a combined Dwarf and Imperial army took his head in Sylvania. He had Vlad's ferocity but none of his guile.
- Mannfred von Carstein: The most cunning of Vlad's brood. He waged the Third Vampire War from 2122 to 2145 IC, with a weaker army than Vlad's but far greater powers of infiltration — he nearly paralysed the Elector system from within. He survived defeat and went underground, biding his time. During the End Times he was tasked with resurrecting Nagash, then betrayed his allies repeatedly, engineering his own doom.

**Consort:** Isabella von Carstein, Vlad's sole vulnerability. In the End Times she was corrupted by Nurgle. Seeing this, Vlad drove a stake through his own heart and died alongside her in the chaos of battle.

**Bloodline Traits:** The von Carsteins did not rely on stealth or assassination; they sought open sovereignty. Under their rule, living subjects farmed and paid taxes as normal, with a lord who never changed — not out of mercy, but from the cold calculation that "a living empire is more worth ruling than a wasteland." Among all bloodlines, only this one built an order resembling a state.

**Principle Domain:** All of Sylvania, with Castle Drakenhof as the seat of power, projecting influence over the eastern Imperial provinces.

**Assessment:** Vlad twice brought his armies to the gates of Altdorf, coming within inches of the crown. He was not defeated by Ghal Maraz; he was defeated by a nameless thief who stole his resurrection ring. A conqueror undone by a rodent's theft. At the very end he chose to array his forces on the side of the Empire, keeping Chaos beyond the line — self-serving, no doubt, but still the final, mournful roar of a would-be king.

## II. The Blood Dragon Bloodline

**Progenitor:** Abhorash, captain of the Lahmian royal guard. His physique was peerless, his martial skill supreme in his age. After drinking the blood, he was consumed with shame at the need for human blood, and swore to scour the world for a cure. He wandered the Old World alone for centuries, slaying monsters and dragons, until at last he faced an ancient red dragon in the north. After a brutal battle, Abhorash killed the dragon and drank its blood — the dragon's blood burned so fiercely that it washed away his thirst for human gore. From that day forth he needed no human blood, sustaining himself on draconic vitae alone, and thus he established a path: to tame the bloodlust through martial discipline.

**Later Generations:**
- Walach Harkon: The most outstanding of Abhorash's disciples. He built Blood Keep deep in the Grey Mountains, took in disciples, and institutionalised the Blood Dragon way — all who entered must renounce every worldly identity and live by knightly vows alone.
- The Red Duke: Originally the Duke of Aquitaine in Bretonnia, a warrior of towering renown. Turned vampire, he fell to the Blood Dragon bloodline and at one point led an undead army that ravaged several Bretonnian duchies before being driven into the shadows by a coalition. He spent his life torn between glory and curse, dying unresolved.

**Bloodline Traits:** The Blood Dragons make a faith of martial arts. They spin no intrigues, found no kingdoms, and take no part in the political chess of the other bloodlines. They roam the world year after year seeking a foe worthy of full effort — monsters, dragons, Chaos champions, even other vampires. They believe that only in a fight given everything can they prove they remain "men" and not "beasts."

**Principal Domain:** No fixed territory. Blood Keep once served as a bastion during the bloodline's resurgence before being abandoned. Blood Dragon knights live like errants, dwelling wherever their enemies are found.

**Assessment:** The Blood Dragons are the most tragic branch of the vampires — knowing the curse is irreversible, yet determined to subdue it through the blade. Abhorash slew a dragon and drank its blood, an act that seemed like "purification" but in truth merely swapped one addiction for another. Dragon's blood is irreproducible; later Blood Dragon knights could never escape human blood, the strictest discipline no match for instinct's rampage. But it is precisely this refusal to yield despite futility that gives the Blood Dragons, alone among the five bloodlines, a knight's peculiar dignity.

## III. The Lahmian Bloodline

**Progenitor:** Neferata, Nehekhara's first queen-pharaoh, the Queen of Lahmia. She is the pivotal figure in the origin of vampires — Nagash taught her the secret of the Elixir of Life, but she remade it in the depths of the Lahmian court into the Cup of Blood, more potent and with deeper side effects. In a sense, Neferata is closer than Nagash to being "the mother of vampires."

After Lahmia's fall, Neferata led her inner circle north, deep into the World's Edge Mountains, where she found an abandoned city and refashioned it into the Silver Pinnacle — a palace of luxury hidden amid the peaks. Thereafter she rarely acted directly, instead weaving a spy network spanning the entire Old World.

**Later Generations:** The Lahmian Sisterhood — an organisation of women that infiltrated noble courts, temple hierarchies, and even the Imperial Electoral Council. Its members used beauty, seduction, and the gift of blood to turn the powerful of every realm into their eyes and slaves. In the upper politics of the Old World, countless decisions that appeared normal had the Silver Pinnacle's hidden hand behind them.

**Bloodline Traits:** The Lahmians do not contest thrones, do not practise war, do not pursue scholarship. They do only one thing: control people. A lover, a handmaiden, a seemingly harmless distant cousin — any of them could be a thread leading back to the Silver Pinnacle. Among all bloodlines, they hide deepest and are hardest to root out.

**Principal Domain:** The Silver Pinnacle (deep in the central World's Edge Mountains). No open territory. But the upper social circles of the entire Old World constitute their invisible realm.

**Assessment:** Neferata is the hardest of all vampires to judge. She is neither a pure tyrant like Nagash, nor a king like Vlad, nor an ascetic like Abhorash. She is like a mother spider at the centre of her web, never stopping her weaving and controlling for four thousand years. Her weakness arises from precisely this — when the forces of Chaos surged and the whole world collapsed, no web, however fine, could catch the end of an age. The Silver Pinnacle was swallowed by Chaos; Neferata's fate is unknown. One who made control her ultimate pursuit ultimately failed to control her own fate.

## IV. The Strigoi Bloodline

**Progenitor:** Ushoran, also among the first generation of vampires. After Lahmia's fall, the band of fugitives he led retreated deep into the wilderness far from civilisation and, among ruins and tombs, built a kingdom recorded in no chronicle — Strigos.

**Later Generations:**
- Vorag (the Ghoul King): The most famous surviving leader after Strigos's fall, commanding a host of half-human, half-beast ghoul-kindred haunting the Old World's desolate barrows. His strength rivalled the Blood Dragons'; his appearance made a skeleton look dignified.
- Gashnag and other petty chieftains: The Strigoi have no unified system, having scattered into local ghoul colonies under various chiefs, their hierarchy confused, their breeding endogamous, gradually indistinguishable from the monsters of the grave.

**Bloodline Traits:** The tragedy of the Strigoi is this — they did not choose to degenerate into beasts, but their environment forced them to. Prolonged isolation from civilisation, closed breeding, and the compounding of the curse regressed their appearance into something half-human, half-beast. Their strength ranks near the top of the five bloodlines; their intelligence ranks at the bottom. More darkly ironic, their most vicious persecutors were often not men but other bloodlines — both the von Carsteins and the Lahmians regarded the Strigoi as "the disgrace of the vampire race" and repeatedly joined forces to purge them.

**Principal Domain:** The fallen realm of Strigos, afterward scattered across the wastes, ancient tombs, and abandoned cities of the Old World.

**Assessment:** The Strigoi are the purest tragedy among the five bloodlines. The other four all retained, to varying degrees, an attachment to having once been part of civilisation — the von Carsteins wanted a throne, the Blood Dragons wanted honour, the Lahmians wanted control, the Necrarchs wanted knowledge. Only the Strigoi lost even such attachments, worn away by time and degeneration. Their end was also the least conspicuous — no heroic death in battle, no assassination amid intrigue, but silently crushed in a forgotten corner of the wastes as the Chaos hosts rolled over them without a second thought.

## V. The Necrarch Bloodline

**Progenitor:** W'soran, originally a disciple under Nagash, who received the blood at the feast of Lahmia. He was among the very few vampires to have studied under both Nagash and Neferata. After receiving the blood, he advanced in the direction opposite to Abhorash — not subjugating the curse through martial arts, but understanding it through knowledge.

**Later Generations:**
- Melkhior: W'soran's direct disciple, also his deadliest academic rival. Legend tells that the two fought for three days and nights in an ancient tomb over a single necromantic grimoire, after which both vanished from all public record — none knows who slew whom.
- Zacharias the Everliving: The most famous Necrarch vampire of the late Imperial era. He successfully summoned and enslaved an Undead Dragon. At one point he sought to build his own undead kingdom in the southern Empire, and was surrounded and destroyed by a coalition of the Colleges of Magic.

**Bloodline Traits:** The Necrarchs are scholars taken to an extreme. They care nothing for appearance (the more withered the body, the purer the mental focus), nothing for power, nothing for the continuation of lineage. Their sole obsession: to plumb the ultimate depths of necromancy, to surpass Nagash. If the other bloodlines all sought to "keep living" under Nagash's shadow, the Necrarchs sought to "be better than him."

**Principal Domain:** No fixed domain; they dwell in isolated mountain towers, abandoned crypts, and lightless subterranean studies.

**Assessment:** The Necrarch ambition is the greatest among the five bloodlines — Nagash was merely the "first draft" who accidentally handed them necromancy; they mean to become "the sequel that surpasses the original." But four thousand years have passed, and not a single Necrarch has approached Nagash's level, let alone surpassed it. This is not a matter of intellect — Nagash's necromancy was born of some dark gift they cannot replicate, something innate, not learned. The Necrarchs can study until their bodies crumble to bone and their towers weather to dust; they will remain Nagash's second draft — neater script, never enough power.

## Commentary

The four-thousand-year history of the vampire race is, at root, a story of people building their own palaces on Nagash's foundations — palaces that never truly left their base.

Nagash invented the Elixir of Life, Neferata refashioned it, and the five bloodlines each developed it further — but no matter how far they walked, the magic in their veins remained Nagash's, their power dependent upon Nagash's system, their immortality built upon a house of cards of Nagash's making. The von Carsteins tried to package this with statecraft, the Blood Dragons tried to launder it with knighthood, the Lahmians tried to bypass it through political manipulation, the Strigoi were consumed and devoured by it, and the Necrarchs tried to surpass it through scholarship — all failed.

The cruelest irony lies in the outcome of the End Times: every bloodline system that tried to stand independent of Nagash crumbled, one by one, and the sole survivor was the Mannfred branch that returned to Nagash's banner. For a race in which freedom and immortality are the ultimate pursuit, this is nearly a verdict.

The price of so-called "undeath" is not the blood-thirst — that is only the surface. The true price is this: you live long enough to watch everything you built crushed by time, long enough to realise you can never escape the power that made you. Vampires can avoid death, but they cannot be free.

The rise and fall of the five vampire bloodlines can be summarised thus: a single cup of blood opened five dynasties. For over three thousand years they rose and fell in succession, and when the cataclysm of the End Times descended, not one bloodline could truly endure independent of Nagash. Their tragedy lies not in having chosen immortality, but in having mistaken immortality for the end of the road to freedom.
