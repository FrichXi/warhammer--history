# The Line of the Phoenix Kings

The Phoenix King, also called the Beloved of Asuryan, rules Ulthuan alongside the Everqueen upon the twin thrones. The Phoenix King presides over war and outward expansion; the Everqueen over internal affairs and the renewal of all living things. The Phoenix King is not hereditary — the princes of Ulthuan's ten kingdoms elect their candidate at the Shrine of Asuryan, and the chosen one must walk into the Sacred Flame and emerge unscathed to prove divine favour. From the first Phoenix King, Aenarion, to the End Times, there were eleven orthodox Phoenix Kings; two more took the throne briefly amidst the fires of the End Times, and at last Malekith fulfilled a millennia-long ambition to become the final Phoenix King. Listed below in order of accession.

---

## I. Aenarion — the Defender

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 80 (c. −5500 to −5420 IC), reigned 80 years.

Aenarion was the first, the greatest, and also the most tragic of the Elven Phoenix Kings.

When the first great Chaos incursion swept the world, the Elves of Ulthuan knew nothing of swords. Daemon legions shattered cities and desecrated the shrines of the Elven gods one by one. Aenarion, a mortal, walked into the Sacred Flame of Asuryan. The fire did not harm him — it remade him as the Phoenix King, the first Elf in history chosen by a god. He donned armour, took up arms, rallied the dragons to war by sheer force of will, and tamed the star-drake Indraugnir as his mount. In forty days and nights of bloodshed he drove back the forward edge of the Chaos tide.

But the cost of war crushed Aenarion's spirit. His first wife, Astarielle, and their two children were lost in the fall of Avelorn; he believed them dead. Driven by grief and fury, he flew to the Blighted Isle, drew the Sword of Khaine — Widowmaker — from its altar, a blade that could slay even gods. Afterward he wed the sorceress Morathi, who bore him a son, Malekith. The sword's power made him invincible, yet stained his bloodline with the curse of Khaine. Every swing of the blade planted seeds of ruin for his descendants.

In the final battle, the Archmage Caledor Dragontamer led the full might of Ulthuan's mages to the Isle of the Dead, weaving the Great Vortex to drain the Chaos energy sustaining the daemons. Aenarion held the shore alone, battling daemon champions for three days and nights, sustaining mortal wounds. The Vortex took shape, the daemons were banished back to the Realm of Chaos, and Aenarion returned the Sword of Khaine to its altar before breathing his last beside his dragon Indraugnir.

**Cause of Death:** Died of battle wounds. Fought off waves of Greater Daemons while defending the Vortex ritual. After the Vortex was complete, he returned the cursed blade and died upon his dragon's back.

---

## II. Bel-Shanaor — the Explorer

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 1670 (c. −4419 to −2749 IC), reigned 1,670 years.

A year after Aenarion's death, the princes gathered at the Shrine of Asuryan to elect a successor. Many expected the throne to pass to Aenarion's son Malekith — the late king's only son, a warrior of great renown. But the princes mistrusted the shadowy reputation of the Nagarythian court (where Morathi secretly spread the worship of Slaanesh) and chose instead the prince of Tiranoc, Bel-Shanaor. Malekith publicly accepted the decision, swearing the first oath of fealty to the new king.

Bel-Shanaor ushered in the first golden age of the Elves. He dispatched explorers across the world, planting colonies along the coasts of the Old World, the Southlands, and far-flung island chains. Elves and Dwarfs met for the first time, signing treaties of friendship and establishing trade. Ulthuan's ten kingdoms flourished, and Elven fleets covered every sea.

But beneath the gold, currents stirred. Morathi used the Nagarythian court as a base to secretly found the Cult of Pleasure devoted to Slaanesh. The cult's tendrils reached the nobility of all ten kingdoms, and some even suspected Bel-Shanaor himself of involvement. Malekith returned to Ulthuan on the pretext of purging the cult, convened the princes at the Shrine of Asuryan, publicly accused Bel-Shanaor of belonging to the cult, and poisoned the old Phoenix King with tainted wine.

**Cause of Death:** Poisoned by Malekith. Afterward Malekith stepped into the Sacred Flame to claim the throne — but the Flame judged him unworthy and seared him near to death. Malekith's followers then slaughtered the princes in the shrine, triggering the Elven civil war known as the Sundering.

---

## III. Caledor I — the Conqueror

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 552 (c. −2749 to −2198 IC), reigned 552 years.

After Malekith's betrayal and Bel-Shanaor's death, the princes scrambled to elect a king to salvage the crisis. They chose the grandson of Caledor Dragontamer — the prince of Caledor, originally named Imrik. His coronation flames had barely settled before Malekith raised the banner of rebellion in Nagarythe, and the Sundering erupted in full.

Caledor I's greatest achievement was holding the Great Vortex. After failing to take Ulthuan by force, Malekith gambled on unravelling the enchantments binding the Vortex, hoping to unleash Chaos once more and destroy Ulthuan. Caledor and his loyal mages struck first, reflecting the backlash of magical energy back upon Nagarythe — the earth split, and colossal waves swallowed over half of Nagarythe's realm in what came to be called "the Great Sinking." Malekith and his followers — henceforth the Dark Elves, or Druchii — fled overseas, founding a kingdom of hate upon the frozen wastes of Naggaroth.

After the war, Caledor fortified the fortress chain guarding Ulthuan's inner sea, reclaimed the Blighted Isle, and resealed the Sword of Khaine. He laid the defensive framework upon which the High Elves would survive for millennia. Yet he never managed to destroy Malekith entirely — the debt he left uncollected would be settled by no one.

**Cause of Death:** Surrounded at sea by Dark Elf corsairs. His flagship was sunk. Caledor, knowing that capture meant a fate worse than death, threw himself into the waves.

---

## IV. Caledor II — the Warrior

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 600 (c. −2198 to −1599 IC), reigned 600 years.

The princes elevated Caledor I's son, hoping the son would match the father. They soon discovered the difference between the two was not ability but wisdom. Caledor I was measured and prudent; Caledor II was arrogant and rash.

To shatter relations between Elves and Dwarfs, Malekith ordered Dark Elves to pose as Ulthuan warriors and raid Dwarf caravans. The Dwarf High King Gotrek Starbreaker sent emissaries to Ulthuan demanding answers. Caledor II's response: "The Phoenix King grants boons, not answers to extortion." When the Dwarfs sent a second embassy, he had the delegates' beards shaved — to a Dwarf, the gravest insult imaginable — and told them to come to Ulthuan themselves to "collect compensation."

Thus erupted the War of the Beard, also called the War of Vengeance. The Dwarfs laid siege to Tor Alessi, the greatest Elven port in the Old World, in fourteen pitched battles. Desperate, Caledor II took the field himself. In the fourteenth siege, he faced High King Gotrek in single combat and was struck down on the spot. Gotrek took the Phoenix Crown from his corpse and carried it back to Karaz-a-Karak — where it remains to this day, sealed in the Great Book of Grudges, an eternal testament to the hatred between the two races.

**Cause of Death:** Slain in single combat before the walls of Tor Alessi by the Dwarf High King Gotrek Starbreaker. The Phoenix Crown was taken and remains sealed in the Book of Grudges at Karaz-a-Karak.

---

## V. Caradryel — the Conciliator

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 604 (c. −1599 to −996 IC), reigned 604 years.

After Caledor II fell on the battlefield, the Elves found themselves in an impossible position: the War of the Beard hung in the balance while Dark Elf fleets struck Ulthuan again, seizing the Blighted Isle and much of the Shadowlands. The Elves bled on two fronts simultaneously; their armies and fleets were near exhaustion.

In desperation, the princes elected Caradryel, prince of Yvresse. He could not have been more different from his predecessor — calm, pragmatic, indifferent to vanity. His first decree stunned the court: abandon all overseas colonies, recall every soldier and settler, and make Ulthuan itself the absolute priority. Many colonists refused to leave, most famously the Elves of Athel Loren — they chose independence, eventually evolving into the Wood Elves. Caradryel commissioned a new Phoenix Crown, saying, "Better to lose a crown than a kingdom."

He delegated warfare to his generals (foremost among them Tethlis, later to become Phoenix King) and devoted himself to rebuilding the logistics and fortifications of the inner-sea defences. The Druchii onslaught was eventually contained, and Ulthuan narrowly survived the strangulation of two-front war. Caradryel was among the rare Phoenix Kings to die peacefully.

**Cause of Death:** Died of old age — the first Phoenix King to pass peacefully.

---

## VI. Tethlis — the Slayer

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 306 (c. −996 to −691 IC), reigned 306 years.

The finest general to serve under Caradryel. His entire family had been slaughtered in a Dark Elf raid, leaving him with a single thought: root the Druchii out of Ulthuan entirely. He ignored politics and palaces upon taking the throne, pouring every ounce of energy into one thing — war.

Acknowledging the decline of the dragons (the great drakes had begun to slumber, their numbers plummeting), he rebuilt the Citizen Levy as a disciplined army based on drill rather than dragon riders. He then launched a series of merciless campaigns, compressing the Dark Elves northward step by step. He stormed the Druchii capital of Anlec in the Shadowlands, razed it to the ground, and left no survivors. Finally, at the Battle of the Waves, he landed on the Blighted Isle and annihilated its garrison, reclaiming the lost territory.

With Ulthuan retaken, Tethlis marshalled his forces for a counter-invasion of Naggaroth. But he stopped at the altar of Khaine — where Widowmaker still rested upon its stone. What happened next is disputed. One account says he tried to draw the blade to gain the power to defeat Malekith, and his own Phoenix Guard cut him down on the spot. Another says he was assassinated by concealed Druchii agents. Whatever the truth, Tethlis died on the Blighted Isle, one step from vengeance.

**Cause of Death:** Perished before the Altar of Khaine on the Blighted Isle. Either slain by his own Phoenix Guard as he reached for the cursed blade, or murdered by Dark Elf assassins. The planned counter-invasion of Naggaroth was abandoned.

---

## VII. Bel-Korhadris — the Scholar King

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 1190 (c. −691 to 499 IC), reigned 1,190 years.

After the long wars, the princes selected the scholarly Prince of Saphery, Bel-Korhadris. His was the longest reign of any Phoenix King, and he delivered Ulthuan into its second golden age. The Dark Elves, gutted by Tethlis's bloodletting, fell silent for a thousand years.

Bel-Korhadris's greatest legacy was the White Tower of Hoeth. In Saphery he raised this colossal edifice — library, magical academy, and house of study combined — assembling all the knowledge the Elven race had ever accumulated. Within its halls he founded the Order of Loremasters, compiled the *Book of Days*, and established the Swordmasters of Hoeth to guard the tower's wisdom. He did not neglect the martial arts, encouraging the nobility to hunt and drive back monsters spilling from the Annulii Mountains.

Henceforth the Elves' scholars, mages, and military elite all emerged from the White Tower. Bel-Korhadris died of old age shortly after its completion; it is said his spirit still lingers in the tower's catacombs, occasionally conversing with the Loremasters.

**Cause of Death:** Died of old age, shortly after the White Tower's completion.

---

## VIII. Aethis — the Poet

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 623 (499–1121 IC), reigned 623 years.

Aethis inherited an Ulthuan as stable as bedrock from Bel-Korhadris. There had been too much peace. The Dark Elves seemed to have vanished, the Dwarfs were distant and no longer hostile, and Mankind still floundered in ignorance. None perceived the rot beneath the surface — Elven birth rates were falling, the great cities silently emptying.

Aethis was a poet-king. He based his court at Saphery, gathering Ulthuan's finest sculptors, musicians, architects, and scribes. Half the Elven literary and architectural masterpieces that survive to later ages were created during his reign. Trade reached as far as Cathay, and Lothern became the wealthiest port in the world. Alongside this prosperity came the complete atrophy of military readiness. Armies were cut, fleets rotted, and the nobility diverted their energy from exercise to court intrigue. Northern border lords who sounded warnings were drowned out by court slander — the whistleblowers found themselves isolated.

The darkness had never truly gone. When Druchii spies surfaced and the truth came to light, it was already too late.

**Cause of Death:** Stabbed with a poisoned blade by his chief minister, who was in fact a Naggarothi plant.

---

## IX. Morvael — the Impetuous

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 383 (1121–1503 IC), reigned 383 years.

Word of the chief minister's assassination of the Phoenix King spread across Ulthuan, shocking the realm. The princes chose the High Loremaster of the White Tower — Morvael of Yvresse — as the ninth Phoenix King. He was deeply learned but had zero military or administrative experience.

Eager to restore face, Morvael ordered an expedition against Naggaroth almost immediately after his coronation — it was annihilated, the fleet destroyed in a Druchii ambush. This disaster invited a full Dunk Elf counterstroke: Malekith's armada struck Ulthuan directly, retook the Blighted Isle, rebuilt Anlec, and besieged the Griffon Gate. The Elves were caught utterly off guard; the defence nearly collapsed.

For the next century and more, Morvael held on in fear. He recognised his own limitations with painful clarity and made a rare wise choice: handing full field command to the Caledorian general Mentheus while withdrawing to the rear to manage logistics and purge internal spies and surviving pleasure cults. He instituted universal conscription — the Citizen Levy system — obliging every adult Elf to serve, structurally remedying the chronic manpower shortage. But deep inside, this scholar-king was tormented by nightmares and shame; he believed himself an unworthy Phoenix King.

In the final siege of Anlec, the High Elves again prevailed, but Mentheus fell leading a charge. This news broke Morvael. He walked alone into the Sacred Flame of Asuryan — as Aenarion had once done — but the Flame did not accept him.

**Cause of Death:** Self-immolation in the Sacred Flame of Asuryan. Mentally shattered, he walked into the fire and was consumed. His ashes were scattered into the Inner Sea.

---

## X. Bel-Hathor — the Sage

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to 661 (1503–2163 IC), reigned 661 years.

The princes deadlocked between pro-war and pro-peace factions, ultimately choosing Prince Bel-Hathor of Saphery as a compromise candidate — a pedantic old scholar who seemed eccentric and easy to manipulate. They misjudged.

On his first day in office, Bel-Hathor showed he was no puppet. He rejected the war faction's calls for a transoceanic expedition: "Our people grow fewer by the year — we cannot afford a foreign war." He invested the realm's strength in defence instead. He summoned Ulthuan's most powerful mages and spent decades weaving a magical barrier around the entire island — mists, mirages, treacherous currents, and phantom reefs enveloped Ulthuan such that no foreign vessel could approach without an Elven pilot. Mortal sailors spread tales of "the Elven island that eats ships" — a direct result of this barrier.

Bel-Hathor did not seal Ulthuan entirely. He dispatched Prince Finubar of Lothern to tour the Old World and report on the rising human realms and the Wood Elves' condition. When Finubar returned advocating openness, Bel-Hathor agreed to open Lothern as a trade port — the beginning of the Elves' renewed formal contact with the outside world.

**Cause of Death:** Died of old age. Left a testament recommending Finubar as his successor.

---

## XI. Finubar — the Seafarer

**Reign:** Phoenix King Year 1 to c. 363 (2163 IC – the End Times), reigned approximately 363 years.

The first Phoenix King to ascend on the recommendation of his predecessor rather than by election alone. Finubar, Prince of Lothern, had sailed, traded, and travelled the Old World since youth — his was the widest vision of any Elven monarch. He lacked the typical High Elven arrogance toward humanity, so much so that his frequent diplomatic missions abroad led court officials to impeach him for "absence from his post." The Everqueen Alarielle personally interceded on his behalf to quash the charges.

His reign saw two great crises. The first was the Great Chaos Incursion — Malekith himself led the main Druchii host against Ulthuan, a war shouldered for Finubar by the twin heroes Tyrion and Teclis, whom Finubar thereafter drew into his inner council. The second was the Greenskin warlord Grom the Paunch bypassing the World's Edge Mountains and invading Ulthuan's eastern shores — Elves had never suffered Orcs burning and pillaging on their home soil before, and the nation was stunned. Both crises were barely weathered, but Elven strength had gravely diminished.

When the End Times came, Malekith marched south once more. One of Finubar's most trusted vassals — Prince Imrik of Caledor — secretly turned traitor and opened the Eagle Gate. Malekith infiltrated Finubar's tower and slew him with his own hand.

**Cause of Death:** Personally assassinated by Malekith during the End Times. His death threw Ulthuan's court into chaos; Tyrion was hastily proclaimed regent, and events spiralled toward catastrophe.

---

## XII · The End Times — Tyrion — Avatar of Khaine

Tyrion was Ulthuan's greatest warrior, the general most trusted at Finubar's court. After Finubar's assassination, the princes unanimously named him regent — one step from formal coronation as Phoenix King, though there was no time for ceremony.

Tyrion faced an Elven apocalypse without precedent: the Dark Elves had breached the Griffon Gate, Chaos stirred in the north, and allies dwindled. Desperation drove him to the Altar of Khaine — as Aenarion had done before — and he drew Widowmaker. The sword's power made him unchallengeable on the battlefield, but the curse devoured his mind. He was no longer Tyrion; he was the vessel of Khaine's will.

Teclis watched his brother slide step by step into the abyss and made the hardest, riskiest decision of all: he resurrected Malekith. He believed that only one who had walked a thousand years of darkness and suffering could truly comprehend Asuryan's true design.

**Cause of Death:** Slain during the Elven civil war of the End Times by Malekith (who had by then been accepted by the Sacred Flame) — or rather, the part of Khaine within him was killed, while Tyrion's soul was set free.

---

## XIII · The End Times — Malekith — the True Phoenix King

Malekith's story is the longest arc in the entire Warhammer cosmos. He was the son of Aenarion, architect of the Sundering, and the Witch King of the Dark Elves for millennia. But during the End Times, Teclis — in a staggering act of judgement — resurrected him and brought him back to Ulthuan to walk into the Flame of Asuryan, which had split into two halves: light and darkness.

This time, the Flame accepted him. Asuryan had not wanted a flawless heir raised in peacetime, but rather one who had walked the darkest of roads, endured the deepest suffering, committed the gravest wrongs, and yet chosen to return. Malekith went from one consumed by fire to the true Phoenix King — the one he should have been a thousand years before. As he emerged from the Flame, his burns healed.

Malekith then united the remnants of the High Elves, Dark Elves, and Wood Elves, leading the combined Elven host against Chaos and fighting to the very end of the Old World. After the world's destruction, he became Malerion in the Mortal Realms — the god of the Realm of Shadow, persisting in the form of shadow and secrecy.

---

## Final Appraisal: The Tragic Cycle of the Phoenix Kings

Looking across the entire line of Phoenix Kings — from Aenarion plunging into the Sacred Flame to Malekith's rebirth in that same fire — a repeating trajectory runs through nearly every king's fate. This is no accident; it is a structural tragedy rooted deep in Elven nature.

**The First Layer: the Curse of Aenarion.** The first Phoenix King drew the Sword of Khaine, saving the world with cursed power. From that moment, his bloodline became the vector of the curse. His son Malekith fell into darkness because of it; his descendant Tyrion repeated the act of drawing the blade. Every Phoenix King wrestled with this invisible bloodline, and most lost.

**The Second Layer: the path of the obsessive king.** Caledor I swore to destroy Malekith and could not; Tethlis died a step from revenge; Morvael bore a weight never meant for his shoulders and chose the fire. These faces share a pattern: each ascended with an impossible task — the annihilation of Malekith — and spent their entire reign fighting it, only to end in tragedy. Yet the peace-time Phoenix Kings fared no better: Bel-Shanaor was poisoned amid song and dance, Aethis stabbed at the height of culture, Finubar betrayed by one he trusted. The Elves never learned how not to slacken in strength, nor how not to panic in decline.

**The Third Layer: the irreversible decline of the Elven race.** From Bel-Shanaor's golden age of colonisation to Finubar's island redoubt, the dominion and ambition of the Phoenix Kings shrank relentlessly. Caledor II destroyed the overseas colonies, Caradryel abandoned more, Bel-Hathor simply shrouded the entire island in mist. Elven power diminished permanently with every war — the Elves who died under each Phoenix King could never return, the dragons fell deeper into slumber, and humanity's birth rate and the Skaven's fecundity were something the Elves could never match. No Phoenix King could reverse this structural decline.

**The Fourth Layer — and perhaps the sole redemption: Asuryan's ultimate design.** When the fates of these Phoenix Kings are laid side by side, an astonishing narrative emerges: Malekith — the name vilified in the roll of Phoenix Kings for ten thousand years — was the one the Sacred Flame had always been waiting for. Not because his virtue surpassed his predecessors, but because the long night he had walked made him the only one capable of reuniting the fractured three kindreds of the Elves. The first, Aenarion, was the warrior. The ten Phoenix Kings after him were explorer, conqueror, scholar, peacenik, berserker, poet, and recluse — and the final one had to return after experiencing it all, to assemble the scattered pieces into a whole. Asuryan's standard of testing was never human perfection; it was the total experience of darkness and failure.

From Aenarion plunging into the flame to Malekith's rebirth within it: this forms a closed loop spanning six thousand years. The beginning was sacrifice; the end was return. The eleven generations in between — whether brilliant, mediocre, or tragic — were all merely paving the way for that final moment.
