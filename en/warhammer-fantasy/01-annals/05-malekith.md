# 05 · Malekith, the Witch King

## Overview

Malekith, son of Aenarion the first Phoenix King, the most legitimate heir to the throne of Ulthuan. He was rejected in the election for Phoenix King, walked into the Flame of Asuryan and was burned into something half-living, half-spectre, then unleashed the Sundering, founded the dark kingdom of Naggaroth, and ruled as the Witch King for five thousand years. In the narrative of Warhammer Fantasy, he is the traitor most hated by the High Elves and the tyrant most feared by the Dark Elves — until the End Times, when the flames revealed the truth: he was the true Phoenix King chosen by Asuryan all along. Five millennia of exile and suffering were nothing but the god's cruel purification. Before the Old World collapsed, he united the three Elven kindreds, and in the Age of Sigmar he ascended as Malerion, lord of the Realm of Shadow.

## Origin

Malekith was born into the highest bloodline of Ulthuan — his father was Aenarion, the first Phoenix King who saved the world, and his mother was Morathi, the most beautiful Elven sorceress of her age. He was born at the close of the first great Chaos invasion, which meant he entered life already standing in the enormous shadow of his father's sacrifice: Aenarion drew the cursed Sword of Khaine and drove back Chaos, at the cost of binding himself and his entire bloodline to an eternal curse.

From childhood, Malekith was the template of a perfect heir: courageous, decisive, covered in battle honours, combining his father's gift for war with his mother's political instinct. With the first Phoenix King dead, the Elven princes needed to choose a new leader. By blood and by deed, Malekith was the most obvious candidate in the world.

The princes chose Bel-Shanaar. Not him.

That decision changed everything. The reasons may have been more than one: the princes feared the curse of Khaine that came with Aenarion's blood, and they feared Morathi's widening influence at court. Whatever the cause, Malekith was passed over. He accepted the result outwardly, taking the post of supreme general and devoting himself to purging the Chaos remnants still clinging to Ulthuan's islands — in this period, Malekith remained one of the greatest heroes in Elven history: cleansing Chaos, establishing diplomatic ties with the Dwarfs, founding overseas colonies. All of it was his work.

But there was one person who did not intend to let this go.

## Deeds

### The Flame of Asuryan and the Sundering

Morathi, Malekith's mother, presented herself publicly as a widowed Elven noblewoman. In secret, she was a devotee of Slaanesh. She directed the Cult of Pleasure, spreading corruption through Ulthuan's shadows, while whispering ceaselessly into her son's ear: your throne was stolen, Bel-Shanaar is unworthy, the Phoenix Crown is yours.

For a time, Malekith resisted this path. But political hostility and his mother's long manipulation ate through his restraint. When Bel-Shanaar died under suspicious circumstances — the official record says "poisoning" — Malekith resolved to prove his legitimacy in the most direct way possible: he would walk into the Flame of Asuryan.

This was the ritual every Phoenix King had to complete before coronation. The flame would burn away the impure and leave the god-chosen intact. Malekith stepped into the fire. All of Ulthuan waited to watch him be reborn.

He was not. The flame judged him unworthy. It burned him until his flesh was blackened, his skin peeling, barely a breath left in his body.

That burning is the single great watershed of Elven history. By every law of the Asur, a man rejected by the sacred fire could never again lay claim to the throne. But Malekith, in the agony and towering rage of his burns, chose another path. He unleashed his magic from within the fire itself, struck down the Elven nobles who had opposed him, and called his followers to arms.

This was the Sundering — a civil war that consumed centuries. Brother killed brother. Dragon met dragon. Magic tore the sky. In the final, decisive battle, Malekith attempted to unmake the great magical Vortex that stabilises Ulthuan, to sink the entire continent — he would rather see it beneath the waves than belong to another. The plan half-succeeded and half-failed. One corner of Ulthuan was swallowed by the sea. Malekith fled north with his followers into the frozen wastes.

### The Witch King and Naggaroth

The exiles built a new kingdom on the ice-bound continent of the far north — Naggaroth. The land was barren, sunless, brutal, and it slowly tempered the soul of every inhabitant into the same quality. Malekith's body, scorched by the Flame of Asuryan, could never heal by natural means. He could survive only sealed inside a specially forged suit of magical armour. No one ever saw his face again.

He styled himself the Witch King. For five thousand years, Naggaroth was a black-iron empire built on slavery. Dark Elf fleets raided the coasts of the Old World and as far south as Nehekhara for living captives. Their military academies produced dread knights, Black Guard, and cold-blooded assassins. The great noble houses professed loyalty to the Witch King in public while plotting each other's murder in private. Morathi's Cult of Pleasure and the blood-cult of Khaine waged shadow wars against each other. And Malekith himself remained trapped inside that suit of armour — a living dead man, rejected by a god, scarred by fire, driven by hatred.

He launched at least seven major invasions of Ulthuan. Each began with a naval landing and ended in long, bloody stalemate. On the other side, generation after generation of Phoenix Kings treated "hold Ulthuan and keep Malekith out" as the most fundamental national policy. The two sides fought for five thousand years and became mirrors of each other, unable to exist without the enemy they defined themselves against.

### The End Times: The Flame Speaks

Everything turned on its head in the final chapters of Warhammer Fantasy.

In the End Times, the legions of Chaos swallowed nearly every front in the Old World. The High Elves were pushed to the brink of extinction. The Phoenix Throne stood empty. Doom was at hand. And Teclis — the greatest mage of the High Elves, a man who never believed in coincidence — did something that detonated across Ulthuan: he sought out Malekith and urged him to return.

Teclis's logic was brutally simple. For five thousand years, every generation believed Malekith had been rejected by the Flame. But if the Flame rejected him, why did he survive? Every impure soul who ever walked into the Flame of Asuryan died. Malekith was the only one who lived.

Malekith returned to Ulthuan. He walked into the Flame of Asuryan a second time.

This time, the fire did not burn him. It healed him.

Five thousand years of burns closed in the fire. The armour fell away from his body, revealing a whole human face beneath. In that moment, the truth was laid bare: Malekith had always been the true Phoenix King chosen by Asuryan. The problem was this — Asuryan did not want a proud, naturally perfect heir. He wanted an heir who had been rejected, cast into darkness, and forced to spend five thousand years in agony learning everything. The Flame's first touch was not a rejection. It was the beginning of a long, brutal tempering.

Malekith, Phoenix King — destiny returned the crown to him at the moment when that crown had crushed everything, when the Old World itself had begun to collapse.

He united the High Elves, the Dark Elves, and the Wood Elves under a single command, leading the last Elven host against Chaos and holding the line until the Old World's final hour. It is hard to call this a "victory" — the world was still severed at its foundations by Archaon and sank into Chaos — but at least the Elves did not perish in mutual hatred.

## Fate & Present Status

### Age of Sigmar: Malerion

When the Old World collapsed and the Mortal Realms crystallised out of Chaos, Malekith ascended as the god and ruler of Ulgu, the Realm of Shadow, taking the name **Malerion**, meaning "Lord of Shadows." He commands the Shadow Aelves and maintains a complex, arm's-length relationship with Morathi (now a core goddess of the Khainite murder-cult), Teclis, and the Pantheon of Order.

For the man who was defined by hatred for five thousand years, the flame of the End Times accomplished at least one thing: it proved that he was never wrong in the first place.

## Verdict

Malekith is the hardest figure in the Warhammer universe to categorise simply.

He is the greatest of villains. Five thousand years of Dark Elf predation, enslavement, and blood-soaked internal power struggles cannot be laundered by any justification. Naggaroth was an empire built on the bones and blood of slaves, and the Witch King stood at the apex of that entire machinery of violence.

He is also the most wronged tragedy. If the princes had not passed him over, if Bel-Shanaar had not been "poisoned," if the trial of Asuryan had not required five thousand years to complete — he would never have become the Witch King. The entire Elven civil war, at its root, was detonated by a divine flame over which he had no control.

Sigmar exited the stage after completing his work and became a god. Nagash was a pure creature of ambition; every defeat only made him find a new posture for his resurrection. Archaon chose to hate all gods. Settra refused to submit to anyone.

Only Malekith — his entire story is a road split in two. The first half: the wronged becomes the wrongdoer. The second half: the wrongdoer is proven to have been right from the beginning. When you reach the moment in the End Times where he walks into the Flame a second time, what you feel is not the curtain call of a villain. It is a man, misunderstood by his entire race for five millennia, finally taking off the armour.
