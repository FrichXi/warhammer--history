# 03 · Archaon, the Everchosen

## Overview

Archaon, the Everchosen of Chaos, the Three-Eyed King, the man who set the End Times in motion and destroyed the Old World. In the long history of the Warhammer cosmos, Sigmar founded the Empire, Nagash invented necromancy, Settra conquered Nehekhara — and Archaon brought all of it to an end. He fell from Sigmar's most devout Templar Knight to become the supreme champion of the four Chaos Gods, and with his own hands he hurled the Old World into the abyss. Even after the collapse of that world, when the Mortal Realms were born anew among the stars, he remained the supreme commander of the Grand Alliance of Chaos, locked in eternal duel with Sigmar the God-King. He is the most destructive — and the most tragic — figure in the entire narrative of Warhammer Fantasy.

## Origin

Archaon's birth name was Diederick Kastner. He was born in the Imperial province of Nordland, the son of a Sigmarite Templar Knight. His mother had been raped by a Chaos marauder, and Diederick was the child of that violation — a fact unknown to anyone until he was grown. His adoptive father, the Templar, raised him as his own, instructing him in the strict doctrines of Sigmar. Diederick became one of the most brilliant Templars of his generation, devout to the point of zealotry, dedicated above all to purging the forces of Chaos. His faith was so unshakeable that few among his fellow Templars could match it.

The turning point came during a mission to cleanse a Chaos warband. In the ruins of a burned-out Chaos shrine, he found secret scrolls documenting the truth of his birth. What they recorded shattered him: he was not the son of a Templar Knight. He was the son of a Chaos marauder. The blood in his veins belonged to the very thing he had sworn his life to destroy.

This discovery did not make him surrender to fate. It drove him into a deeper crisis of faith. He began devouring the forbidden Chaos texts the Church had suppressed, hunting desperately for evidence that Sigmar had "abandoned" him. His logic grew extreme and precise: if the highest god does not protect his most faithful servant, then either the gods are impotent, or they simply do not care about the fate of mortals. Both conclusions led to the same place: these gods deserve to die.

In a state of mind woven from despair and towering rage, Diederick walked alone into the farthest north — into the Chaos Wastes. He found the legendary Temple of Altdorf, the holy sanctum of the Chaos Gods. There, of his own free will, he accepted every trial the four gods set before him. When he emerged, Diederick Kastner no longer existed. What walked out was Archaon, the Everchosen of Chaos.

## Deeds

### Gathering the Six Treasures of Chaos

The coronation of an Everchosen is not a single ceremony, but a pilgrimage. Archaon had to gather the Six Treasures of Chaos, an iron law passed down since the time of Morkar, the first Everchosen.

The first was the **Crown of Domination**, hidden within the first great Chaos shrine deep in the Wastes. It is the symbol of sovereign rule over Chaos — whoever dons it, the tribes of the north will acclaim as their one true master. The second was the **Eye of Sheerian**, originally Morkar's, granting its bearer the power of prescience. The third was the **Sword of Dorghar** — also called "Kingslayer" — the daemon-blade once wielded by Morkar, forged with a bound daemon that thirsts for the blood of all living things. The fourth was the **Armour of Morkar**, hammered from metal of the Realm of Chaos, proof against any blade. The fifth was the **Steed of the Apocalypse**, a three-headed daemon-steed from the Realm of Chaos whose iron hooves leave nothing alive where they tread. The sixth was the **Mark of Chaos** — the supreme mark bestowed jointly by Khorne, Tzeentch, Nurgle, and Slaanesh, signifying their simultaneous recognition of a single supreme champion.

With the Six Treasures assembled, Archaon was no longer a fallen Templar. He was the incarnate will of the four Chaos Gods in the mortal world — he was the apocalypse itself.

### Uniting the North

The second task Archaon undertook was to unite the tribes of the Chaos Wastes. The northern tribes had always fought among themselves, slaughtering one another in the eternal games between the Blood God and the Changer of Ways. For a thousand years, Chaos had never been able to grind the southern civilisations to dust — not for want of strength, but for want of unity.

Archaon brought this to an end. Bearing the Six Treasures, he rode across the Wastes, defeating or subjugating every warband chieftain who dared challenge him. The berserkers of Khorne, the sorcerer-kings of Tzeentch, the blighted lords of Nurgle, the blade-dancers of Slaanesh — every force of Chaos converged beneath his banner. For the first time, the Chaos Wastes were united under a single will. With his own hands, he gathered the scattered violence of Chaos and forged it into a spearhead aimed directly south.

### The Storm of Chaos

In 2522 IC, Archaon launched an invasion of the Old World on a scale never seen before — what posterity would call the Storm of Chaos.

His host swept south in three great columns. The northern thrust drove into Kislev, besieging the city of Praag until not a stone was dry. The southern column swung wide to cut the Empire's supply lines. The central column, commanded by Archaon himself, drove straight for Middenheim, the City of the White Wolf, the northern capital of the Empire.

The Siege of Middenheim was the climax of the Storm of Chaos. Before the city walls, Archaon faced Valten — the mortal vessel of Sigmar — in direct combat. Two utterly opposed forces collided at the gates: on one side, the will to annihilation channelled through the Everchosen; on the other, the divine power of Sigmar made manifest through a mortal champion. The duel reached no decisive end. Archaon ultimately failed to take Middenheim, but his withdrawal was strategic. The Storm of Chaos did not achieve total tactical victory, yet on the strategic level it revealed a terrifying truth: the Empire's defences could be torn open.

### The End Times

The true endgame unfolded after the Storm of Chaos, in the End Times themselves.

This time, Archaon did not seek cities or territory. His objective was direct and absolute: the destruction of the Old World itself. He marched south again at the head of the Chaos legions, and this time, no force on earth could stop him. The combined armies of the Empire, Bretonnia, and Kislev collapsed before his advance. The three Elven kindreds united, the surviving Dwarf holds, the undead legions of the Tomb Kings — every power in the Old World was drawn into the final war, and at the centre of the Chaos host, Archaon was always the absolute core.

In the end, he descended into the secret passages beneath Middenheim and with his own hands severed the nodal point that held the world's magical equilibrium in place. The Old World collapsed in his grip. Continents sank. Oceans tipped. Civilisation turned to ash. The Empire that Sigmar had built with his own hands, the kingdom of death that Nagash had coveted for millennia, Ulthuan that the Elves called their pride — all of it ended at the hands of a Templar Knight abandoned by faith.

## Fate & Present Status

### The Age of the Mortal Realms

The destruction of the Old World was not Archaon's terminus. When the Mortal Realms coalesced among the stars, the Grand Alliance of Chaos took form, and Archaon's station did not diminish — it elevated from "Destroyer" to "Conqueror." He is the highest-ranking mortal champion within the Grand Alliance of Chaos, taking orders directly from the Chaos Gods, and in certain respects his status exceeds even that of Daemon Princes.

His record in the Mortal Realms is equally formidable: he has captured multiple cities of Sigmar in the Celestial Realm of Azyr, established Chaos strongholds in the Realm of Fire, and commands the "final war of Chaos" in unceasing opposition against Sigmar's Stormcast Eternals. Sigmar became a God-King; Archaon became the supreme commander of Chaos. The two continue their duel — the duel that began in the Old World — under a different sky.

## Verdict

Archaon's madness was born of an extreme clarity.

He is not purely selfish like Nagash, nor driven by a single hatred across millennia like Malekith. Archaon's descent followed a unique path: he was first a fervent believer in Sigmar, and when his faith collapsed, he turned to its opposite. But the kernel of his logic never changed — he merely carried out his original mode of being to a more rigorous conclusion. He hates Sigmar. He hates all gods. Yet his entire method — uncompromising, solitary, pitting a single will against the world — is Sigmar's method, mirrored. That is the deepest tragedy of Archaon: he is the mirror-image of the thing he hates.

In his view, Chaos is the sole truth of the universe.

> What is Chaos but the rejection of order?

Within his worldview, order is a temporary illusion; Chaos is the eternal destination. Civilisation, law, religion, empire — all of them are rafts that humanity has lashed together on the surface of the Chaos-sea, destined to be swallowed by the current. He destroyed the Old World not for revenge, but for proof. Proof that Sigmar was wrong. Proof that order cannot stand. Proof that the road he chose is the only reality.

From a Templar abandoned by faith, to the mightiest mortal champion of Chaos, to the man who destroyed the Old World with his own hands, to the supreme commander of Chaos in the Mortal Realms — Archaon's trajectory is not a fall. It is the logical extension of a man who was never inconsistent with himself. He did not go mad; he simply saw what he believed to be the truth sooner than anyone else, and he acted to make it real.

The third eye of the Three-Eyed King does not gaze upon this world. It gazes upon the endless Chaos that lies beyond the end of it.
