# 04 · Settra the Imperishable

## Overview

Settra, called the Imperishable, King of Kings of Nehekhara, first among the Tomb Kings. He was the mightiest pharaoh of the ancient Nehekharan empire — in life, he conquered all of Nehekhara; in death, he commands legions of the dead with an unbroken will. In the history of the Warhammer world, Settra is remembered for the words that shook the realms: he refused to kneel to any being, including Nagash, including the four Chaos Gods. **Settra does not serve.**

## Origin

Settra was born in Khemri, the capital of ancient Nehekhara, born to be priest-king. Nehekhara was an ancient human civilisation centred on death-worship, lying in the deserts south of the Old World. The pharaoh was not merely king but the highest of all priests. From the moment Settra inherited the throne, his gaze never rested within the borders of Nehekhara — he intended to conquer every land, visible and invisible.

## Deeds

### Conquest in Life

Upon his coronation, Settra launched a war of unification at astonishing speed. He broke Numas, Ashur, Quatar, Lahmia, and every other city-state in sequence, grinding every rival king beneath his heel and ending the thousand-year fragmentation of the Nehekharan city-states. By the time of his death, Settra's domain stretched from the coast of Araby to the foothills of the World's Edge Mountains. He crowned himself "King of Kings," a title that would forever after belong exclusively to the supreme ruler of Nehekhara.

But conquering the land was not enough. Settra perceived an enemy more fundamental than any nation on earth — death itself.

He established the Nehekharan Mortuary Cult, commanding the empire's finest priests to devote themselves entirely to the study of immortality. The priests laboured day and night, but a human lifespan was too short. Settra could not wait. From his sickbed, he issued his final decree: if the priests had not achieved immortality by the moment of his death, every last one of them would be executed, to serve him in the afterlife.

The priests did not succeed before his last breath left him. Still, Settra was interred with the grandest funeral in Nehekharan history — embalmed into a mummy, sealed inside a colossal pyramid. On his sarcophagus he had inscribed a passage that still echoes among the marching legions of the dead:

> Gold is my right. Jewels are my property. The living grovel before me; the dead serve at my back. I am Settra, the Imperishable, King of Kings. Look upon my works, ye mighty, and despair.

### Awakening and Reconquest

Settra's body lay dormant in its pyramid for centuries — until Nagash unleashed the Great Ritual of Awakening that convulsed Nehekhara. Conceived as an act of ambition to convert every Nehekharan dead into Nagash's slaves, the ritual did something its architect never intended: it roused Settra and the kings of ages past from their tombs.

Settra opened eyes that had been dry for millennia. He found his empire reduced to bleaching bones in the sand. The kings who had once sworn fealty had each awakened and claimed their own thrones. Worse still, a necromancer called Nagash sought to make a slave of him.

Settra did not hesitate. He sat up, mustered his chariot legions and skeletal warriors, and launched his second war of conquest — this time in undeath. He shattered the Praetorian legions of Numas. He burned the fleet of Ashur. He crushed the chariot formations of Quatar. He swept aside the remnant vampire powers in Lahmia. Two thousand years before, he had ruled among the living. Two thousand years later, he ruled among the dead.

### Rejecting Nagash

When the End Times descended and the Chaos hosts threatened to grind the Old World to powder, Nagash sought to amalgamate every undead force to oppose Chaos — or, more accurately, to use the pretext of opposing Chaos to absorb all the Undead under his command. He demanded Settra's submission.

Settra had conquered all his life. He had never bowed his head. But facing the annihilation of the world itself, he made a decision that scorched his own soul: he handed over the baton of command and allowed Nagash to deploy his legions.

He did it. But he did not accept it.

At the critical hour, as Nagash's forces were locked in ferocious battle against the Chaos host, Settra withdrew every one of his troops from the field. Nagash's flank collapsed, and the battle with it. Settra was not seeking victory. He was telling Nagash one thing: you may have temporarily held command, but you will never hold me.

Nagash, in a rage, struck off Settra's head with his own hand.

### The Defining Scene — Settra Does Not Serve

Settra's body was broken by Nagash, and his soul hung suspended in the liminal space between the mortal world and the Realm of Chaos. And there, four presences far vaster than Nagash descended before him.

The four Chaos Gods manifested in person.

Khorne offered him endless enemies to kill and battlefields that would never run dry. Tzeentch offered prescient wisdom and the mastery of all intrigue. Nurgle promised that his flesh would grow again and never rot. Slaanesh unrolled before him an eternity of pleasure he had never imagined, let alone entered.

The four spoke as one: kneel, and all of this — immortality, dominion, power — would be his.

Settra's answer was one sentence, the single least-compromising sentence in the entire Warhammer cosmos:

**"Settra does not serve. Settra rules."**

The Chaos Gods erupted. They tore his soul to fragments, and every fragment was subjected to torments beyond naming. Through every piece, not one word of Settra's changed.

His will was shattered. It was not broken.

Thereafter, the fragments of Settra's soul coalesced anew within the Realm of Shyish, becoming a node of deathly power independent of Nagash. He is no longer the King of Kings. He has become the ultimate definition of the word unyielding.

## Fate & Present Status

In the Age of Sigmar, Settra's soul-fragments endure as an independent force within the Realm of Shyish, a power of death that answers to neither Nagash nor Chaos. He does not command an empire of the dead as he once did, but neither does he obey. His presence in the Mortal Realms is quiet, scattered, yet impossible to extinguish — a splinter in the side of every god who believes he can possess what belongs to another.

## Verdict

Settra's strength may not exceed Nagash's. His martial deeds may not surpass Archaon's. His lifespan may not match Malekith's. But in one thing, he surpasses every figure in the Warhammer world: no one is more impossible to own.

Nagash wanted him as a servant; Settra bowed and then betrayed. The four Chaos Gods descended with the full weight of divinity to buy him, and he did not waver for a single second. He is not a good man — he is a tyrant, a slaver, a conqueror who crushed a continent under an iron heel. But he refuses to be dominated by any power, even if the cost is the splintering of his soul.

He did not reject Chaos out of righteousness. He did not reject Nagash out of loyalty. He rejected everything, simply because he is Settra.

That is the Imperishable. Not the flesh. The will that cannot kneel — that, refusing to rot.
