# Warhammer Quotes Compendium

> Warhammer 40,000 and Warhammer Fantasy are two colossal worlds built on the foundation of "war." Their stories do not tell of justice triumphing over evil—they tell of every race, every faction, struggling, expanding, or rotting in their own fashion against a backdrop of boundless darkness. And these lines—from Space Marine battle-cries to the whispers of the Dark Gods, from Primarch soliloquies to Ork roars—are like rivets driven into the bones of their era. A single sentence is a window into an entire worldview.
>
> The seventy-plus classic quotes collected below are organised by theme, with original English text, Chinese interpretive translation, and brief notes on provenance and usage. Whether for essay citation, RPG table lines, or pure source-material pleasure, they are ready to serve.

---

## I. The Emperor & the Imperium of Man

The Imperium of Man is the central stage of Warhammer 40K—a galaxy-spanning theocratic authoritarian state sustained by a corpse-Emperor's psychic will. Its quotes are distillations of fanatical doctrine, declarations of sacrifice, and the grammar of dark destiny.

> *"In the grim darkness of the far future, there is only war."*
>
> — The opening line of the 40K setting, encapsulating the entire tone of the universe.

> *"The Emperor protects."*
>
> — The universal benediction of the Imperial Cult, the equivalent of "God bless you."

> *"Only in death does duty end."*
>
> — Space Marine credo, affirming lifetime service.

> *"For the Emperor!"*
>
> — The Imperial forces' battle-cry, the most ubiquitous charge-call in the galaxy.

> *"Hope is the first step on the road to disappointment."*
>
> — An Imperial Library aphorism, embodying the setting's anti-utopian essence.

> *"An open mind is like a fortress with its gates unbarred and unguarded."*
>
> — The Imperium's fear of heretical thought; dogma spoken from the heart.

> *"Blessed is the mind too small for doubt."*
>
> — One of the most bitterly ironic lines in the Imperial canon, glorifying blind obedience.

> *"There is no such thing as innocence, only degrees of guilt."*
>
> — The Inquisition's opening formula during interrogation—all are presumed guilty.

> *"Burn the heretic. Kill the mutant. Purge the unclean."*
>
> — The Inquisition / Adepta Sororitas operating protocol: the standard Imperial procedure for dealing with deviancy.

> *"Success is measured in blood; yours or your enemy's."*
>
> — Imperial military maxim; pragmatic cruelty in its purest form.

> *"Ignorance is a virtue."*
>
> — The Imperium's deliberate suppression of knowledge and technology as policy.

> *"Innocence proves nothing."*
>
> — Another signature Inquisitorial axiom: being innocent does not mean you are safe.

> *"Faith is purest when it is unquestioning."*
>
> — The Ecclesiarchy's demand of its faithful: abandon thought and be pious.

> *"The blood of martyrs is the seed of the Imperium."*
>
> — Ecclesiarchal dictum, adapted from the early Christian saying.

> *"Even a man who has nothing can still offer his life."*
>
> — Commonly used in Imperial conscription and mobilisation rhetoric, reaching the heights of tragic grandeur.

> *"Heresy is like a tree; its roots lie in darkness while its leaves wave in the sun."*
>
> — An explanation that heresy disguises itself and spreads; it must be torn out at the root.

> *"A small mind is easily filled with faith."*
>
> — The companion line to "Blessed is the mind too small for doubt."

> *"To question is to doubt. To doubt is to invite heresy."*
>
> — The Imperium's attitude toward independent thought: zero tolerance.

> *"The Emperor is not a god. He is the greatest man who ever lived."*
>
> — The position of the Emperor himself and of certain Primarchs; in the later Imperium, this became heresy.

> *"No world shall be beyond my rule; no enemy shall be beyond my wrath."*
>
> — An Imperial commander's / Inquisitor's proclamation of dominion, in all its domineering weight.

## II. Chaos & the Dark Gods

The words of the Chaos Gods (Khorne, Tzeentch, Nurgle, Slaanesh) and their servants represent the "antagonist" philosophy of the 40K universe—madness, destruction, and corruption, but with an internal logic all their own.

> *"Blood for the Blood God! Skulls for the Skull Throne!"*
>
> — The war-cry of Khorne's faithful; the most recognisable slogan of the Chaos faction.

> *"All is dust."*
>
> — The lament of the Thousand Sons / Magnus the Red, from the novel *A Thousand Sons*.

> *"I am not your enemy. I am THE enemy."*
>
> — Abaddon the Despoiler, supreme commander of Chaos, introducing himself.

> *"Let the galaxy burn!"*
>
> — The iconic line of the Horus Heresy era.

> *"Death to the False Emperor!"*
>
> — The universal battle-cry of Chaos Space Marines.

> *"Sanity is for the weak."*
>
> — A Chaos worshipper's bravado: to abandon reason is to embrace power.

> *"The strong do what they can and the weak suffer what they must."*
>
> — Thucydides' maxim, repeatedly quoted in Warhammer as the shared realist philosophy of both Chaos and the Imperium.

> *"From the depths of the warp we rise."*
>
> — The prayer / declaration of Chaos Space Marines before going to war.

> *"This silence offends Slaanesh!"*
>
> — A devotee of Slaanesh, from the Noise Marine culture: silence is blasphemy against the God of Pleasure.

> *"Change is the only constant."*
>
> — The core philosophy of Tzeentch.

> *"Papa Nurgle loves you."*
>
> — The "heartwarming" slogan of Nurgle's faithful, reframing disease and decay as paternal love.

> *"What is chaos but the rejection of order?"*
>
> — A theoretical definition of Chaos, defining itself from the angle of "anti-order."

> *"I am the architect of fate."*
>
> — The whisper of Tzeentch, claiming mastery over all change and conspiracy.

> *"In the embrace of great Nurgle, we are all equal."*
>
> — Nurgle's doctrine: disease does not discriminate; before rot, there is no high or low.

## III. Primarch Sayings

The twenty gene-sons (and the Emperor himself) are the central figures of the Warhammer 40K narrative. Their humanity, divinity, and fall constitute the skeleton of the entire saga. The following collect the lines that best distil each figure's character.

> *"Loyalty is its own reward."*
>
> — Lion El'Jonson, Primarch of the Dark Angels; the credo of a man of few words.

> *"I hear the voice of the Emperor calling me. I will answer."*
>
> — Sanguinius, his vow during the Siege of Terra, flying toward a battle he knew would be his death.

> *"The difference between gods and daemons depends largely on where one stands."*
>
> — Lorgar, Primarch of the Word Bearers, revealing the constructed nature of "godhood"—and hinting at the root of his own fall.

> *"I am what I am, nothing more."*
>
> — Leman Russ, Primarch of the Space Wolves; a self-definition so blunt it borders on arrogance.

> *"Courage is not the absence of fear. It is the conquest of it."*
>
> — Attributed to Sanguinius / the Emperor and quoted widely; Warhammer's core definition of heroism.

> *"They shall be my finest warriors, these men who give of themselves to me."*
>
> — The Emperor's original description of the Space Marines; the opening passage that became the preamble to the *Codex Astartes*.

> *"What is a man but the sum of his memories?"*
>
> — A philosophical question on identity and personhood, appearing in multiple Primarch dialogues.

> *"Death is nothing compared to vindication."*
>
> — Konrad Curze, Primarch of the Night Lords, in paranoid soliloquy; dying is cheap—being proven right is all that matters.

> *"Knowledge is power."*
>
> — Magnus the Red, Primarch of the Thousand Sons—and also Tzeentch's bait. In Warhammer, this line is a double-edged sword.

> *"Iron within, iron without."*
>
> — Perturabo, Primarch of the Iron Warriors; the ultimate declaration of a character forged to head-butting rigidity.

> *"I was there the day Horus slew the Emperor."*
>
> — The iconic opening line of *Horus Rising*, the narrative hook of the entire Heresy series.

> *"Get up."*
>
> — Khârn of the World Eaters, spoken to a defeated opponent in the fighting pits—a line that threads through his entire life and betrayal.

> *"We have come for you."*
>
> — Corvus Corax / the Raven Guard: a declaration of war so terse it is terrifying.

> *"Into the fires of battle, unto the anvil of war!"*
>
> — Vulkan, Primarch of the Salamanders; the perfect fusion of fire and smith-imagery in a battle-prayer.

> *"The flesh is weak."*
>
> — Ferrus Manus, Primarch of the Iron Hands; later adopted by the Adeptus Mechanicus—steel surpasses flesh.

## IV. Space Marines & Military

The tactical maxims, oaths, and credos of the Adeptus Astartes and Imperial military. These lines are the marrow of Warhammer martial culture.

> *"And they shall know no fear."*
>
> — One of the physiological / psychological results of Space Marine modification; a defining trait of the Astartes.

> *"Victory needs no explanation. Defeat allows none."*
>
> — Imperial military dictum: victory or defeat is the final judgement.

> *"A moment of laxity spawns a lifetime of heresy."*
>
> — Inquisition / Sororitas maxim, emphasizing the absolute necessity of vigilance and discipline.

> *"Pain is temporary. Honour is forever."*
>
> — Commonly used by Space Marines to inspire recruits, almost a veteran's mantra.

> *"It is better to die for the Emperor than to live for yourself."*
>
> — The fundamental value of the Imperial Guard / Ecclesiarchy.

> *"Through the destruction of our enemies do we earn our salvation."*
>
> — The devotional declaration of zealot Chapters such as the Black Templars.

> *"Even in death I still serve."*
>
> — The motto of the Dreadnoughts; even after death in battle, interred forever in a steel sarcophagus to continue fighting.

> *"For those we cherish, we die in glory!"*
>
> — The battle-cry of the Lamenters Chapter; one of the most tragically heroic lines in all 40K.

> *"The first rule of warfare is to know your enemy."*
>
> — The foundational tactical principle of the more "rational" Legions, such as the Ultramarines.

> *"Walk softly, and carry a big gun."*
>
> — A Space Marine tactical maxim, riffing on the Roosevelt proverb; martial power coupled with caution.

> *"The Emperor's will be done."*
>
> — The closing line of a Sister of Battle / Inquisitor's pre-battle prayer.

> *"None can withstand our faith."*
>
> — The characteristic confidence of the Black Templars / Adepta Sororitas.

> *"Only the insane have strength enough to prosper. Only those who prosper may judge what is sane."*
>
> — The pragmatically deranged philosophy of the 40K universe: when the world itself is twisted, sanity is a liability.

> *"Our presence remakes the past."*
>
> — An assertion made at high strategic levels, affirming that action retroactively defines history.

## V. Xenos & Miscellany

Humanity is not the galaxy's only voice. Greenskins, Aeldari, the T'au Empire, Necrons—each xenos species has its own worldview distilled into maxims.

> *"WAAAGH!"*
>
> — The Orks' universal expression: hello, charge, goodbye, exclamation; the single most energy-dense monosyllable in the galaxy.

> *"Red ones go fasta!"*
>
> — Ork teknology-belief: because they *believe* red vehicles are faster, red vehicles genuinely become faster.

> *"Orkses is never defeated in battle."*
>
> — Ork logic: win and it's a win; lose but escape and it's not a loss; die and you definitely don't care either way.

> *"For the Greater Good."*
>
> — The core tenet of the T'au Empire: the collective good above all—though it may also be an instrument of indoctrination.

> *"The galaxy is vast, and we are but a candle in the dark."*
>
> — A shared lament of the Aeldari and humanity: the insignificance of the individual on a galactic scale.

> *"The dead do not rest easy."*
>
> — The theme-line of the Necrons: their awakening after sixty million years is precisely to reclaim the galaxy.

> *"What is death but a transition?"*
>
> — The Aeldari view of death (the soul endures, entering the Spirit Stone or the Infinity Circuit).

> *"Our souls shall shine brighter than the stars."*
>
> — The Aeldari's tragic-romantic expression of their race's destiny.

> *"The stars once lived for our song."*
>
> — The classic Aeldari sigh of nostalgia for their ancient golden age.

> *"Fear not the psyker."*
>
> — The opening of anti-psyker propaganda—usually followed by "but fear the power behind him."

> *"We shall cleanse the stars."*
>
> — The Necron declaration of galactic purification, directed at all organic life.

## VI. Warhammer Fantasy (The Old World)

Warhammer Fantasy shares the Chaos Gods with 40K, but possesses its own unique system of races, myths, and heroes.

> *"Sigmar preserve us."*
>
> — The common prayer of the Empire, the spiritual pillar of Old World humanity.

> *"For the Lady!"*
>
> — The battle-cry of Bretonnian knights; the "Lady" refers to the Lady of the Lake.

> *"Grudges are not forgotten."*
>
> — The core of dwarf national character—every debt is recorded in the Great Book of Grudges.

> *"By Ghal Maraz!"*
>
> — Sigmar's warhammer, the supreme symbol of Imperial authority.

> *"The Horned Rat rises!"*
>
> — The ultimate rallying call of the Skaven, signalling that the Council of Thirteen's united front is now in motion.

> *"Nagash was, Nagash is, Nagash shall be."*
>
> — The tripartite declaration of the necromancer Nagash, self-proclaimed undying god.

> *"Khazukan Kazakit-ha!"*
>
> — "Look out! The dwarfs are upon you!" — the dwarf charge-cry; the loudest line in Khazalid.

> *"Summon the Elector Counts!"*
>
> — Emperor Karl Franz's famous line; the first command at the convening of the imperial war-council.

> *"This action does not have my consent."*
>
> — Another Karl Franz classic; the Emperor's quiet, unyielding authority over a general's overreach.

> *"Bring me to my men."*
>
> — Karl Franz's calm command after being unhorsed before the battle-line; a general's composure.

> *"Warpstone!"*
>
> — The instinctive shriek of a Skaven upon sighting warpstone—this substance is both power-source and narcotic.

> *"Die-die, man-things!"*
>
> — The signature Skaven speech pattern; every word is repeated.

> *"Asuryan's flame burns within us."*
>
> — A High Elf declaration of faith, in the name of the elven king of the gods.

> *"Ulthuan shall not fall."*
>
> — The ultimate oath of the High Elves in defence of their homeland.

> *"The book of grudges grows."*
>
> — A dwarf expression, half-self-mockery, half-rage; every new grudge adds weight to the national obsession.

## VII. The Skeleton of the Worldview

The following four critical commentaries distil, in condensed prose, the most central threads of the Warhammer meta-narrative: humanity's eternal war, the loyalty-and-treason enigma of the Horus Heresy, the misplaced ends-and-means of the Mechanicus, and the fundamental divergence between xenos races on the question of courage.

> **The Emperor sits upon the Golden Throne; the galaxy is strung with the Imperium's domains; war burns without end, and the darkness is infinite. Gods are unlike gods; daemons unlike daemons—all are projections of mortal hearts, incarnations of obsession.**
>
> — A summation of ten thousand years of the Imperium of Man: the Emperor sustains the Astronomican with his broken body; the Imperium spans the galaxy, yet remains perpetually on the brink of war and collapse. The so-called Chaos Gods are nothing more than the emotional and desirous projections of sapient races upon the Warp.

> **Loyalty was not truly loyalty, and treachery was not merely treachery. Brother fought brother, father and son tore at each other—how could the rise and fall of an age ever be a matter of a single thought?**
>
> — On the Horus Heresy: the Primarchs' betrayal was the compound result of personality, power, divine manipulation, and the Emperor's own contradictions—never a simple case of "good men turning bad." Loyalty and betrayal are counterposed here, not binary opposites.

> **The flesh is indeed weak; iron is indeed eternal. But steel can surpass flesh and yet never surpass the heart.**
>
> — On the paradox of the Adeptus Mechanicus and the Iron Hands: the worship of mechanisation, taken to its extreme, strips away humanity—and the loss of humanity is itself another form of Chaos.

> **The Orks know no fear, for the Orks know not what life is. The Tyranids know no fear, for the Tyranids possess no concept of "I." Only humanity, knowing death and fighting still—that is true courage.**
>
> — A comparative study of xenos combat philosophy: the Orks fight from pure joy; the Tyranid hive is pure instinct. Only human fear contains the element of choice—the choice to face what is feared, and that alone is courage.
