# 02 · Abaddon the Despoiler

## Overview

Ezekyle Abaddon was the First Captain of the Sons of Horus Legion. He inherited nothing from Horus after his gene-sire's death. Instead, he unified the scattered, self-destructing Traitor Legions, founded the Black Legion, and became the de facto supreme military commander of the forces of Chaos. Over ten thousand years, he launched thirteen Black Crusades. With the thirteenth, he shattered Cadia, tore the galaxy in two, and carved the Cicatrix Maledictum across the stars. He is the greatest living threat from Chaos in the 41st Millennium — the Imperium's most wanted enemy.

## Origin

Abaddon was born on Cthonia, a subterranean mining world near the Sol System — the very same world where Horus himself was raised. Cthonia's street-gang culture forged his early character: violence was the baseline language, loyalty was scarce currency, and the only way to survive was to keep your own judgment sharp. "Street loyalty" meant not blind obedience, but following someone because they had the strength to earn it.

He was among the earliest recruits into the Luna Wolves, later renamed the Sons of Horus. Ferocious in close combat and ruthless in battlefield instinct, he rose swiftly to command the First Company — the Justaerin Terminators, the elite within the elite. When Horus was named Warmaster, Abaddon became his most trusted lieutenant. He was not the quiet counselor whispering strategy in the war tent. He was the one who would openly contradict Horus in a high council — something almost unimaginable between a Primarch and a subordinate — and the one always at the front of every fight. In the entire Legion, only he could tell Horus "You're wrong" to his face. Horus kept him close precisely because everyone else only knew how to nod.

## Deeds

### The Horus Heresy — Following a Warrior, Not a Pantheon

When the Horus Heresy erupted, Abaddon sided with the Warmaster without hesitation. But his motive was far simpler than that of most traitors: he followed Horus the man, not Chaos the power. He was never claimed by any single Chaos God — he did not worship Khorne, offered no tribute to Tzeentch, swore no allegiance to Nurgle, and never indulged Slaanesh's sensory snares. He fought this war because he believed the Emperor had betrayed the promises made to the Primarchs and their Legions when He withdrew to Terra — and that his Warmaster was in the right.

In the final moments of the Siege of Terra, Horus was annihilated — body and soul, completely erased by the Emperor — aboard the Vengeful Spirit. When word reached the fleet, the Traitor Legions collapsed instantly. Abaddon was among the first to storm into the throne room. He did not search for Horus's body — there was no body to find. What he found was the Talon of Horus, the legendary weapon that had once gripped the Emperor's hand and torn open Sanguinius's throat. He took the lightning claw onto his own fist. That weapon became the generational symbol of the Black Legion's Warmaster, and to this day it whispers with a ghostly hum on Abaddon's left hand.

### The Founding of the Black Legion — Tear Down the Failed Banner

After Horus's death, the nine Traitor Legions fell into an infighting far more lethal than any battlefield defeat. Each Legion retreated into the Eye of Terror, warring against one another, carving up territory. And the Sons of Horus became the scapegoat and prize of every other traitor — branded as failures, hunted and plundered by former allies, nearly wiped out.

Every observer expected Abaddon to step forward, "rebuild the Sons of Horus," raise the Warmaster's old banner, and claim the mantle of Chaos's new leader.

He did not.

The first thing Abaddon did was take a remnant force deep into the Eye of Terror and seize the Vengeful Spirit itself. Standing aboard that vessel, he personally tore down the Sons of Horus insignia and battle standards, then replaced the Legion's iconic sea-green ceramite with a matte, lightless black. He did not rebuild the Sons of Horus — he ended them.

From nothing, he created the Black Legion. One rule: bloodline does not matter, only allegiance. Word Bearer or World Eater, Emperor's Children remnant or any other — strip off your old colors, put on black, pledge loyalty to the Black Legion and to Warmaster Abaddon. You are in. This Legion, assembled from traitors of every origin, became the largest and most terrifying unified force in the Chaos camp.

Meanwhile, the Chaos Gods repeatedly extended Abaddon the offer: ascend to Daemon Prince, accept the singular patronage of one god, gain near-immortal power today. Every time, Abaddon refused. Not out of pride — out of clarity. Horus's ultimate failure came precisely because the four gods' combined patronage turned him into a puppet paralyzed by their competing agendas. The moment you accept a single god's exclusive blessing, you are no longer your own master. He wields the power of Chaos, but never lets Chaos own him.

### Thirteen Black Crusades — They Mocked His Failures While He Quietly Stripped Cadia Bare

From the late M31 onward, Abaddon launched a series of Black Crusades from the Eye of Terror against the Imperium. Across ten millennia, the Imperium's official records count thirteen.

Imperial propaganda loved to mock the first twelve Crusades: each time Abaddon stormed out of the Eye with thunderous fury, and each time he slunk back in retreat. Among the citizenry and the legions, they called him "Failbaddon the Armless," mocking how Cadia's gate always turned him back — and how his arm was even lost into the Warp during one Crusade (it still drifts somewhere in that dimension to this day).

But viewed as a whole, the first twelve Crusades follow an entirely different logic. Every single one had a specific strategic objective: seizing the daemon-sword Drach'nyen, destroying a key fortress, annihilating a particular Adeptus Astartes Chapter, clearing obstructive satellite bastions from the outer defensive rings. Abaddon spent ten thousand years, like dismantling a precision timepiece, stripping away Cadia's peripheral defenses layer by layer, planet by planet. By the time the Thirteenth Black Crusade's fleet appeared in Cadia's orbit, the Imperium discovered that its ten-thousand-year bulwark had been hollowed out beyond recognition.

### The Thirteenth Black Crusade — Reshaping the Galaxy

M41.999. The Thirteenth Black Crusade.

This time Abaddon committed everything — the entire Black Legion, daemon-fleets, the servants of every Chaos God, and every warband willing to fight under his banner. Objective: Cadia. This planet, militarized for ten thousand years, was the lock on the only stable passage out of the Eye of Terror. It had no natural geography left — its continental plates had been hollowed into a continent-spanning fortress, mountain ranges rebuilt as bastion walls.

The carnage approached Horus Heresy levels. The Imperial Guard, multiple Space Marine Chapters, even Aeldari reinforcements were thrown into the fight. In the final defensive battle on Cadia's surface, Abaddon himself walked onto the field. Cadia's ultimate destruction came when a Blackstone Fortress was driven into the planet's surface as an impact weapon — the energy release and Warp resonance triggered a chain reaction.

The Eye of Terror was no longer an "eye." It convulsed and expanded, tearing open like a celestial wound that would never heal, stretching from one side of the galaxy to the other — the Cicatrix Maledictum, called by the Imperium the "Great Rift." The galaxy was cut in half: one side still saw the light of the Astronomican, the Imperium Sanctus; the other, cut off from Terra, became a lawless hunting ground for Chaos and xenos — the Imperium Nihilus. The Imperium's population, military, and resources were bisected in an instant.

This was the greatest Chaos victory over the Imperium since the Horus Heresy.

> Let the galaxy burn!

When Horus first spoke those words, it was a threat. Abaddon spent ten thousand years turning it into strategic reality. He achieved what Horus could not — not besieging Terra, but severing the Imperium itself.

## Fate & Present Status

Abaddon stands at the helm of the Black Legion, stronger than at any point in the past ten millennia. The Great Rift has become his hunting ground. The Imperium Nihilus bleeds, the Imperium Sanctus reels, and Abaddon's campaign continues — not in frenzied bursts, but with the patience of a warlord who has already waited ten thousand years and is fully prepared to wait ten thousand more. He remains mortal. He remains uncorrupted by any single god. And he remains the single most dangerous warlord in the galaxy.

## Verdict

To view Abaddon as "Horus's replacement" or "Little Horus" is the most fundamental misreading of the character.

Horus was a Primarch — a demigod gene-forged by the Emperor, radiating the aura that commands armies, hailed by all as the invincible commander at the forefront of the Great Crusade. Yet precisely because he was a Primarch, he never truly escaped Chaos's grip — his fall was engineered, his "rage" was implanted, his "free will" never existed outside the conspiracies of the Word Bearers and the manipulations of the four gods.

Abaddon is different. He is a mortal-born gene-enhanced warrior — superhuman by mortal standards, but nothing before a Primarch. He was born with no aura of command, no gene-gifted psychic talent. For ten thousand years, he has relied on nothing but himself: a strategic mind so lucid it is almost cold, the psychological endurance to pursue a single goal across a hundred centuries, and an iron discipline that never crosses the red line — no exclusive god's patronage, no daemonic ascension, never surrendering his soul to anything.

Horus exited the stage through the catastrophic failure of one decisive battle. Abaddon rewrote the galaxy's power map through thirteen Crusades and a ten-thousand-year war of attrition. Horus's rebellion was rooted in the personal, emotional entanglement between father and son. Abaddon's goal is a cold and systematic endgame: the total eradication of the Imperium from every conceivable foundation.

He is not Horus's successor. He is more dangerous than Horus ever was. Because he is awake.
