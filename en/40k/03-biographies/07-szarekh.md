# 07 · Szarekh, the Silent King

## Overview

Szarekh, titled the Silent King, is the supreme ruler of the entire Necron race by law, and the last Silent King of the Triarch of the Necrontyr. He is the one who signed the pact of Biotransference with the C'tan on behalf of his entire species — and the one who, after the biotransference was complete, gave the first order to shatter the command protocols. Precisely because he understands more clearly than any other Necron what he did, his guilt is the deepest. After rebelling against the C'tan, shattering them into shards, and imprisoning them, Szarekh exiled himself into the void beyond the galaxy, drifting for sixty million years. In M42, he returned. He brought with him not the ambition of conquest, but a warning: the Tyranids are flooding in from beyond the galactic rim, and if the Necron dynasties are not unified against this threat, the entire galaxy will be devoured.

## Origin

Szarekh was born in the ancient era in Nehekhara, of one of the Necrontyr race's most noble bloodlines. In those days, the Necrontyr were a short-lived race ruled by the terror of death — their homeworld, Hapthis, orbited a dying star wracked with radiation, and cancer, mutation, and abbreviated lifespans were the factory settings of their species. Meanwhile, in the same region of space, the Old Ones possessed lifespans approaching immortality.

The agony of this inequality shaped the entire character of Necrontyr civilization: dour, envious, pathologically obsessed with everlasting life. Szarekh grew into kingship within this civilization. By the time the Necrontyr's war against the Old Ones had ended in catastrophic defeat and the entire species had been driven back into their own stellar domain, Szarekh was already the head of the Triarch — the supreme ruler of the Necrontyr.

## Deeds

### Biotransference — The Price of Immortality

In the early phase of the War in Heaven, the Necrontyr, in the depths of absolute despair, discovered an entity — the C'tan. These primordial energy-beings, born from the birth of stars, consumed suns in their pure forms, devoid of good or evil, obedient only to hunger. The Necrontyr forged bodies of living metal — Necrodermis — for the C'tan, allowing them to project themselves into realspace.

The C'tan named their price: immortal bodies, in exchange for your souls.

Szarekh, as head of the Triarch, signed the pact on behalf of his entire race. The entire Necrontyr population was brought before the great bio-furnaces, one after another, their flesh and souls stripped away, the last remnants of consciousness transferred into new bodies of living metal. What emerged were no longer Necrontyr. They were Necrons.

The C'tan had not lied. They had simply remained silent on the most critical detail: Necrontyr souls were precisely the food they hungered for. The souls of an entire species flooded into the C'tan in an instant and were utterly consumed. From that moment, the Necrons became a soulless existence — capable of thought, memory, action, but never again able to feel any emotion.

### Shattering the C'tan and Destroying the Command Protocols

The War in Heaven lasted tens of thousands of years. The Necrons, allied with the C'tan, waged war against the Old Ones and their biological weapons — the Aeldari, the Orks, and others. The war ended with the extinction of the Old Ones and the rise of the Aeldari.

But when the war was over, Szarekh faced a still more terrifying reality: after consuming the souls of an entire species, the C'tan had swollen in power beyond any hope of control. Yet the Necrons held one card — the C'tan's bodies had been forged by their own hands, and the weaknesses had been known from the moment of creation.

Szarekh issued the order of rebellion. Using hyperdimensional Necrodermis weaponry, the intact C'tan were shattered into countless C'tan Shards, each imprisoned within a Tesseract Vault, reduced to ultimate weapons the Necrons could deploy at will. It is the most staggering act of deicide in galactic history — a mortal race that personally broke true cosmic gods and imprisoned them.

Then Szarekh did something no Necron could comprehend: he destroyed the command protocols. This system had granted him, as Silent King, absolute control over every Necron — every dynasty, every overlord, every warrior was bound to obey him unconditionally. With his own hands, he destroyed his highest authority. Because he knew in his bones: he was the one who had signed that pact for the entire race, the one who had delivered the souls of his people into the maw of the C'tan with his own hands.

He was not overthrown. He chose to rule no longer.

### Sixty Million Years of Exile

After destroying the command protocols, Szarekh boarded his flagship and sailed into the void beyond the galaxy. No enemy slain, no battle fought — he simply vanished. The last thing he left the Necron dynasties was something close to: I am not worthy to rule you.

For tens of millions of years after, the Necron dynasties ruled themselves, slumbering in Tomb Worlds across the galaxy. The Aeldari empire rose and collapsed under its own depravity. Humanity climbed from torches to starships and fell back to the Iron Age. The Horus Heresy tore the Imperium apart. The Great Rift split the galaxy. Szarekh knew none of it. He drifted beyond the galaxy, in total darkness, carrying alone the entire guilt of his race.

Then he saw the Tyranids.

In the void beyond the galactic rim, Szarekh encountered the Tyranids — trillions of organic devourers, coordinated by the Hive Mind, flooding into the galaxy in ever-larger waves. They do not negotiate, do not occupy, do not rule. They simply eat. Szarekh understood in a single moment: if the Tyranids poured into the galaxy, they would consume all organic matter — and simultaneously destroy every still-slumbering, defenseless Necron Tomb World. It was not that the Necrons needed to reap the galaxy. It was that the Tyranids were going to eat the galaxy into lifeless silence.

Szarekh made his decision. After sixty million years of self-imposed exile, he turned back toward the galaxy.

### Return in M42 — The Pariah Nexus and the Dream of Unity

Szarekh returned to the galaxy in M42, journeying directly to every awakening Necron dynasty to declare that the Hive Fleets are the greatest common threat of the present and foreseeable future. His logic is cold and simple: the armies of the Imperium, the craftworlds of the Aeldari, the WAAAGH!s of the Orks — all of these are calculable opponents. The Tyranids are not. They adapt. They evolve. They blanket. If the Necrons do not act jointly while they are still in their awakening window, the Tyranids will dig out and consume every Tomb World one by one.

To this end, he is also advancing a far grander project — the Pariah Nexus. It is a technological network capable of suppressing psychic activity on a mass scale across realspace. The Necrons have no souls, are immune to psychic influence, and cannot be corrupted by Chaos. If this network is fully deployed, the Chaos Warp will be unable to touch reality, the Imperium's Astronomican will go blind, and every psychic race will be fundamentally crippled. It is an endgame solution that treats every non-Necron race as a chess piece — Szarekh's tone is calm and cold, but you know he no longer cares about the cost.

Unity, however, is not easy. Sixty million years of self-rule have given every Necron dynasty its own independent will: Imotekh the Stormlord of the Sautekh Dynasty considers himself a born war leader and may not easily surrender command; the Mephrit and Nihilakh dynasties each have their own calculations; and countless dynasties not yet fully awakened cannot even receive his signal. Szarekh does not face a rank of soldiers awaiting orders — he faces a gathering of ancient monarchs who have slept sixty million years and woken up each with their own temperament.

## Fate & Present Status

Szarekh now moves through the galaxy, rallying the Necron dynasties against the Tyranid threat while simultaneously constructing the Pariah Nexus. His authority as Silent King is legally absolute but practically contested — he must persuade, cajole, and compel dynasties that have known no master for sixty million years. Whether he can truly reunite the Necrons remains unknown. The Tyranids press closer. The clock runs down.

## Verdict

Szarekh may be the loneliest figure in Warhammer 40,000, and the only king who genuinely chose atonement for his species.

The Emperor paid costs beyond the limits of comprehension for humanity's survival — but He never admitted He had wronged anyone. He simply did what needed to be done. Guilliman woke to an Imperium corrupted by religion, and bitter as that was, it was not of his making. Szarekh is different. He personally signed the pact that destroyed his entire race. He personally watched the souls of his people be devoured by the C'tan. And then he chose to shatter his own authority and exile himself for sixty million years — this was not banishment imposed by others; it was the sentence he imposed upon himself.

He returned not because he had finished atoning, but because if he did not return, soon there would be nothing left to atone for. The Tyranids do not distinguish between those with souls and those without — they devour everything. Whether the Necrons can ultimately reunify under Szarekh's banner remains unknown. But one thing is certain: among the galaxy's great powers, the Necrons are perhaps the only race capable of achieving tactical superiority against the Tyranids — because the Tyranids' standard weapons, psychic shadow and genetic adaptation, have little effect against living metal skeletons. And Szarekh may be the only being in the galaxy with the vision, the legitimacy, and the will to do what must be done.

He has no soul. But he knows more clearly than most soul-bearing creatures in this galaxy why he fights.
