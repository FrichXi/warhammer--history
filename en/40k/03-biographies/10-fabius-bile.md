# 10 · Fabius Bile — The Spider

## Overview

Fabius Bile, called the Spider, former Chief Apothecary of the Emperor's Children Legion, is the purest mad scientist in the Chaos ranks. He believes in neither the Emperor nor the Chaos Gods — only a scalpel and a vial of genetic sample. For ten thousand years, he has devoted himself to creating the "New Men," a species intended to replace both baseline humanity and the Space Marines — not for power, not for vengeance, but to prove a single proposition: evolution can be designed.

He is also the loneliest figure among the forces of Chaos. Slaanesh finds him amusing, Tzeentch finds him promising, but neither truly owns him. Every warband in the Eye of Terror wants him dead — because he is too insane, too uncontrollable, too indifferent to anyone's rules.

## Origin

Bile came up through the Emperor's Children Legion and was already serving as its Chief Apothecary during the Great Crusade. The Emperor's Children were defined by their pursuit of perfection; the Primarch Fulgrim's obsession with artistry and aesthetics was woven into the Legion's marrow. Bile inherited another face of that tradition — the pursuit of "perfection" in life forms. It was simply that his understanding of perfection and Fulgrim's understanding of perfection had never, from the very beginning, been the same thing.

Fulgrim sought the pinnacle of martial artistry; Bile sought the pinnacle of genetic modification. Long before the Horus Heresy erupted, Bile was already conducting fringe experiments on the gene-seed of the Emperor's Children. The Legion's Apothecaries regarded him with a mixture of awe and fear — awe at his genius, fear of his methods.

## Deeds

The Horus Heresy was the day Bile's true laboratory opened. War tore apart every rule of the Imperium, and no one was left to tell him what he could and could not do. He began genetic modification experiments on a massive scale — prisoners from traitor Legions, xenobiological specimens from the Warp, volunteers (and non-volunteers) from his own Legion — everything became material.

He created countless mutant warriors and gene-wrought monstrosities. Some had muscle density enhanced to near-invulnerability; some had poison glands grafted beneath the skin; some were simply living weapons stripped of pain, fear, and everything but obedience. A significant portion of the Emperor's Children's later battle formations consisted of "Bile's creations" — warriors doubly twisted by Slaanesh's aesthetic and Bile's gene-surgery, equal parts artwork and armament.

But it is Bile's attitude toward the Chaos Gods that sets him apart from every other Chaos Space Marine. He does not worship them. Khorne? Too crude, only knows how to chop. Nurgle? Too lazy, only knows how to rot. Tzeentch? Too convoluted, only knows how to deceive. Slaanesh? Fulgrim already knelt. Bile is just borrowing the lab. His stance toward the Chaos Gods is one of extreme pragmatism — when he needs Chaos power, he uses it; when he needs Chaos knowledge, he studies it; but he will never kneel, never surrender his soul. He does not believe in gods. Any gods.

This is exceedingly rare in the Eye of Terror. For ten thousand years, Bile has been an aberration within the Chaos camp — others wage war for the glory of their gods, Bile wages war to acquire more experimental subjects. Slaanesh's Daemon Princes find him objectionable; the lords of the Black Legion find him inconvenient; even his old brethren of the Emperor's Children have largely severed contact. Ezekyle Abaddon, when launching his Black Crusades, will occasionally trade with him — but never trust him.

The core project of Bile's life is the New Men. He believes that both baseline humanity and the Space Marines have reached an evolutionary dead end — human flesh is too fragile, and the Space Marine gene-template has fundamental flaws. He intends to use genetic engineering to create an entirely new, more perfect intelligent species to replace them. These New Men, their gene sequences personally designed by his hand, are cultivated in batches within his secret laboratories. They obey Bile as they would their creator, dispersed across hidden outposts and worlds throughout the galaxy, slowly but steadily multiplying. Bile regards them as his "children" — though, naturally, defective individuals are dissected by his own hand.

He has also done something that sends a chill through the entire galaxy: mass-cloning himself. Bile is acutely aware that he has made enemies beyond counting and will eventually die, so he has prepared a stockpile of clone bodies in advance — each carrying a complete backup of his memories and consciousness. When one Bile is killed, another awakens in some hidden laboratory and resumes the work. He has even cloned a Primarch — including a "perfect" clone of Fulgrim, one that possessed all the Primarch's memories and personality but was not corrupted by Chaos as the original had been. Bile regarded it as his finest creation, only to destroy it with his own hands — because he realised that a perfect, uncorrupted Fulgrim would rob him of control over the situation.

His signature wargear is the externalisation of his madness: the Torment, a gene-alchemical tool implanted along his spine that serves as both weapon and surgical instrument; and the Chirurgeon, a semi-living cloak that stores countless genetic samples within it, its tentacle-like appendages capable of executing surgical commands autonomously.

## Fate & Present Status

Bile continues his work across the galaxy through his clone network, ever elusive, ever hunted, ever hunting. His New Men multiply in secret. His laboratories number in the dozens or hundreds — no one knows the true count. He remains a wild card that no faction can control and none can afford to ignore.

## Verdict

Fabius Bile is one of the most unsettling figures in the 40K universe — not because he is the most powerful, but because he is the most unclassifiable.

He is a scientist without morality, without faith, without limits. The Imperium regards him as a heretic among heretics; Chaos regards him as a traitor among traitors. Yet within Bile's own logic, all of this is perfectly reasonable — morality is an obstacle to experimentation, faith is an excuse to avoid the truth, and Chaos and the Emperor are merely two mutually self-aggrandising superstition systems. The only thing he bows to is the gene, evolution itself.

His deepest tragedy lies in this: he may be the most brilliant geneticist the Imperium has ever produced — so brilliant that even the Emperor's Primaris Space Marine programme, in some sense, resembles a race against him. But he chose a path that no faction can accommodate. He is forever creating new life, yet forever living in solitude — facing his own "children," facing row upon row of his own clones, facing a galaxy that will never accept him.

What he pursues is not power. What he pursues is — writing a single perfect line of genetic code, and then proving it to the universe.
