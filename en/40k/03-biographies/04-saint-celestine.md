# 04 · Saint Celestine

## Overview

Saint Celestine, the Living Saint of the Imperium of Man, rose from the ranks of the Adepta Sororitas to become a living embodiment of the Emperor's will. She is no Primarch, no Space Marine — in the strictest sense, she is no longer an ordinary mortal. She is the most direct evidence of the Emperor's divinity: an angelic warrior who can return from death. In the dark centuries of the 41st and 42nd Millennia, as hell tore open and the Imperium Nihilus was swallowed by Chaos, she returned again and again from the Warp, wings blazing with sacred fire, to tell the Imperium: the Emperor is still watching.

## Origin

In life, Celestine was an ordinary Battle-Sister squad leader. Her birth name, her homeworld, the Order she belonged to — all of these records were long ago buried beneath the Ecclesiarchy's hagiographies, leaving behind so many contradictory versions that the truth is irretrievable. But it does not matter. The Sisters of Battle are the daughters of the Imperium: they carry no Primarch's superhuman gene-seed, wear no ceramite black carapace of the Astartes. They stand between mankind and hell armed with nothing but faith and a bolter.

Celestine was one such woman. In a campaign whose records have been lost, her force faced overwhelming enemies and was annihilated to the last woman. She died beside her sisters, broken sword in hand, facing the enemy.

For an ordinary person, that would be the end of the story. For Celestine, the story was only beginning.

## Deeds

### The First Resurrection

When Celestine fell, her soul did not dissolve into the chaotic tides of the Warp. Quite the opposite — an irresistible force seized her soul and pulled her into a realm between reality and the Immaterium. Later Imperial theologians would name this place the Mountains of Judgment, or the Emperor's Path of Trial.

In that psychic purgatory, Celestine did not face the enemy. She faced herself. She had to walk, over and over, through a mountain path lined with ordeals: reliving every choice she had made in life, confronting every person she had failed to save, accepting every vow she had been unable to fulfill. This path was not the Emperor punishing her — it was the Emperor asking her: Will you continue? Are you still willing to return?

Every time, she chose to continue.

When Celestine completed the final trial and climbed to the summit, the Emperor's will touched her. A golden psychic flame remade her — not a resurrection, but a rebirth. She regained a physical body and reappeared in realspace, but she was no longer the ordinary squad leader she had been. Wings of psychic light unfurled behind her, sacred fire burned around her without consuming, and the sword in her hand was no longer steel forged by mortals — it was an extension of the Emperor's will.

She became the first Living Saint formally recognized by the Imperium.

### The Path of a Saint

After becoming a Living Saint, Celestine fought in a series of campaigns that shaped the fate of the Imperium. Her presence alone was a strategic variable — not because of how many enemies she could kill, but because where she appeared, Imperial soldiers stopped retreating.

She soared across battlefields, wings of light spread wide, sacred fire consuming the filth of Chaos. Daemons that caught the scent of her light shrieked and fled, because her very existence — that pure extension of the Emperor's will — corresponded in the Warp to the thing the Chaos Gods loathe most: the refusal to fall.

Celestine's weapons have no names. The Imperial Cult has sought to forge legendary relics for her, but Celestine never asks for these things. Her sword is her sword. Her fire is her faith. Her power does not rest upon armament — she herself is the weapon.

### Death and Rebirth

Celestine's most unique ability is not her swordsmanship nor her psychic power, but her immortality.

Every time she falls, her soul is cast back to the foot of the Mountains of Judgment. She becomes again that bare-handed mortal woman, facing all the regret, fear, and exhaustion accumulated across an entire lifetime. The Emperor never grants her a shortcut — every trial is as brutal as the first, or worse, because this time she knows more clearly what she stands to lose. Every time, she must choose anew: Am I still willing to serve the Emperor? Can I still endure all of this?

Every time, she rises again. Every time, she passes through the fire at the summit and spreads her wings once more.

This mechanism of resurrection is not merely physiological — it is an eternal psychic symbol. In the Era Indomitus of the 42nd Millennium, the Emperor's power seems stronger than at any point in the past ten thousand years, and the frequency and number of Living Saints have increased. Celestine is the first and most powerful, but reports suggest she is no longer alone — the Emperor is returning to the material universe in this unprecedented fashion.

### The Banner of the Imperium

Celestine's most significant appearance came in one of the Imperium's darkest moments. When the Cicatrix Maledictum tore the galaxy apart, severing Imperium Nihilus from Imperium Sanctus, plunging half the Imperium into darkness cut off from Terra — she was there.

In regions where the Astronomican's light could not reach, in worlds being consumed by Chaos, alien xenos, and despair, Celestine became the spiritual anchor of the Imperium's remnant forces. She is not a general. She does not command grand armies. She is simply an angel — but an angel is itself the most powerful declaration: the Emperor has not forgotten this half of the Imperium, and His will still burns in the dark.

Countless scattered Chapters, survivors of fallen worlds, shattered regiments of the Imperial Guard — on the edge of the abyss, ready to give up — saw a golden light ignite on the horizon and heard the sound of wings breaking the air. They knew what it meant: it is not over yet.

## Fate & Present Status

Saint Celestine continues to appear across the warzones of the 42nd Millennium, most frequently in the darkness of Imperium Nihilus, where the Astronomican's light fails to reach. She cannot rest, for her resurrection is not a reward but a commission — the Emperor keeps sending her back. With every death, she walks the Mountains of Judgment again. With every rebirth, she descends into some new hell with wings aflame. The cycle has no end in sight.

## Verdict

In the universe of Warhammer 40,000, hope is a commodity so scarce it borders on the absurd. The Imperium of Man is built on the corpses of trillions, the Emperor has sat silent on the Golden Throne for ten thousand years, the Primarchs are dead, lost, or turned daemon, and loyalty and sacrifice in this brutal galaxy are often rewarded with nothing. Open any 40K sourcebook and you will find endless war, endless darkness, endless despair.

And Saint Celestine's existence is the only thing in all of it that is purely, unambiguously white.

She is not like Roboute Guilliman, who was physically resurrected through gene-craft, the Emperor's Sword, and the death-magic of the Aeldari — a rationalist struggling to adapt to a religified Imperium. Nor is she like the Primarchs — the Emperor's creations, engineered from birth to be superhuman. Celestine is an after-the-fact addition: she was once weak, once mortal, once just like any of us reading these words.

The Emperor chose her.

This is not a metaphor. In 40K's cosmology, whether the Emperor is actually a god is the single most central unresolved question in the entire setting. The Ecclesiarchy spent ten thousand years deifying the Emperor — yet the Emperor Himself ruled in life under the Imperial Truth, which explicitly stated that there are no gods. The emergence of the Living Saints forces the most acute deviation from that paradigm: if the Emperor is not a god, then what is a Living Saint? Celestine's power is not a psyker mutation, not alien technology, not Chaos corruption — she was born directly from the Emperor's will. Her existence is itself theological evidence.

But this also raises a darker question: the Emperor chose a mortal to endure an endless cycle of death and trial. Celestine is not basking in bliss — she is forever fighting, forever sacrificing, forever walking that desolate road through the Mountains of Judgment alone. The Emperor will not let her rest, because the Emperor still needs her.

She is hope. But hope, in this universe, is a weapon.

Every time Celestine spreads her golden wings and flies toward a battlefield on the brink of collapse, her presence tells the Imperium and every enemy of the Imperium one thing: no matter how dark the galaxy grows, the Emperor's will has not gone out. And a fire that refuses to die, even a single flame, can still set a world ablaze.
