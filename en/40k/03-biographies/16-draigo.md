# 16 · Kaldor Draigo — Supreme Grand Master of the Grey Knights

## Overview

Kaldor Draigo, Supreme Grand Master of the Grey Knights, supreme commander of the Emperor's most elite daemon-hunting Chapter. But he does not issue orders from the monastery on Titan. He is not even, strictly speaking, "in" realspace. He is trapped in the Warp — trapped in that dimension ruled by Chaos, trapped in the homeland of daemons, trapped in the deepest layer of fear shared by every civilisation. The length of time he has been trapped in the enemy's house can no longer be precisely calculated. And his response has been: slaughter daemons inside it, burn Nurgle's garden, carve the names of fallen Grey Knights onto the heart of a Lord of Change. He is not a victim. He is a hunter who walked through the wrong door and has since decided not to leave.

## Origin

Draigo's early life is buried deep under Grey Knights classification protocols — this is Grey Knights tradition; personal information is more tightly controlled than sacred relics. But several things are certain: he is a powerful psyker, and on Titan's sixth moon he underwent the most brutal selection and training the Imperium of Man has to offer, passing the Six Hundred and Sixty-Six Trials. This is probably not a figure of speech. The Grey Knights take "trials" with absolute seriousness; every stage can kill you, and dead candidates are not recorded as failures — merely as unqualified.

His rise within the Grey Knights was unusually swift. He slew a Bloodthirster of Khorne, sealed multiple Warp rifts, and hunted a Daemon Prince through the shadow of Mordrak. When he was elevated to Supreme Grand Master, his peers raised precisely one objection: too young. He was not yet thirty when he ascended to the highest seat among all daemon hunters. The objection was quickly withdrawn — not because of his eloquence, but because of the speed of his blade.

His signature weapon is the Titansword, a psychically-charged blade forged by the Grey Knights' most ancient techniques, said to carry within it some measure of the Emperor's own blessing. He also bears a Storm Shield inscribed with the names of fallen Grey Knights. He remembers every name. Every one of them, he intends to avenge personally.

## Deeds

### Becoming Supreme Grand Master

Before his elevation to Supreme Grand Master, Draigo's most famous deed was confronting the Daemon Prince M'kar the Reborn during the War of Mordrak. In that battle, he was nearly torn apart — the daemon's claws punched through his Terminator Armour, and the outer shell across half his chest was almost entirely slagged. At the brink of death, he drove the Titansword into M'kar's heart-core. The Daemon Prince did not die — daemons rarely die — but it was banished. For the Grey Knights, banishing a Daemon Prince is not a record; it is a workday.

That action made his name, but more importantly: the sheer refusal to be defeated that he displayed in that battle became the only currency he would later rely upon to survive inside the Warp.

### The Fall — The Curse of Kornovin

Draigo's entrapment occurred in the Kornovin system. M'kar returned, this time anchoring a Warp rift in realspace and threatening to drag the entire star system into Chaos. Draigo led an elite Grey Knights strike force into the rift and fought M'kar on the borderlands of the Warp. This time he won. He plunged the Titansword into M'kar's psychic core and shattered it utterly.

The problem lay in the retreat. When the rift collapsed, Draigo stood on the wrong side. He was not pushed in — he held the rear guard voluntarily. His Terminator Armour shrieked with structural stress under the rift's implosion; the boundary between reality and the Warp healed shut behind him like a wound scarring over. The Grey Knights fleet activated every augur array, every Navigator, every psychic communications relay, and searched for him for thirty-seven standard Terran days. No signal. No trace. Nothing.

The Grey Knights held a funeral rite for their Supreme Grand Master. This was somewhere around mid-M41.

What they did not know was that Draigo was not dead.

### The Hunter in the Warp

No one knows what it takes to survive inside the Warp. It is not an environment you can "adapt" to. The Warp has no physical laws, no time, no causality. Daemons are not the intruders there — you are. Every whisper there is trying to pierce your skull; every colour is attempting to twist your reflexes; every direction leads toward the domains of the Chaos Gods. An ordinary human exposed to the Warp for seconds will go mad, mutate, or have their soul consumed outright. A Space Marine can last minutes to hours. A Grey Knight can last longer.

Draigo has lasted — no one knows how long. Because time in the Warp is not linear. He may have been trapped there for millennia, or for days. His perception of time has lost all referential value.

He did something no daemon anticipated: he treated their home as a hunting ground.

Draigo hunts daemons alone in the Warp. No supplies, no reinforcements, no retreat corridor. The psychic ward of his Terminator Armour is his sole dividing line — inside the armour is himself; outside the armour is infinite Chaos. He sustains the armour's systems on the psychic infusion of the Emperor's will. He sustains his own sanity on pure hatred. He sleeps on piles of daemon corpses, drinks something indescribable wrung from the domains of Chaos, and fights off the whispers that invade his dreams every time his eyes close.

The most famous account to have filtered back to realspace — no one dares say it is one hundred percent true, but it is referenced in a classified Grey Knights codex — is that Draigo **burned Nurgle's garden**. This is not hyperbole. The Garden of Nurgle is the Plaguefather's most cherished domain, the psychic source of all cycles of decay and rebirth. Draigo walked in, set the blighted blooms ablaze with psychic fire, carved open a vast swathe of diseased blossoms with the Titansword, and then left. He is said to have shouted something on the way out — not an apology. Nurgle's daemons pursued him through an unknown number of Warp layers. They did not catch him.

Another account, also passed down, describes him battling a Lord of Change inside Tzeentch's Crystal Labyrinth and defeating it. He did not kill it — his treatment of the greater daemon of Tzeentch was crueller: he carved the names of fallen Grey Knights onto the daemon's heart. Every time that Lord of Change attempts to reconstitute itself, those names sear its psychic form. It will forever remember that it was humiliated by a mortal — and the names of the Grey Knights' dead are thus permanently branded into the heart of an unkillable enemy, an indelible monument.

### Between Reality and Unreality

Draigo's condition carries one unstable feature: he occasionally manifests in realspace. Without any warning, at the moment the Grey Knights need him most, his form will condense out of the air — the battle scars on his Terminator Armour multiplied beyond count since the last appearance, the Titansword still sharp, but his eyes a little more tired, a little sharper. He joins the fight, helps the Grey Knights defeat the enemy, speaks a few words of direction, and then vanishes — bursting like a bubble in water, pulled back into the Warp.

The Grey Knights have no official position on this state of affairs. Draigo cannot be counted as "returned," because he cannot remain in reality for long. He cannot be counted as "fallen," because he shows up at irregular intervals. He occupies a grey zone within the Chapter's official structure that no one can explain — the Supreme Grand Master is still serving, just not on Titan. The current Grey Knights leadership operates a system of "acting" command, but every senior officer knows: as long as Draigo is hunting more daemons inside the Warp than any Grey Knights strike team, they have neither the standing nor the desire to elect a new Supreme Grand Master.

Some call it a curse — to be eternally trapped in the enemy's domain. But what more Grey Knights see is an entirely different image: their Supreme Grand Master is the only human who turned the territory of Chaos into his personal hunting ground. Daemons come through rifts to kill mortals. Draigo went to the other side of the rift and waits for them on their own doorstep.

## Fate & Present Status

Draigo remains trapped in the Warp, continuing his one-man war. He manifests in realspace at unpredictable intervals, always in the midst of battle, always when the Grey Knights' need is greatest. The Chapter maintains his title as Supreme Grand Master; no successor has been appointed. His final recorded words, spoken to a Grey Knights strike squad that gathered around him during one of his manifestations, were six words long:

*"I am still killing. Do not worry."*

When the Supreme Grand Master says "do not worry," he is probably not talking about himself.

## Verdict

Kaldor Draigo is one of the very few figures in the 41st Millennium who can silence both an Exterminatus order and a Daemon Prince with equal finality. To the ordinary mortal who reads only the Imperium's "official" propaganda, he is a tragic hero — cursed to wander forever in the Warp. But ask a Chaos sorcerer "what do you fear most," and the name Kaldor Draigo will emerge from the mouths of multiple daemons in a particular tone: not hatred — fear.

A Bloodthirster being ripped apart by psychic force does not inspire fear — that is a normal outcome of combat. But when some figure in silver-grey Terminator Armour walks into Nurgle's most sacred garden, sets fire to flowers that should not be flammable, calmly slaughters every Nurgling that pursues him, and pauses on the way out to glance back at the flames — that is the stuff of a daemon's nightmares. Because daemons in the Warp cannot truly be killed; upon death, they return to the Chaos realm to reconstitute. The problem is that Draigo *lives* in the Chaos realm. For him, the concept of "banishment" does not apply. When he kills a daemon, that daemon's soul may not have time to flee and reform before his second strike lands. And even if it does reform — the thing wakes up still inside the Warp. And Draigo is still there. This may be the most hellish infinite loop in forty thousand years: a daemon dies, goes home, and finds the killer already standing at the door.

Draigo's true strength does not lie in psychic power, or swordsmanship, or his suit of Terminator Armour. It lies in his refusal. Every second inside the Warp, voices are telling you: *give in, join us, enjoy, surrender.* The promises of the Chaos Gods are not lies — they genuinely can give you everything you want, if only you yield. Draigo has heard this for an unknown span of millennia, and his answer has never changed: he draws his sword. There is no "struggling against corruption" drama about him — he is not *resisting* temptation. He genuinely does not find these things tempting. His mental architecture baffles daemons: a will that can walk alone through the centre of an ocean of infinite malice without a single crack.

The Imperium of Man fears the Warp. This is common sense. Draigo took that common sense, shoved it into the mud, and strangled it. His very existence is a bug that Chaos logic cannot parse: a man trapped inside the enemy's domain, who has, in turn, made the entire enemy domain afraid of him.
