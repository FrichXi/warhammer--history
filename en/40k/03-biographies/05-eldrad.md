# 05 · Eldrad Ulthran

## Overview

Eldrad Ulthran, High Farseer of Craftworld Ulthwé, is one of the oldest and most psychically powerful mortals in the galaxy. He has lived over ten thousand years, spanning nearly the entirety of Aeldari history from the Fall to the present day. His foresight is so formidable as to border on the terrifying — he can trace the threads of fate across centuries and millennia, and place his pieces upon the galactic board accordingly. He is not humanity's ally. He is not even what anyone would call a "good person." But in the dark galaxy of the 41st Millennium, if you are looking for someone who genuinely understands the greater picture and is willing to intervene at critical moments — Eldrad's name will surface sooner or later.

Craftworld Ulthwé is itself an anomaly. It drifts dangerously close to the Eye of Terror, permanently exposed to the Warp's radiation, making its Farseers more powerful than those of any other craftworld — and earning it the epithet "the Damned" in the lore. Eldrad is the most exceptional of these Farseers, and the most willing among them to act.

## Origin

Eldrad was born before the Fall of the Aeldari — in the age before Slaanesh was born, when the Aeldari were immortal, their pleasures unbounded, their souls reincarnating automatically upon death. Records of his early years are scarce. But by the time Slaanesh finally awakened from the collective depravity of the Aeldari civilization and the empire collapsed in a single instant, Eldrad was already a psyker of formidable foresight.

He did not join the pleasure cults, nor did he succumb to the Fall like the vast majority of his kind. He had foreseen the coming catastrophe early and boarded Craftworld Ulthwé well before the end. From that moment, his life had a single core objective: find a path to survival for the Aeldari race.

Ulthwé's Seer Council functions as the de facto governing body of the craftworld. Farseers are those Aeldari who have walked the Path of the Seer to its conclusion — they have sacrificed sensory experience and emotional freedom to spend the remainder of their lives interpreting fate. Supported by the souls of the dead residing in the Infinity Circuit, Farseers can glimpse the infinite branches of the future. Eldrad distinguished himself among them not because his prophecies were the most accurate — but because he was willing to act after the prophecy. Most Farseers interpret fate and then wait. Eldrad steps onto the field himself.

## Deeds

### The Horus Heresy — A Warning Unheeded

Eldrad's most famous and lamentable intervention came in the 31st Millennium, on the eve of the Horus Heresy — the most catastrophic civil war in the history of the Imperium of Man.

Before Chaos had fully corrupted Horus Lupercal, Eldrad's foresight had already captured the coming horror: the Warmaster's betrayal, Terra in flames, the Imperium brought to the brink of annihilation — and the cascading consequences that would ultimately swell the power of the Chaos Gods to threaten every species, including the already endangered Aeldari. His judgment: the human Imperium had to survive this crisis, or the entire galaxy would be dragged into the Warp.

So Eldrad made an extraordinarily bold decision — he would make contact with humanity in person. He found Fulgrim, Primarch of the Emperor's Children, the III Legion. Eldrad told him that Horus would be corrupted by Chaos on Davin, warned him that he must stop the slaughter on Isstvan — and specifically warned that Fulgrim himself was destined to kill his closest brother, Ferrus Manus. But Fulgrim was already tainted by Slaanesh. He dismissed Eldrad's counsel as the insidious manipulations of a xenos.

What would have happened if Fulgrim had listened? That question has never been answered. Fulgrim killed Ferrus Manus with his own hands, severing the head of his once-dearest brother and presenting it to Horus. The Imperium lost an entire Legion and two Primarchs in the Heresy — wounds from which it has yet to recover.

Eldrad watched the catastrophe he had foreseen unfold frame by frame, unable to stop any of it. It was among the most profound lessons of his long existence: the most accurate prophecy in the galaxy is meaningless if no one is willing to listen. And most of the time, those willing to listen to an Aeldari — are almost non-existent.

### Ten Thousand Years of Foresight and Intervention

In the ten millennia since the Fall, Eldrad has never ceased to act. His objective has remained clear: preserve the last embers of the Aeldari, and find a path to escape Slaanesh's grip entirely.

The number of schemes he has orchestrated across those millennia is so vast that almost no one can track them in full. His hallmark is that he never acts directly — Eldrad operates through chains of indirect events, applying a precise push at a precise moment so that you believe the decision was yours, when every step was already laid out in his prophecies. The leaders of the Aeldari craftworlds regard him with a mixture of awe and dread, because he is willing to sacrifice an entire generation for a goal a thousand years away. The Imperium's attitude toward him is unalloyed hostility — in Imperial eyes, all xenos are to be exterminated, and a xenos prophet capable of foreseeing the Imperium's fate is the most terrifying entity imaginable.

But Eldrad does not care. He has intervened at critical junctures to influence the Imperium's fate time and again — not out of love for humanity, but because the Imperium is the largest barrier standing between the galaxy and Chaos. If the Imperium falls, the Aeldari are next.

He foresaw Abaddon's Thirteenth Black Crusade, foresaw that Cadia would fall and the Great Rift would tear the galaxy apart. In all of these events, he operated from the shadows — sometimes passing information to the right party, sometimes manipulating an unrelated battle to redirect the flow of personnel and matériel, and sometimes simply ensuring that one specific person appeared at one specific place at one specific moment.

### The Birth of the Ynnari — Planting the Seed

Of all Eldrad's grand designs, the most important concerns the prophecy of Ynnead, the Aeldari God of the Dead.

Ancient Aeldari lore speaks of a prophecy: when the souls of all dead Aeldari have gathered in sufficient strength within the Infinity Circuits, a new deity — Ynnead — will awaken from the collective soul. Ynnead's singular purpose is to slay Slaanesh and end the curse upon the Aeldari race.

This prophecy had circulated among the Aeldari for millennia, but for thousands of years it remained nothing more than a distant myth. No one knew if it would ever come true, and no one knew how to make it happen.

Eldrad was the first to treat this prophecy as an executable plan. He sought out Yvraine — at the time, an unremarkable Aeldari warrior from Craftworld Biel-Tan — and passed to her the prophecy and the mission of Ynnead. Yvraine later underwent a series of near-death experiences and rebirths, established direct contact with the Yncarne, the avatar of Ynnead, and ultimately became the prophet and leader of the Ynnari.

It is no exaggeration to say that without Eldrad, the Ynnari would not exist. He planted the seed, then stepped back and let the forces of fate turn on their own.

However, shortly after the Ynnari's founding, Eldrad chose to depart. The exact reasons have never been fully disclosed — some speculate he had reservations about the true conditions for Ynnead's awakening, others suggest he recognized that a younger leader was better suited to carry the banner. Whatever the case, having lit the flame, he returned to his most natural role: alone, operating from the shadows.

### The Price of Ten Millennia

It must be said that Eldrad's ten-thousand-year career is not a story of unbroken victory. Quite the opposite — most of his grand designs have ended in failure, at least in the short term. The Horus Heresy was not stopped. The Great Rift was not prevented. The Aeldari remain on the brink of extinction. Time and again, he has tried to rewrite fate, only to watch the worst possible outcome materialize before his eyes.

But the point is not that he failed. The point is that without him, the Aeldari would likely no longer exist.

He ensured that spirit stone after spirit stone was safely recovered. He enabled craftworld after craftworld to retreat in time before the advance of Chaos armadas. At countless microscopic inflection points, he made correct decisions that went entirely unnoticed, so that the Aeldari — though critically endangered — are still alive. For a race that has already lost ninety-nine percent of itself, "alive" is the greatest victory of all.

## Fate & Present Status

Eldrad Ulthran remains active in the 42nd Millennium. He parted ways with the Ynnari after their founding and has returned to his accustomed mode of solitary operation — drifting between the threads of fate, placing pieces on the board no one else can see, intervening where intervention matters most. His physical form, sustained by psychic power and a will that has endured for ten thousand years, persists — but no one, perhaps not even Eldrad himself, knows how much longer it can last.

## Verdict

Eldrad Ulthran is not a hero. Do not be misled by his occasional interventions on behalf of the Imperium — every single time, no matter how much it resembles "saving humanity," the ultimate beneficiary is the Aeldari. He will sacrifice an entire human fleet if it allows one Aeldari craftworld to pass safely. He will interfere covertly in a battle five hundred years in the future, because the outcome of that battle will determine a critical node another millennium downstream.

But in this galaxy, sometimes the enemy of your enemy is the most reliable force available. With the Emperor seated on the Golden Throne and the Chaos Gods watching hungrily from the Warp — against that configuration, Eldrad has chosen an implicit, mutually unacknowledged "alliance" with the Imperium. Not because he trusts the Imperium, but because without the Imperium, Chaos wins.

He is a true prophet — but not the kind who stands on a mountaintop gazing down upon the world and dispensing wisdom. He is the prophet standing on a battlefield of mud and corpses, plucking the strings of fate with bloodied fingertips. He understands better than anyone that the Aeldari are fated to die, and everything he does is simply an effort to make that end come later.

If one day the galaxy finally arrives at some kind of breakthrough — whatever that may be — somewhere in the web of cause and effect behind it all, there will be a cord whose end is held in Eldrad's hand. He has no name, seeks no glory, and in the files of the Imperium he is marked "Extremely Dangerous Xenos, Kill on Sight." But in the faith of the Aeldari, every surviving Aeldari soul owes him a silent debt of gratitude.
