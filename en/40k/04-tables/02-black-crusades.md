# 02 · The Black Crusades and Champions of Chaos

> After Horus fell, the traitors scattered into the Eye of Terror. The Emperor ascended the Golden Throne. The Imperium's wounds were still raw. Deprived of their Warmaster, the Chaos Gods began selecting mortal champions to enact slaughter, sorcery, pestilence, and excess on their behalf. And Abaddon — once Horus's foremost captain — gathered the remnants of the nine Traitor Legions, raised the banner of black, and launched thirteen campaigns out of the Warp across ten thousand years, hammering at the gates of Cadia. The survival of the Imperium has hung on this line of defense.
>
> Herein: a chronicle of the Black Crusades and a record of the Champions of Chaos.

---

## Part I · Abaddon's Thirteen Black Crusades: A Chronicle

The Eye of Terror is a vast wound torn across the northern galaxy. Within it the forces of Chaos hold dominion, and the Cadian System is the sole stable corridor into realspace. Cadia is a fortress world built by the Emperor's own decree, guarding this gate for over ten thousand years. The thirteen Black Crusades are the thirteen times Abaddon led his hosts against that gate.

| No. | Approximate Date | Key Events |
|:---:|------|------|
| **1st** | Late M31 (shortly after the Horus Heresy) | Abaddon first gathered the remnants of the nine Legions and tested the Cadian Gate. The Imperium, reeling from the Heresy, had left its borders thinly defended. Cadia nearly fell. Yet the traitor forces were fractured, each warband fighting its own war — Abaddon had not yet truly united their wills. Imperial reinforcements drove them back. Though a failure, this campaign set the pattern for the next ten thousand years: Cadia is the key, and Abaddon swore to break it. |
| **2nd** | M32–M33 | After consolidating his strength, Abaddon sallied forth again. Far smaller than the first, this campaign focused on raiding the Imperium's frontier — probing defenses and seizing resources. The Imperium dismissed it as a border skirmish, unaware of the larger design behind it. |
| **3rd–6th** | M33–M34 | Four intermittent expeditions, each claiming some prize but none shaking Cadia's foundations. In the intervals, Abaddon steadily achieved absolute control over the Black Legion — purging rivals with an iron fist, binding warriors through victory. Cynics call these four crusades "Abaddon's training ground." |
| **7th** | Mid-to-late M35 | Called the *Ghost War*. This campaign broke from all precedent: no massed frontal assault, but sorcery, infiltration, and the spreading of terror. Sorcerers of the Thousand Sons participated heavily, threading Warp-powers through Imperial defenses like wraiths. Though the gate held, this war exposed fatal flaws in the Imperium's frontier system — Chaos no longer relied on frontal charges alone. |
| **8th–11th** | M36–M39 | Four millennia of grinding assault. The Cadian Gate stood firm under the shelling, but uncounted Imperial worlds in the surrounding sectors were reduced to ash. During these centuries Abaddon brought renegade warbands like the Red Corsairs to heel, and the Black Legion's prestige swelled. |
| **12th** | End of M39 to early M41. Called the *Gothic War*. | A major strategic shift. Rather than striking Cadia directly, Abaddon moved against the Gothic Sector with a specific objective: the **Blackstone Fortresses**. These were super-weapons left by an ancient xenos race, six of them scattered across the Gothic Sector. Over decades of warfare, Abaddon seized two. The Imperial Navy suffered grievous losses; the Gothic Sector was crippled. Only after the war did the Imperium grasp Abaddon's purpose: the Blackstone Fortresses were not trophies — they were battering rams being collected for the ultimate assault. |
| **13th** | M41.999 | **The Fall of Cadia**. This campaign dwarfed all that came before. Abaddon brought to bear the culmination of ten thousand years of preparation: the full Black Legion, the remnants of the nine Traitor Legions, countless daemons and cultists. A Blackstone Fortress descended from the sky; the ground turned to a sea of blood. The Cadian defenders held out for months with inconceivable will. In the final act, Abaddon personally led elites to the surface, and Cadia was destroyed. As the planet shattered, the Eye of Terror ripped outward, expanding into the **Great Rift** (*Cicatrix Maledictum*), cleaving the galaxy in two. The Imperium Nihilus was severed from Terra. Chaos's greatest victory since the Horus Heresy was complete. |

> Of the thirteen Black Crusades, the first twelve might be called "unfinished business." Only the thirteenth finally broke through. This is not because Abaddon was foolish at first and clever later — it is because he operated on the scale of millennia: the first crusade tested the defenses, the seventh shifted tactical doctrine, the twelfth gathered siege engines, and the thirteenth smashed the gate in one blow. No other Chaos champion possesses this degree of patience. Horus was driven by fury onto the battlefield and ended it all in a hundred days. Abaddon sharpened his blade for ten thousand years and finally split the galaxy in two.

## Part II · The Champions of Chaos: A Record

Each of the four Chaos Gods has favored servants. Once the elite of the Astartes Legions, these champions now bear the gods' will in mortal flesh, having slain uncounted lives across the millennia with neither repentance nor end. Beyond them exist those who bow to no single god, wielding the powers of Chaos through their own will — Abaddon foremost among them.

### Champions of the Four

| God | Champion | Original Legion | Living/Dead | Deeds |
|:---:|------|----------|:---:|------|
| **Khorne** | Khârn the Betrayer | World Eaters | Undying for ten millennia (Khorne resurrects him whenever slain) | Former Captain of the World Eaters' 8th Assault Company, foremost berserker under Angron. Unlike most berserkers lost to mindless rage, Khârn kills with cold clarity — he slays friend and foe alike. At the Siege of Terra he first earned the title "Betrayer" by butchering both sides simultaneously. He wields Gorechild, a Khorne-blessed chain-axe. At Armageddon he took a direct hit from a Titan's main cannon and was reduced to shredded meat — Khorne promptly restored him whole. His existence has one meaning: to kill, in the purest fashion, until the galaxy is bleached bone. |
| **Tzeentch** | Ahzek Ahriman | Thousand Sons | Alive | One of the most powerful psykers the Thousand Sons ever produced, Magnus's Chief Librarian. After the Burning of Prospero, the Flesh-change mutation accelerated beyond control. Ahriman cast the "Rubric of Ahriman" — a supreme psychic working intended to halt the mutation once and for all. The result was an eternal tragedy: Thousand Sons with weaker psychic ability were turned into Rubric Marines — soul-ash sealed within silent armor — while the strong grew stronger. Magnus, enraged by the Rubric, sought to destroy Ahriman, but Tzeentch himself intervened. Ahriman is Tzeentch's most treasured plaything. Exiled from the Planet of the Sorcerers, he has spent ten thousand years scouring the galaxy for the Aeldari Black Library, seeking the ultimate knowledge to reverse the Rubric. The closer he gets to the truth, the deeper he sinks into the conspiracy. |
| **Nurgle** | Typhus (Calas Typhon) | Death Guard | Alive | First Captain of the Death Guard, Mortarion's most trusted lieutenant — and the true architect of the Legion's fall to Nurgle. During the Horus Heresy, as the Death Guard fleet crossed the Warp, Typhus — who had already secretly pledged himself to Nurgle — guided a corrupting plague through the ships, trapping the entire Legion in Nurgle's putrefying storm. When Mortarion finally knelt to save his sons, he discovered his trusted right hand had betrayed him from the start. Ascended as the Herald of Nurgle, Typhus pilots the plague-vessel *Terminus Est* across the galaxy, spreading Grandfather Nurgle's "love." Unlike Mortarion — who surrendered under duress — Typhus is a willing convert. |
| **Slaanesh** | Lucius the Eternal | Emperor's Children | Eternal (special mechanism) | Captain of the Emperor's Children's 13th Company, widely regarded as one of the finest swordsmen the galaxy has ever known. An undefeated legend in the dueling pits before the Heresy, his fall to Slaanesh drove him to seek ever more extreme sensations of pain and pleasure. Slaanesh gifted him a unique "blessing": anyone who kills Lucius, and feels even a flicker of satisfaction or pride in doing so, is possessed by Lucius's soul. The victim's body slowly reshapes into Lucius's visage, while the victim's soul is imprisoned within one of the faces upon Lucius's armor — joining the silent, screaming gallery. For this reason his armor is densely covered in agonized faces, each one a swordsman who once bested him and was devoured from within. He can die — but he can never be truly slain. |

### Champions Undivided

| Champion | Original Legion | Title | Deeds |
|------|----------|------|------|
| Ezekyle Abaddon | Sons of Horus | Warmaster of the Black Legion / the Despoiler | First Captain of Horus. After his primarch's death, he forged the remnants of the nine Traitor Legions into the Black Legion. Over ten thousand years he launched thirteen Black Crusades, finally destroying Cadia and tearing the galaxy apart with the thirteenth. Unlike Horus — Abaddon refuses to be a puppet of the Chaos Gods. He wields Chaos without submitting to any single god. Bearing the daemon-sword Drach'nyen and the Talon of Horus upon his arm, he is the supreme commander of Chaos forces in the present galaxy. |
| Fabius Bile | Emperor's Children | The Gene-Alchemist / the Spider | Former Chief Apothecary of the Emperor's Children, one of the greatest genetic engineers the galaxy has produced. He serves no god (in truth, he believes in none), surviving independently in the domain of Chaos as a pure amoral scientist. He has cloned every Primarch (including a perfect clone of Fulgrim) and created the "New Men," a genetically modified subspecies. He has lived ten thousand years through cloned bodies and consciousness transfer. Bile represents the strangest path within Chaos — he does not worship gods, only genes. |

> Surveying the champions of Chaos, the road to damnation differs for each. Khârn died by violence and earned immortality through violence; Ahriman tried to save his brothers and destroyed them instead; Typhus willingly became a servant of pestilence; Lucius is trapped forever in the mirror of his own narcissism. Only Abaddon refuses to be any god's puppet, wielding divine power through human will, his ambition unshaken for ten thousand years. Even from the opposing side, one must concede: this is a figure of consequence.
>
> The Chaos Gods drink mortal souls like strong liquor, and the Champions of Chaos are the strongest shot in the bottle.
