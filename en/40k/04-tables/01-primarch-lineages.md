# 01 · The Twenty Primarch Lineages

> The Emperor had twenty sons, all created through genetic engineering. Scattered across the galaxy by Chaos while still infants, they were recovered one by one by the Emperor. Each son commanded a Legion, designated as an Adeptus Astartes chapter. By the time the Horus Heresy erupted, nine sons had turned traitor, nine remained loyal, and the records of two had been expunged. Their lineages are laid out below.

## Assessment

The twenty sons were like pottery fired by the Emperor's own hands — each with its own shape, each with its own crack. The Emperor gave them superhuman strength, immortal lifespans, and the authority to command Legions. What he did not give them was the wisdom to comprehend their fate.

The Horus Heresy was never, at root, a military rebellion. It was a chain reaction of father-son tragedy. Look down this table: Fulgrim destroyed by a daemon-blade; Angron destroyed because the Emperor denied him an honorable death; Mortarion destroyed because the Emperor stole his revenge; Lorgar destroyed because the Emperor publicly humiliated his faith; Magnus destroyed by a well-intentioned warning. Even Horus himself was nothing more than a cage of lies set by the Chaos Gods — the lie that "the Emperor will betray you." The Emperor forged twenty weapons and twenty unresolved psychological wounds, and Chaos needed only to give the slightest push for it all to tear apart.

Of the twenty sons, half turned traitor, half stayed loyal — but the symmetry is misleading. Among the loyal nine, not all truly "agreed" with the Emperor. The Khan chose after careful deliberation. Russ was a blind executor. Corax harbored grievances deep within. Later successors like Dante fought for faith, not for the Emperor himself. Among the traitor nine, save for Horus and Lorgar, few truly believed in Chaos — Angron hated the Emperor more than he loved Khorne, Mortarion surrendered under duress, Perturabo despised corruption.

Only two men embraced their choice from beginning to end: Sanguinius, who flew to Terra knowing he would die; and Konrad Curze, who did not dodge the assassin, knowing he would die. The former is the Imperium's most exalted martyr; the latter is its darkest accuser.

In the M42 era, the loyalists have Lion and Guilliman as twin regents, while the traitors field six Daemon Princes and countless scattered warbands. But what truly matters is not the balance of power — it is the "return" itself. The sons of the Emperor are rising, one by one, from slumber, exile, and the lost. This means the story of the galaxy is not approaching its end. The oldest page has only just been turned.

This table of the twenty Primarch lineages is the bloodline map of the Imperium's ten-thousand-year enmity. Only by reading its sequence can one read this history.
