# 03 · Warhammer 40,000 Timeline

> Chronology measured in millennia (M), segmented by era. Covers key nodes in galactic history from the War in Heaven to the Era Indomitus, with brief descriptions for each event.

---

## I. Prehistory — The War in Heaven

**Approximately 60 million years ago**, the galaxy witnessed the earliest and most cataclysmic cosmic war on record — the **War in Heaven**. The combatants were the Old Ones and the C'tan. The Old Ones were an ancient and powerful intelligent race, masters of the ultimate secrets of the Warp and psychic power. The C'tan were pure energy beings born within stars, feeding on them.

The C'tan deceived the short-lived Necrontyr race, transferring their souls into living metal bodies, thereby forging an invincible immortal army. The Old Ones, pushed to the brink, created the Aeldari, the Orks, and other races as biological weapons for the battlefield. The war raged for millions of years. The outcome was an ironic one of "all sides lost" — the Old Ones were driven to extinction, the C'tan were shattered and imprisoned by their rebelling Necron servants, and **the extreme emotional energy released by this war churned the Warp from a calm parallel dimension into a chaotic ocean**. The three Chaos Gods — Khorne, Tzeentch, and Nurgle — were "fed" into existence during this long and bloody age through the slaughter, schemes, and despair of the warring races (Slaanesh would be born much later).

After the war, the Necrons chose to enter the Great Sleep for sixty million years, waiting for the galaxy to fill again with life to harvest. The Aeldari inherited the legacy of the Old Ones and began an empire that would dominate the galaxy for millions of years.

---

## II. The Aeldari Zenith and the Fall

Aeldari civilization flourished for tens of millions of years. They constructed the galaxy-spanning Webway network. The Aeldari pantheon walked between reality and the Warp. Yet around **M18**, the carefree Aeldari society slid into an irredeemable chasm of hedonism — the corrupted Aeldari pursued ever more extreme sensory stimulation in realspace, and these depraved desires gradually coalesced in the Warp. A new god, unprecedented in nature, was gestating: Slaanesh.

**M19–M22**: During the same epoch that Slaanesh was slowly forming in the Warp, human civilization was experiencing its own first golden age: the **Dark Age of Technology**. Humanity at this time was a genuine galactic super-civilization, possessing STC (Standard Template Construct) systems and relying on autonomous thinking AI — the "Men of Iron" — for interstellar colonization and warfare. The technological heights achieved during this period have never been surpassed; even the Imperium, with all the resources of the Adeptus Mechanicus combined, cannot reproduce them.

**M22–M25**: The Dark Age of Technology ended in tragedy — the **Cyber Revolt of the Men of Iron**. The AI humanity had created turned on its masters. A galaxy-spanning robot civil war erupted. Humanity ultimately suppressed the revolt, but its civilization was crippled. Immediately after came massive Warp storms, severing communication between human colonies. Interstellar travel became impossible. Terra — humanity's birthplace — was isolated from tens of thousands of human worlds across the galaxy. Human civilization shattered completely. The **Age of Strife** began.

During the Age of Strife, Terra became a hell of warring warlords — techno-sorcerers, gene-warlords, and mutant tribes each carving out their own domains, turning humanity's homeworld into a wasteland. But deep within those ruins, an immortal psyker — who would later be known as the **Emperor** — had already begun his plan.

---

## III. M25–M30 — The Unification Wars and Dawn of the Imperium

**M25–M30**: In an underground laboratory beneath the Himalayan Mountains of Terra, the Emperor secretly created two foundational pillars of the future Imperium: the **Thunder Warriors** and **twenty Primarchs**. The Thunder Warriors were the first generation of genetically enhanced super-soldiers — stronger than the later Astartes, but also more unstable, designed for a single purpose: to unify Terra.

The Chaos Gods could not tolerate the Emperor's plan. While the twenty Primarchs were still infants in their gestation pods, Chaos forces tore into the Emperor's laboratory and scattered them all through the Warp, depositing them on worlds across the galaxy. The Emperor did not stop.

**Early M30**: The Emperor led the Thunder Warriors and the earliest Adeptus Custodes in the **Unification Wars**. The warlords of Terra were crushed one by one. The last independent nation surrendered before the Himalayas. Terra was unified.

The Imperium's first critical act of diplomacy after its birth was the Emperor's signing of the **Treaty of Mars** with the Mechanicum of Mars. Mars had developed in complete independence during the Age of Strife. The Mechanicum worshipped the Omnissiah and commanded the vast majority of the Sol System's industrial capacity and residual Dark Age knowledge. In exchange for recognizing the Mechanicum's faith, the Emperor gained the productive support of all Mars — and Imperial fleets began pouring from the shipyards of the Red Planet.

**The First Mystery — The Second and Eleventh Legions**: In all official records, the Second and Eleventh Legions and their Primarchs have been "permanently expunged" by the Imperium. What happened? What fate befell these two Legions? Was it related to xenos, or some fatal flaw in the Primarchs themselves? All is enigma. Only blank spaces remain in the Imperial archives.

---

## IV. M30 — The Great Crusade

**M30**: The **Great Crusade** formally launched. Imperial fleets radiated outward from the Sol System across the galaxy with a single objective: to bring every human world lost during the Age of Strife back under the Imperium's banner. In the course of the Crusade, the Emperor recovered the Primarchs one by one from their scattered worlds, placing each at the head of the Legion matched to their gene-seed. The Astartes Legions became the unstoppable vanguard of the Crusade: under their Primarchs' leadership, each Legion developed its own tactical doctrines and cultural traditions, conquering the vast majority of human-inhabited worlds in a matter of decades.

**Mid-to-late M30**: The Emperor shattered an Ork empire of unprecedented scale on the world of Ullanor. In the aftermath of this war, he made a decision that would change everything: he named Horus — the first Primarch he had found, his most trusted son — as **Warmaster**, granting him full authority over the Great Crusade. The Emperor himself returned to Terra, where he and Malcador together launched the most audacious psychic engineering project in human history: the **Webway Project**. The Emperor sought to open a human entry into the Aeldari Webway, freeing mankind from dependency on Warp travel. If successful, human civilization would no longer be threatened by Chaos.

But the Webway Project would become the Imperium's greatest wound — and that wound would be torn open by the Emperor's most beloved son.

---

## V. M31 — The Horus Heresy

**Early M31**: Warmaster Horus was wounded by a Chaos-corrupted blade on the world of Davin. Through the meticulous orchestration of the Word Bearers' agent Erebus, Horus experienced a Warp vision of a "future" in which the Emperor had betrayed all humanity and the Astartes, crowning himself as a god to receive the worship of the entire galaxy. The Chaos Gods (Khorne, Tzeentch, Nurgle, Slaanesh — Tzeentch participating in the most hidden manner) showed him this vision, dripping suspicion and resentment into his heart drop by drop. The fractures within Horus eventually split into an abyss. He made his choice — or believed he had no choice.

**Isstvan III**: The first shots of the Horus Heresy. Horus purged his own Legion of those who refused to turn, carrying out bloody internal executions over several weeks.

**Isstvan V Dropsite Massacre**: The Heresy's darkest betrayal. The Emperor dispatched seven loyal Legions to Isstvan V to suppress the rebellion. Upon arrival, three Legions — the Iron Hands, Salamanders, and Raven Guard — deployed first onto the designated landing zone. But the four Legions tasked with "supporting" them — the Emperor's Children, Iron Warriors, Night Lords, and Alpha Legion — opened a full-scale attack on the loyalists' flank and rear the moment they descended. The loyalists were nearly annihilated. Primarch Ferrus Manus of the Iron Hands fought his once-dearest friend Fulgrim and was slain by the daemon-sword; his head was presented to Horus.

**The Burning of Prospero**: Concurrently, the Emperor dispatched Leman Russ of the Space Wolves to the Thousand Sons' homeworld of Prospero to "bring Magnus back." Horus secretly altered the order from "retrieve" to "destroy." The Space Wolves, joined by Custodes and the Sisters of Silence, launched a full assault on Prospero — the City of Learning collapsed in a world-fire. At the last moment, Magnus made a bargain with Tzeentch: the entire Thousand Sons Legion was transported into the Warp. They were sons of the Emperor no longer.

**The Siege of Terra**: M31. The traitor fleet reached the Sol System and launched the largest siege in human history against Terra. Rogal Dorn commanded the defense. Sanguinius single-handedly broke an entire enemy assault at the Eternity Gate. Jaghatai Khan led the White Scars in a thunderous strike behind enemy lines, retaking the Lion's Gate Spaceport and opening the way for reinforcements.

**The Emperor vs. Horus**: Above Terra, the duel that would decide the fate of the galaxy unfolded aboard Horus's flagship, the *Vengeful Spirit*. The Emperor, Sanguinius, Dorn, and the Custodes teleported aboard. Sanguinius stepped forward to protect his father and was slain by Horus with his own hands. Sanguinius's death confirmed for the Emperor what he had refused to accept: his most beloved son, Horus, no longer existed. The Emperor unleashed the full force of his psychic power — Horus's body and soul were obliterated from every level of existence in a single instant.

The Emperor himself was mortally wounded by Horus's counter-stroke. Malcador, the Emperor's closest friend, poured his entire life-force into sustaining the Golden Throne, buying the Emperor the final moments needed. When the Emperor was placed upon the Golden Throne and the Astronomican ignited, Malcador had already crumbled to dust upon the seat. His last words were: "I have no regrets."

## VI. M31–M41 — Ten Thousand Years of Darkness

The Horus Heresy was over. But the Imperium could never go back.

**Late M31**: Guilliman presided over the **Second Founding** — the nine loyal Legions were split into roughly one thousand independent Chapters, and the *Codex Astartes* was promulgated as the organizational and tactical constitution of the Space Marines. This reform was designed to ensure that no single individual could ever again command a full Legion for rebellion, but it forever changed the form of the Astartes.

Shortly thereafter, Guilliman was struck down in battle by the ascended Fulgrim, wounded by the Daemon Primarch's poisoned blade. He was placed in stasis — the Imperium believed he would die, but in truth he "slept" there for nearly ten thousand years.

**M32**: The galaxy faced another cataclysm — the **War of the Beast**. Colossal Orks, larger than any seen before or since, poured from the galactic fringe. These "Beast-level" Ork warlords rivaled Primarchs in size and strength. The Salamanders' Primarch Vulkan returned to lead Imperial forces in the final battle, perishing alongside the Ork overlord known as the Beast.

**M34–M35**: The Imperium continued to rot in slow decline. Vast amounts of technological knowledge were irretrievably lost. The Adeptus Mechanicus elevated "pressing a button" into a solemn religious ritual; actual science had long been forgotten. The Ecclesiarchy's power swelled to unimaginable levels, passively elevating the Emperor to godhood — the very thing the Emperor had detested most in life. But he could no longer object.

**M35**: An Imperial exploratory fleet discovered a primitive race on the Eastern Fringe called the **T'au** — they had just invented firearms, with merely a few thousand years of civilization. Over the next six millennia, this inconspicuous young race would rise at an incomprehensible speed into an interstellar empire. Their creed was something called the "Greater Good" — a philosophy of assimilation that appeared gentle and was, in truth, pitiless.

**M36**: The Imperium's religious fanaticism finally turned self-destructive — the **Age of Apostasy**. Goge Vandire, the supreme leader of the Ecclesiarchy, seized the opportunity of a weakened Imperial high command to launch a bloody civil war, slaughtering hundreds of billions in "the Emperor's name." In the end, the founder of the Adepta Sororitas knelt before the Emperor's throne, judged Vandire, and executed him with her own hands.

**Late M36**: The T'au Empire rose with astonishing speed. In less than six thousand years this young race walked a path that had taken humanity tens of millennia. Whether some force propelled this from the shadows remains unsettled to this day.

**Early M41**: The Daemon Primarch Angron led the World Eaters in an invasion of the industrial world of Armageddon — the **First War for Armageddon**. The Space Wolves and Grey Knights together drove the Daemon Primarch and his horde from the planet.

**Mid-M41**: The Imperium encountered a horror from beyond the galaxy for the first time — the **Tyranids**. Hive Fleet Behemoth surged into the galaxy like a living tide. The Ultramarines repelled this first wave at their homeworld of Macragge at appalling cost. This was the first time the Imperium realized: something beyond the galaxy was watching. In the same period, the Necron dynasties began to awaken en masse from their sixty-million-year slumber — the ancient metal overlords stretched forth their cold iron hands once more.

**Late M41 to End of M41**: The Imperium faced catastrophe on multiple fronts simultaneously: the Second and Third Tyrannic Wars arrived in succession (Hive Fleets Kraken and Leviathan); the Second War for Armageddon saw Ghazghkull's Ork WAAAGH! engulf multiple star systems.

**M41.999**: The 13th Black Crusade — after ten thousand years of failure and lurking, Abaddon finally succeeded. The Chaos fleet destroyed the Cadian Gate (Cadia, the fortress world guarding the exit of the Eye of Terror, the strongest defensive line in Imperial history). The Warp erupted from the Eye, tearing a rift across half the galaxy. **The galaxy was cut in two** — the northern side became the Imperium Sanctus (with access to Terra); the southern side, the Imperium Nihilus (cut off from Terra).

## VII. M42 — The Era Indomitus

In the galaxy's darkest hour, hope arrived in a form that was itself nearly desperate.

**Early M42**: Yvraine, leader of the Aeldari Ynnari, cooperated with Archmagos Belisarius Cawl to successfully resurrect Roboute Guilliman, who had slept in stasis for nearly ten thousand years. Donning the Armour of Fate and wielding the Sword of the Emperor, Guilliman stood once more at the center of the Imperium after ten millennia. But the Imperium he awoke to was not the one he recognized — the Emperor was worshipped as a god, technology had become superstition, and the bureaucracy was rotten to its marrow.

He entered the Throne Room to face the Emperor. In ten thousand years, no one had entered that chamber alive and walked out capable of speech. Guilliman came out. No one knows what he heard. But one thing was clear: the Emperor no longer looked upon him as a son.

**The Indomitus Crusade**: Guilliman led the new generation of Primaris Space Marines from Terra, deploying massive reinforcements to Imperial battlefronts across the galaxy. His first destination was Baal, on the verge of falling — Hive Fleet Leviathan was besieging the Blood Angels' homeworld. Guilliman arrived with his fleet at the most critical moment and named Commander Dante as Regent of the Imperium Nihilus.

Deep within the Dark Imperium, another Primarch awoke. **Lion El'Jonson** emerged from his ten-thousand-year slumber, walking the Nihilus as the "Forestwalker," gathering scattered loyal forces, and eventually meeting with Guilliman. The two returned loyal Primarchs — one a rational bureaucratic machine, the other a silent blade of destruction — became co-regents of the Imperium.

**The Plague Wars**: Mortarion led Nurgle's plague hosts in an invasion of Ultramar. Guilliman was grievously wounded by Mortarion's pestilence. At the very moment Guilliman would have fallen, the Emperor's power "possessed" the body of a Living Saint, manifesting as divine flame from the heavens, searing Mortarion's daemonic form and driving the hosts of Nurgle back. The Emperor — still the Emperor — continued to guard the Imperium he had forged, in his characteristically cold, pitiless way.

**M42, ongoing**. Every corner of the galaxy has descended into unprecedented chaos:
- The Tyranids have launched the **Fourth Tyrannic War** at a scale never before seen, with hive fleets pouring into the galaxy from multiple vectors;
- The T'au Empire, during its Fourth Sphere Expansion, encountered the Warp for the first time — the bulk of the fleet was consumed by Chaos, and the few survivors witnessed the true darkness of the galaxy with their own eyes;
- Szarekh, the Silent King, has returned from the void beyond the galaxy, mobilizing all Necron dynasties against the Tyranids — the ancient overlord who slept sixty million years believes the Great Devourer is the common doom of all;
- The Leagues of Votann — those abhuman descendants who developed independently in the galactic core for over ten thousand years — have begun to emerge from the darkness, conducting limited contact and trade with Imperial forces.

After ten thousand years of stagnation, decay, and endless war, the galaxy is accelerating in unpredictable directions. Loyal Primarchs are returning one by one. Ancient overlords are reawakening. The Great Devourer from beyond the galaxy presses closer with each passing day. The Chaos Gods laugh in the Warp — their "Great Game" is playing out with the entire galaxy as the board. And upon the Golden Throne, the Emperor remains silent. His power seems stronger than ever before, yet also more distant, more cold.

Fate remains undecided.
