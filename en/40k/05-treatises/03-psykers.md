# 03 · Psykers

> Psychic power is not a set of techniques, nor a skill, but a contract signed between the soul and the Warp. Borrow from the formless sea; work tangible force in realspace. Yet anyone who borrows power from the Warp must inevitably pay the price — madness, death, or becoming a plaything of the gods. This is the iron law of the galaxy, unchanged across all the ages.

---

## I. What Is Psychic Power

The essence of psychic power is simple: the Warp is a mirror.

Every emotion experienced by sapient life in the galaxy — rage, hope, despair, desire — stirs ripples in the Warp. Over epochs those ripples coalesced into an ocean. The ocean has fish. When the fish grow large enough, they are given names: Khorne, Tzeentch, Nurgle, Slaanesh. And the psyker is one born with the ability to reach a hand into that sea.

An ordinary human looks at the Warp through the barrier of reality and sees nothing. A psyker is different. Their soul is born with a crack, and through that crack the energy of the Warp flows in, shaped by their will into fire, lightning, prophecy, telepathy — every supernatural power you might find in speculative fiction has, in the 40K universe, a single source: the Warp.

This is why the Imperium simultaneously needs psykers and fears them. The psyker's soul is a lighthouse in the Warp — they can see the sea, and the things in the sea can see them.

## II. The Psyker's Price

Every psyker's life is a countdown gamble. The stake is the soul. The house is Chaos.

The first time a nascent psyker uses their power, they feel a rapture unlike anything before — the first tremor of the soul touching Warp energy directly, like a drowning man's first gasp of water, simultaneously intoxicated by the light beneath the surface. From that moment on, every act of channeling is another knock on the door of the soul.

The problem is, you can never know whether the one opening the door is yourself — or whatever is waiting on the other side.

The mild cases are called "psychic feedback" — loss of control, energy surging back to sear nerves, eyes bursting from their sockets. The more common end is "daemonic possession": at the instant the caster's will wavers, a fish from the sea slides in through the crack. Stage one of possession is whispering — the psyker hears "suggestions" that sound entirely reasonable. Stage two is parasitism — the daemon has moved in but still wears the shell of the host's own consciousness, and the host has no idea they are now acting on the daemon's will. Stage three is blooming — the daemon is ripe, and it tears an exit through the host's body, consuming the soul as its first meal.

The cruelest irony is that the more powerful the psyker, the more attractive the target. A low-grade psyker might be harassed by a minor daemon. An Alpha-level psyker blazes so brightly in the Warp that the Chaos Gods themselves will turn their gaze.

This is the ultimate law of psychic power: **the more water you draw from the sea, the more things in the sea want your life.**

## III. The Emperor and Psychic Power

The Emperor is the most powerful psyker in human history. Not "one of." Not "in the records." The most powerful. There is no upper limit.

His very origin was a psychic ritual: around 8000 BC, the ancient shamans of Anatolia foresaw that humanity's psychic awakening would invite catastrophe. They gathered and committed mass ritual suicide, merging all their souls into a single new being — an immortal, perpetual psyker. That infant would later be called the Emperor.

The Emperor walked hidden through human history for forty thousand years. He knew the truth of the Warp, knew the existence of the Chaos Gods, and knew that humanity's psychic awakening was irreversible. He designed humanity's only viable exit: the Webway.

The Webway is a network of arteries carved by the Old Ones sixty million years ago through the Warp's turbulent sea — tunnels bored into the Immaterium itself. Within the Webway, there is no corruption of Chaos. Travel through it is safe, requiring no reliance on Warp storms or Navigators. The Emperor's plan was simple: move all of humanity into the Webway, let the human race evolve naturally and safely as a psychic species within that protected environment, until it matured enough to withstand Chaos. At that point, the Warp and the Chaos Gods would lose all control over humanity.

To achieve this, he secretly built a human-accessible Webway portal beneath the Imperial Palace on Terra — a vast psychic engineering project creating a direct passage from reality into the Webway. This required the Emperor himself to sit continuously upon the Golden Throne to sustain the psychic barrier.

Then Magnus destroyed it all.

Magnus the Red was the most powerful psyker among the Primarchs. When he foresaw Horus's impending rebellion, he used every psychic means at his disposal to send a warning to Terra — at the price of his psychic blast directly breaching the seals on the Emperor's Webway portal. A flood of Chaotic force from the Warp poured in. Countless daemons surged through the breach into Terra's subterranean tunnels. The Emperor's Custodes and Mechanicum tech-adepts fought the daemons for years in the dark, and ultimately the entire portal had to be permanently sealed behind psychic barriers.

The Emperor's life's work — destroyed by his most brilliant son trying to do the right thing.

The tale of the **Edict of Nikaea** is equally tragic. During the Great Crusade, the Emperor convened a gathering of all the Primarchs at Nikaea and issued a decree: the Astartes Legions were forbidden from using psychic powers, with exceptions only for Navigators and Astropaths. This was later proven by the forces of Chaos to have been a perfect move on their part: it tied the hands of loyalist psykers while doing nothing to restrain the traitors. Chaos sorcerers continued their workings unhindered; loyalist Librarians were bound by their own doctrine, waiting to die. The Edict was revoked late in the Heresy — far too late.

## IV. Psychic Grades

The Imperium's psychic classification system uses Greek letter designations, from low to high:

| Grade | Characteristics |
|------|------|
| **Non-psyker** | Ordinary human, soul sealed to the Warp. The vast majority of humanity. |
| **Rho / Pi and below** | Faint psychic resonance — may manifest as "excellent intuition" or "abnormal luck." Insufficient to qualify as a psyker. |
| **Omicron (O) – Eta** | Formally in psyker territory, but power very weak. The Imperium recruits these as Astropaths or low-grade sanctioned psychic functionaries. |
| **Zeta – Delta** | Appreciable psychic power. Capable of stable offensive manifestations, precognition, mind control. The mainstay of the Imperium's Sanctioned Psykers. |
| **Gamma – Beta** | Extremely powerful. A Beta-level psyker can demolish buildings with thought and shape the course of large-scale battles. Exceedingly rare, and exceedingly dangerous. |
| **Alpha** | A strategic weapon in a single human form. An Alpha-level psyker can destroy a city; they can, in theory, mentally dominate an entire army. Alphas in Imperial history are vanishingly few. |
| **Alpha-Plus** | No upper limit. In theory, capable of affecting an entire world or star system. Magnus stood at this level. The Emperor transcends all classification. |

One brutal fact must be noted: **the higher the psychic grade, the higher the probability of being consumed by Chaos.** Most Alpha-level psykers are dead before the Imperium ever finds them — either possessed by daemons, destroyed by their own uncontrolled power, or burned alive by local citizens with torches and pitchforks.

## V. The Psyker's Place in the Imperium

The Imperium's stance toward psykers can be expressed in four words: **use them and kill them.**

Psykers are the necessary condition for the Imperium's continued existence. Without psykers, the Astronomican cannot be lit — it is a psychic lighthouse projected into the Warp by the Emperor's will, and all of humanity's fleets navigate by it. Without the Astronomican, there is no interstellar civilization. And sustaining the Astronomican requires the **daily sacrifice of one thousand psykers**, their souls fed directly into the Golden Throne to burn as fuel for the Emperor's psychic radiance.

This is the Imperium's deepest irony: the Imperium is built upon the bones of psykers, yet hates psykers down to its marrow.

The Imperium's psyker management system runs roughly as follows:

**The Black Ships** — The Inquisition's Black Ships cruise the entire galaxy, harvesting psykers from every Imperial world. They offer no explanation, no compensation, no regard for consent. Every individual discovered to possess psychic talent — man, woman, child, elder — is taken.

**Sorting** — Psykers are transported to Terra, where the Inquisition screens them:
- Those who are qualified, with sufficiently strong will, are trained as **Sanctioned Psykers**, assigned to serve the Astra Militarum, the Imperial Navy, or the various Ordos of the Inquisition.
- Those with weaker but stable power undergo **Soul-Binding** — their souls are seared by the Emperor's psychic will, allowing them to channel power safely. The cost is blindness and partial loss of personality. These become **Astropaths**, responsible for the Imperium's interstellar communication.
- The unqualified, the unstable, the too-dangerous — taken straight to **fuel the Golden Throne**. One thousand a day, unbroken for ten thousand years.
- The completely uncontrolled — **executed on the spot**.

**The Grey Knights** — The Imperium's sole all-psyker Space Marine Chapter. Every member is a hand-picked psyker, genetically modified and subjected to the most grueling soul-trials. They are the Imperium's ultimate anti-daemon weapon. The very existence of the Grey Knights is the final repudiation of the Edict of Nikaea: the Imperium understood long ago that one cannot fight Chaos without psychic power.

The ordinary human's attitude toward psykers is fear. In the doctrine of the Ecclesiarchy, psykers are "the accursed," their gift a mark of Chaotic taint. On the Imperium's remoter worlds, any child showing psychic tendencies may be reported by their own family, or mobbed to death by the local populace. The Inquisition's stance on this: so long as it doesn't cause a major incident, condone vigilante justice.

## VI. Chaotic Psychic Power and Imperial Psychic Power

Psychic power itself has no moral polarity, but the source and the path taken determine the destination.

The hallmark of **Chaotic psychic power** (or sorcery) is the **absence of boundaries**. Chaos sorcerers impose no restraint, build no barriers, and surrender all power to emotion — rage, desire, ambition, despair. Their power is therefore immense, because the Chaos Gods eagerly grant it. But every ounce of strength is also a link in a chain. Every Chaos psyker will eventually discover one fact: **when a daemon tells you that you are special, it means you have a particularly appetizing flavor.** Examples litter the galaxy: Fulgrim's personality devoured by a daemon-blade. Mortarion imprisoned and tortured into surrender by Nurgle. Magnus, convinced he was exploiting Tzeentch, was played by Tzeentch for ten thousand years.

The hallmark of **Imperial psychic power** (the Sanctioned Psyker system) is **discipline**. The Imperium trains psykers by essentially "building a wall inside the soul." Through ritual, symbol, mental frameworks, and the Emperor's will as the anchor-point, the psyker is held at a distance from the Warp's raging chaos. Every sanctioned psyker murmurs invocations to the Emperor before each channeling — not because the Emperor is a god, but because the ritual itself is a psychic barrier. By concentrating on the ritual, the psyker is not distracted by the whispers of the Warp.

The essential difference lies here:
- Chaos psykers leave their souls **open** to the Warp, trading absolute power for absolute cost;
- Imperial psykers keep their souls **sealed**, leaving only a pinhole passage, trading a drastic reduction in power for safety.

Whether the wall holds, however, comes down to individual will. The history of the Imperium is littered with fallen Inquisitors and Sanctioned Psykers — most of whom began with the rationale "I must use Chaos's own power to fight Chaos," and ended by discovering that the power of Chaos has never cared about your rationale.

---

> Psychic power is humanity's sole weapon of survival in the galaxy — and its most lethal self-destruct switch. The Emperor spent his entire life trying to resolve this paradox. The Webway Project was humanity's closest brush with "safe evolution." It failed. And so the Imperium of Man walks a blood-soaked tightrope: light the Astronomican with your own psykers to guide the fleets, fight Chaos with yet more psykers, and burn the rest at the stake as abominations. This contradictory cycle has turned for ten thousand years with no sign of stopping. Perhaps this is exactly the situation the Chaos Gods most desire: the Imperium is not fighting Chaos — it sustains its own existence through methods indistinguishable from Chaos itself.

---

## VII. Soul-Binding

The logic of Soul-Binding is brutal to the point of cruelty: if a human soul is not sturdy enough, then weld onto it a fragment of the Emperor's own.

The ritual takes place before the Golden Throne. The candidate Astropath cannot face the Emperor directly — an ordinary human who approaches within a certain radius of the Throne is simply incinerated by the Emperor's ambient psychic radiance — but the distance across several layers of psychic barrier suffices. The Emperor's will, that psychic flame that has not dimmed for forty thousand years, pierces the candidate's soul, permanently searing their optic nerves to carbon while simultaneously branding a sliver of the Emperor's own psychic signature into the depths of the soul.

All Astropaths are blind. This is not metaphor. Their eyeballs and optic nerves are carbonized from within by the radiance of the Emperor's psyche, their sockets left with nothing but charred darkness. Yet in losing their physical eyes they gain a deeper sight: the ability to perceive the fluctuations of the Warp directly, to read the texture of souls, to send and receive psychic messages across light-years. An Astropath never "sees" you — they feel the shape of your soul.

The price is not blindness alone. Soul-Binding shears away a corner of the personality. Astropaths are generally silent, withdrawn, emotionally blunted. This is not numbness — it is scarring. A human soul pried open by a fragment of the Emperor's will is like a clay cup filled with molten lead; the cracks never heal. Lifespan is accordingly diminished. Most Astropaths do not survive thirty years beyond Binding.

But the reward is irreplaceable: a Soul-Bound psyker is immune to Warp corruption. No daemon can touch a soul branded with the Emperor's mark. In the eyes of Warp predators, an Astropath is not prey but forbidden territory — the Emperor's claim. This is why Astropaths form the backbone of interstellar communication for the Imperium. Every day hundreds of thousands of psychic transmissions race across the galaxy, sent and received by a population whose physical eyes have been closed forever. The nervous system of the Imperium is a web woven by the blind.

## VIII. The Black Ships

The Inquisition's Black Ships are not merely "black" in hull-paint. The entire vessel is clad inside and out with anti-psychic materials, rendering the ship nearly "silent" in the Warp — simultaneously a cloak to prevent Warp entities from noticing the cargo of psykers aboard, and a cage ensuring that not a wisp of psychic energy leaks to the outside.

The collection protocol leaves no room for mercy. A fleet arrives with seventy-two hours' notice to the planetary governor: prepare your psyker register. All aboard within twenty-four hours. Late — the governor is removed and executed. No exceptions. No bargaining. No consideration that "this child is only six." Psychic talent knows no age; six years old or sixty, wrist irons for all.

Those delivered are a cross-section of humanity: an infant girl in swaddling, her fingers unconsciously tracing psychic arcs in the air. A mine worker who discovered his difference when he held up an entire tunnel with his mind during a cave-in. An old woman who spent a lifetime believing she was merely a "fortune-teller" — until the Inquisition knocked on her door.

Life aboard is a preview of hell. Each psyker is locked into an individual null-coffin, its interior coated with Blackstone dust brought back by the Null-Sisters of the Silent Sisterhood, suppressing all psychic activity. The coffin is just large enough for a body to lie down; the light is dim; sedatives drip through tubes into veins, keeping the brain in a near-comatose state — an alert psyker is a dangerous psyker. The voyage from a frontier sector to Terra can last years. Years spent lying in an iron coffin, half-dreaming, half-awake, occasionally jolted by a sound from the next cell: another psyker has lost control and is turning into something inside their coffin. Then the sound cuts off. The suppressant hums. The Silent Sisters have resolved the matter.

Roughly sixty percent reach Terra alive. The sorting conveyor's criteria are as follows: those with the strongest will and most stable power go to the Astra Militarum as battle-psykers, hurling lightning and mind-slaying heretics on the battlefield — not many survive three campaigns. The stable but weaker undergo Soul-Binding to become Astropaths, blinded then dispatched to communication nodes across the galaxy, spending the rest of their lives sending and receiving invisible words. The moderately unstable but still useful serve as psychic labor, sent to the Astronomican's maintenance crews, the forges of psychic weaponry, the Inquisition's experimental blocks — measured in months of expected lifespan, which suffices. Those for whom no other use has been identified — into the Hollow Mountain, fuel for the Golden Throne. One thousand a day. Those truly dangerous, already showing signs of possession — shot on the spot. A blessed penetrator round through the skull. A charred silence.

The Imperium does not treat psykers as human beings. They are a resource — more precious than promethium, more unstable than a machine-spirit — but equally indispensable.

## IX. The Daily Maintenance of the Golden Throne

The Golden Throne is not a throne. It is a soul engine.

Deep beneath Terra lies a vast psychic facility known as the **Hollow Mountain**, its interior hollowed out, its tunnels snaking hundreds of kilometers, dense with pipes, cables, sacred oil channels, and psychic crystal arrays. At the center is the Golden Throne — upon which the Emperor has rotted for ten thousand years, silent and burning.

The Throne is, in essence, a tripartite fusion of life-support apparatus, psychic amplifier, and soul furnace. The Emperor's physical body can no longer be called "alive," but the Emperor's will is pumped through the Throne into the Warp, taking form as the Astronomican — a psychic lighthouse spanning the galaxy, by which the Imperium's millions of warships orient themselves. No Astronomican, no faster-than-light travel. No travel, no human civilization.

The fuel for this system is psykers. Every day at dawn, the Soul-Rite Management Office selects one thousand psykers from the stocks, each stamped "no other use identified," and sends them into the deepest reaches of the Hollow Mountain. Their souls are connected to the Throne's psychic reflux conduits — not physical pipes, but psychic siphons that draw human soul-energy directly. The life-force of each thousand is poured into the Emperor, and the Emperor's will burns their souls as kindling to light the flame of the Astronomican.

Death is not instantaneous. A psyker's soul is drained at a measured rate; the entire process lasts ten to twelve hours. The body gradually fades, desiccates, finally collapsing like burnt paper, crumbling at the slightest touch. In the last minutes before the end, psychic power will erupt in a brief spasm — the final convulsion of a soul in absolute dissolution. Then the next batch is connected. Twenty-four-hour rotation. No pause. Every day of every year.

A thousand a day. Three hundred sixty-five thousand a year. Over thirty-six billion in ten thousand years. This is the price of the Emperor's continued existence. This is also why the Imperium's deepest secret is not "Is the Emperor still alive?" but the fact that no one truly wants the Emperor to *awaken*. If he awoke and the Astronomican guttered out, the Imperium would within a month dissolve into a million isolated worlds, each to be gnawed apart by Chaos like an ant colony. But anyone who contemplates the fact that the Emperor must consume a thousand souls a day to continue existing will find their faith shaken. The Inquisition's countermeasure is undressed: to be shaken is heresy. Burn the question along with the questioner.

---

## Conclusion

Soul-Binding, the Black Ships, the Golden Throne — these three processes form a closed circuit of the Imperium's psychic system, cold as an assembly line: the psykers of every Imperial world are swept clean by the Black Ships; those with the strongest will are branded with the Emperor's mark, becoming Astropaths, losing their physical sight to carry invisible light for all humanity; those without sufficient will are placed upon the altar and turned into fuel, feeding a civilization that cannibalizes its own kind; those who slip through the net, who lose control, are lynched by neighbors, shot by the Inquisition, devoured by Chaos — where they die matters little, because the machinery of the Imperium will not pause for a single second.

For ten thousand years, this cycle has never broken. Perhaps this is the Emperor's deepest, unspoken terror: not that Chaos will defeat humanity, but that humanity can no longer survive without this "psyker-as-fuel" system. An Imperium built in the name of opposing Chaos, whose daily operation is as cold, as hungry, and as endless as Chaos itself.
