# 07 · Imperial Wargear and Weaponry

## Introduction

This text is a pure reference work — no political struggles, no heroic legends, no organizational structures or cultures. It answers one simple question: what exactly does the Imperium of Man fight with? Each piece of equipment is described with one or two memorable characteristics. The background and organization of the Astra Militarum are covered in Book 05 (*The Astra Militarum*), the genetic origins of the Space Marines in Book 02 (*The Primarchs*), and the manufacturing system of the Mechanicus in Book 04 (*The Mechanicus and Technology*) — this text does not expand on those, only on the weapons themselves.

The Imperium's weapon philosophy is simple: reliability over advancement, quantity over quality, a weapon that works for ten thousand years will never be innovated once.

---

## I. Astra Militarum Wargear

The Astra Militarum is the Imperium's most basic military unit. Its equipment design follows one principle: give the civilians conscripted from this world something they can learn to use.

### 1. Standard Infantry Equipment

**Lasgun (M35 M-Galaxy Pattern)**

The most common standard-issue rifle of the Astra Militarum and the most numerous individual weapon in the galaxy. The lasgun fires a high-energy laser beam; when it hits a target it does not explode or tear — it flash-vaporizes human tissue into a stench of charred steam. Killing power is roughly equivalent to a contemporary assault rifle, but its true advantage lies in logistics: power packs can be recharged in a campfire, on a vehicle engine, or even left in the sun for a day to recover a few shots. On a battlefield where Tyranids and Greenskins pour in from every direction, a rifle that does not depend on an ammunition supply chain is a lifesaver. Downsides: the barrel overheats under sustained fire, humidity or smoke degrades beam potency, and it cannot penetrate heavy armor. But it is enough — the Imperium does not ask you to defeat a Chaos Space Marine, only to take down as many cultists as you can before you die.

**Laspistol**

A scaled-down lasgun, issued to Commissars, officers, and vehicle crew. Power and range are inferior to the rifle, but portability and close-range stopping power remain reliable. Commissars typically carry bolt pistols rather than laspistols — more on this later.

**Frag Grenade and Krak Grenade**

The frag grenade turns a man into shredded meat; the krak grenade focuses its detonation into a single point — designed to bore a melt-hole through armor plate. Astra Militarum doctrine requires each soldier to carry at least two: one to break up the enemy formation, one for dealing with that Ork tin-can you just ran into.

**Bayonet**

A close-quarters blade fixed beneath the lasgun barrel. The Astra Militarum does not expect infantry to fight hand-to-hand — that is the Space Marines' work — but after the trench is breached, a bayonet at least lets you kill one before you die. Kriegers adore bayonet charges; other regiments treat the bayonet as a last resort. Astra Militarum bayonets use no high technology: thirty centimeters of single-edged steel, capable of stabbing through anything not wearing ceramite plate, in any appalling environment.

**Flak Armour**

What the Guardsman wears is a multi-layer heat-resistant synthetic fiber vest with shoulder plates and knee guards. It is called armour, but in truth it cannot stop a bolter round — a Space Marine's bolt will detonate it along with the man inside. Flak armour is designed to stop shrapnel, ricochets, and xenos claws and fangs. Against an Ork choppa's horizontal slash, flak armour turns "split in half" into "a few broken ribs and you can still fire one more shot." Its greatest advantage is weight: a Guardsman in flak armour can march eighteen hours continuously. A significant downside: the moment the enemy sees you wearing flak armour they know you are a basic grunt — lowest priority, but also the easiest to kill with a single lasgun shot.

### 2. Special Weapons

An Astra Militarum infantry squad typically carries one special weapons trooper with equipment considerably more violent than a lasgun.

**Hellgun**

The high-energy variant of the lasgun. Visually distinguished by a thick power cable running from the receiver to a backpack power unit — a micro-fusion cell. The hellgun's laser penetration far exceeds the standard lasgun and can cut through Space Marine ceramite plate — making it the standard armament of the Tempestus Scions, the elite of the Astra Militarum. Downsides: power cells drain quickly, the backpack is heavy, and failure rates are high. A Storm Trooper running across the battlefield with a hellgun is, in essence, carrying a reactor prone to short-circuiting while wearing energised armour.

**Plasma Gun**

One of the most common high-energy weapons in the Imperium of Man — and also one of the most capable of killing its own user. The plasma gun extracts plasma from a miniature hydrogen energy cell and expels it at extreme temperature and kinetic force. Any target struck by a plasma bolt — whether armored vehicle, Space Marine, or Tyranid heavy bioform — is flash-melted into semi-liquid carbonized slag. The cost: the plasma gun's cooling system barely exists. After a certain number of sustained shots (the exact number depends on your luck that day), the weapon overheats. The only choice is to drop it or embrace it and become a blue fireball together. Astra Militarum doctrine sets the training standard for plasma gunners as: before the gun explodes, trade at least three enemy lives. Any plasma gunner who meets this metric passes.

**Meltagun**

The meltagun is the Imperium's ultimate man-portable anti-armor solution. Its operating principle is to heat the molecules of the target area to fission temperature — no explosion, no penetration, but turning metal into a flowing pool of superheated liquid. Effective range is extremely short (within a few dozen meters), but at that range the meltagun can slag Terminator armour, the frontal plate of a Chimera transport, or the welded iron slab of an Ork tank. Astra Militarum tactics involve delivering the meltagunner to the enemy — either through infiltration or by having a hundred men with lasguns take the bullets in front of him. If the meltagunner lives long enough to fire, the mission is accomplished. Whether he can get back out is not in the operational plan.

**Flamer**

The word "purge" in Imperial military culture is almost synonymous with the flamer. A flamer projects a stream of burning promethium fuel — gelatinous, adhesive, sticking to flesh and burning down to the bone. Short range, wide area coverage, extremely effective against massed light-armor targets (cultists, Ork boyz, Tyranid swarms), and with strong psychological deterrence — humans fear being burned alive, and xenos do too. Astra Militarum flamer troopers are positioned at the front of an assault wave, irreplaceable in trench-clearing and hive-city close combat. A flamer requires no precise aim — point in the general direction and pull the trigger. This aligns well with Astra Militarum soldier-training principles: "Don't think too hard. Shoot fire at the enemy."

### 3. Heavy Weapons

Each Astra Militarum infantry platoon incorporates a heavy weapons squad, operated by a two-man team — one carries the weapon, the other hauls ammunition.

**Heavy Bolter**

An upscaled bolter weapon, larger caliber, higher rate of fire. The heavy bolter vomits bolt-shells in unbroken streams, purpose-built for suppressing massed infantry and light armor. The Astra Militarum requires a tripod or vehicle mount to operate it — the recoil from hand-held firing would tear a shoulder from its socket. In defensive combat, a properly emplaced heavy bolter amounts to an impassable wall of purgatory.

**Autocannon**

A large-caliber full-automatic cannon, firing high-explosive or armor-piercing shells, each casing thicker than an adult's wrist. The autocannon's target spectrum is broad: armored vehicles, aircraft, flying Tyranid organisms, Chaos Rhino transports — anything that requires large chunks of metal being hammered into it without pause. High rate of fire, simple maintenance. Regarded by many Astra Militarum heavy weapons teams as the best alternative to the heavy bolter — shells are, after all, cheaper than bolt rounds, and the Imperium needs to save money.

**Lascannon**

The titanic variant of the lasgun. An extremely thick high-energy laser beam capable of piercing the frontal armor of most main battle tanks at hundreds of meters. A lascannon requires several seconds of charge per shot; firing produces a blinding flash and a heat-shimmer wave across the ground. If a lascannon punches through the crew compartment of an enemy tank, the probability of anyone inside walking out is not a matter of concern to anyone. The lascannon's logistical advantage is no physical ammunition — charge only — but its battery unit is heavy enough to require two men to carry.

**Mortar**

The Astra Militarum's indirect-fire weapon. Small, portable, broken into three pieces that one or two men can carry across mountain terrain. Fires high-explosive shells, designed to hit targets hiding behind cover. The mortar is not a killer — single-shell kill radius is small — but it is the "cover talisman" that guides the infantry forward. A volley of mortar shells slamming onto the enemy position buys the few dozen seconds the enemy can't raise their heads — the seconds in which Astra Militarum infantry closes from thirty meters to point-blank range.

**Missile Launcher**

The Astra Militarum's standard shoulder-launched missile system, firing fragmentation or krak warheads. Capable of engaging Ork bombers in the sky or Chaos Rhinos on the ground. No guidance system — relying entirely on the gunner's naked eye. An Astra Militarum missile operator must master a non-scientific art known as "estimating the right lead": guess where a high-speed target will be one second from now, and fire a shot at that empty patch of air.

### 4. Vehicle Overview

Imperial vehicle manufacturing logic is always: thick armor, big engine, all precision components mounted behind panels you can unbolt with bare hands. What follows is not technical specifications, but what each vehicle does on the battlefield.

**Chimera Armoured Transport**

An iron box carrying twelve men plus a fixed heavy weapon. The Chimera is the Astra Militarum's legs — without it, Guardsmen infantry walking from one end of the line to the other would need five days of rations and endure three Ork ambushes. The Chimera's armor stops small arms and shrapnel, but against heavy fire it is an iron coffin. The upside: it's cheap. One Leman Russ buys a dozen Chimeras.

**Leman Russ Battle Tank**

The Imperium's T-34 — crude, numerous, everywhere. The Leman Russ is designed for one purpose: fire its gun in any environment, and kill enough enemies before being killed itself. The main gun is a battle cannon — a large-caliber, low-velocity cannon firing high-explosive shells capable of blowing an Ork battlewagon into the sky. Side sponsons mount heavy bolters or plasma cannons. The Leman Russ is not fast, not agile, not advanced — but it can take multiple direct hits and keep firing. On any battlefield with three or more Leman Russ tanks advancing in line formation, an Astra Militarum commander feels the battle can still be fought.

**Baneblade Super-Heavy Tank**

A moving fortress. A Baneblade is thirteen meters long, six meters tall, with a crew of ten. Its main gun is a mega-battle cannon large enough to turn an entire enemy platoon into debris; secondary armament includes heavy bolter turrets, lascannons, and autocannon arrays. Where it rolls, no infantry clean-up is needed. Baneblades are manufactured only on a handful of Mechanicus forge worlds. Each one has a name and is venerated as a god-machine — its machine-spirit is not merely a set of circuits but an ancient, arrogant will to war. In an offensive, the appearance of a Baneblade is the Imperium saying: this battlefield, I'm taking seriously.

**Basilisk Self-Propelled Artillery**

A long-range artillery piece mounted on a Chimera chassis. The Basilisk's duty is not engaging the enemy directly but hammering shells down from positions kilometers behind the front line. The sound of shelling is the lullaby of the Astra Militarum soldier — in the days before an offensive begins, Basilisks pump shells onto the enemy's head without pause. Its shells carry a shrieking sound that penetrates the soul; everyone who has heard it — Imperial soldier and enemy alike — will never forget it for the rest of their lives.

**Valkyrie Assault Carrier**

The Astra Militarum's standard air-assault transport. Capable of carrying a squad of infantry vertically through the chaotic airspace of a battlefield and deploying them at nap-of-the-earth altitude. Valkyries mount rocket pods and heavy bolters on both flanks, capable of strafing enemy positions while dropping their troops. It is the Astra Militarum's sole rapid-response tool on the battlefield — without the Valkyrie, all Astra Militarum movement is made by two human legs walking.

## II. Space Marine Wargear

Space Marine (Adeptus Astartes) wargear operates on a completely different order of magnitude from that of the Astra Militarum. Their weapons are not designed for ordinary humans to use — the recoil, weight, and operational complexity are all engineered for transhuman bodies alone. Space Marines do not "draw" equipment; their weapons are relics bound to their own gene-lineage, maintained and blessed by the Chapter's Techmarines for centuries at a stretch.

### 1. The Bolter Weapon Family

The bolter is the most iconic weapon of the Space Marines and the symbol of the Adeptus Astartes itself.

**The Bolt**

To understand the bolter, first understand the bolt itself. A bolt is not an ordinary bullet. It is a .75 caliber (approximately 1.9 cm) rocket-propelled, high-explosive, armor-piercing round. Upon firing, a small initial charge propels the bolt clear of the barrel to prevent rocket back-blast from burning the firer. Once clear, the built-in solid-fuel rocket ignites, accelerating the round to supersonic speeds. The tip is a hardened penetrator (adamantium or diamantine), which drills into the target on impact; a delayed fuse detonates a millisecond after penetration, scattering shrapnel and superheated gas throughout the target's body.

In other words: when a bolt enters a human body, it does not simply make a hole. It explodes you from the inside. Hit in the torso — half your torso vanishes. Hit in a limb — the arm flies off, shoulder with it. Hit an ordinary human in the head — there is nothing worth describing. No flak armour can withstand a bolter round — including the Astra Militarum's own.

**Bolt Pistol**

The Space Marine's sidearm and officer's weapon. Same killing power as the bolter, with reduced magazine capacity and range. The vast majority of Astartes officers engage with a bolt pistol paired with a melee weapon. Commissars also use small-caliber bolt pistols — the most visible mark distinguishing them from ordinary soldiers.

**Bolter**

The standard-issue rifle of the Space Marines, firing .75 caliber bolts. In full-automatic mode it can spit four to five rounds per second, recoil sufficient to slam an ordinary man straight to the ground — a Space Marine in power armour feels only a familiar push. The bolter's trajectory is a bright arc of rocket flare; in night combat it looks like clusters of meteors shooting from the hands of the Astartes. Any unit that encounters an Astartes bolter salvo has only one reaction: find cover. If the cover isn't thick enough — pray.

**Heavy Bolter**

As mentioned earlier, the Astra Militarum requires a tripod to use this. The Space Marine holds it in his hands and fires it like an automatic rifle. Larger-caliber bolts against light armored vehicles and massed infantry — a single Astartes heavy bolter gunner can single-handedly seal an entire street. Belt-fed, continuously fed from a backpack ammunition canister.

**Storm Bolter**

Two bolters combined into one, spitting double the bolt-shells at even higher speed. The storm bolter is the standard sidearm of Terminators — the Terminator's power fist integrates sensors so the hand does not need to pull a trigger; neural signals fire the weapon directly. The storm bolter's fire density is sufficient to turn an entire room, within seconds, into a chimney of shredded meat.

### 2. The Power Weapon Family

The core technology of power weapons is the built-in **disruption field** — a high-frequency energy sheath overlaid on the blade or striking surface, capable of severing matter at the molecular level. A power weapon cuts through steel plate as an ordinary man cuts through butter.

**Power Sword**

The most common close-combat weapon for Space Marine officers, and a mark of status. The blade itself may be ceramite or adamantium, but no one cares about the blade's base material — once energized, the disruption field envelops the entire edge, emitting an aura that ranges from blue to gold. One swing can cleave through power-armour chestplate, Ork iron-hide, Tyranid carapace. Maintaining a power sword requires specific benediction rituals; every Chapter has its own methods for offering prayers to the weapon's machine-spirit.

**Power Fist**

An oversized gauntlet covered in a disruption field, a single blow capable of punching through tank armor. The power fist's method of use is "hammer." The door the Astra Militarum cannot open, a power fist punches a crater through. A Chaos Space Marine swings a chain-axe; a power fist meets the axe-head head-on and crushes it. Terminators and officers typically carry power fists as a close-combat option. Some veterans have turned each of the ten fingers on their gauntlet into a trophy rack — every digit threaded through the skull of a different defeated enemy.

**Power Axe, Power Maul, and Thunder Hammer**

The power axe is slower than the power sword but strikes with greater weight, designed specifically for heavily armored targets. The power maul attacks through concussive force — rather than cutting the enemy, it shatters bones and organs inside the body. The Adeptus Custodes prefer the power maul for handling the grotesque results of Warp mutation.

The thunder hammer is the ultimate evolution of the power maul: at the instant the head strikes a target, it releases stored impact energy in a detonation-grade shockwave. A thunder hammer killing blow does not leave a wound — the Chaos Space Marine struck by it is hurled through the air like he was hit by a high-speed train, all bones reduced to splinters. The massive release of force is accompanied by a thunderous roar — hence the name.

**Lightning Claw**

Four power-blades mounted on the outside of the arm, shaped like the talons of a great beast. The lightning claw is designed for rapid cutting and rending — four disruption-field blades slicing into a target simultaneously, shattering armor from multiple vectors. Khorne is especially fond of the blood-spray effect of lightning claws — it is said that the Lord of Battle, upon witnessing a Space Marine tear open enemies with lightning claws, will emit a roar of approval. This approval is not considered an honor by the Emperor-worshipping Space Marines, so they generally do not mention it.

### 3. Terminator Armour (Tactical Dreadnought Armour)

**Terminator Armour**, formally designated Tactical Dreadnought Armor, represents the apex of Imperial individual armor technology. A single suit of Terminator armour weighs approximately four hundred kilograms, constructed from composite layers of ceramite, an adamantium skeleton, and an integrated force-field generator. It is not armor a soldier wears — it is a man-sized tank a soldier walks into. A Space Marine in Terminator armour can survive a direct plasma gun hit, walk through irradiated zones, and be exposed to hard vacuum when a ship's deck is breached.

Terminator armour is not standard equipment. In the vast majority of Astartes Chapters, only the First Company veterans are entitled to wear it — these veterans are formed into Terminator Squads, the Chapter's ultimate reserve of combat power. The manufacture of Terminator armour is also extraordinarily difficult: it is not a factory-line product but hand-crafted at a glacial pace on specific Mechanicus forge worlds. A single suit of Terminator armour, from mold-casting to final benediction, can take a century. Thus every Chapter has a hard cap on the number of Terminator suits it possesses — not by regulation, but by physical impossibility of production.

Three primary patterns of Terminator armour exist:

- **Indomitus Pattern**: The most commonly seen, identifiable by curved pauldrons and heavy chestplate. This is the standard Terminator armour of most Chapters in the M41 era.
- **Cataphractii Pattern**: An early pattern from the Horus Heresy era — heavier, slower, with even greater protection, its pauldrons large enough to fully enclose the sides of the helmet. A minority of Chapters still possess these relics, repaired after every battle by weeping Techmarines.
- **Tartaros Pattern**: A later refinement of the Cataphractii, dramatically improving mobility while maintaining protection. Its rarity falls between the Indomitus and Cataphractii patterns.

All Terminator armour incorporates a **teleport beacon**, allowing orbital vessels to deliver the wearer directly into battle — this is the Terminator's most terrifying tactical application: a ship opens a teleportation channel over the target zone, and an entire Terminator squad materializes in the heart of the enemy's defensive position.

The standard armament of a Terminator is a storm bolter or heavy flamer, paired with a power fist. Terminators carrying heavy flamers can turn an entire corridor into a pyre, with overwhelming extermination effect against Tyranid swarms and Ork charges.

### 4. Dreadnoughts

The Dreadnought is one of the Imperium's cruelest "honors."

When a Space Marine is so grievously wounded that he can no longer fight within power armour — limbs gone, internal organs charred, half his body vaporized by a plasma cannon — the Chapter's Apothecaries may determine he still holds value for continued service. The Space Marine is then extracted from his wreckage and interred within the amniotic life-support sarcophagus of a Dreadnought chassis, his nervous system directly linked to the walker's weapon arms and sensor arrays. To be transformed after death into a walking war machine is considered the supreme honor. What the honor glosses over: every time this "Ancient" is awakened from his long dream, he must, in the moment of consciousness interface, relive the full agony of his own death. And he knows this awakening is very likely his last — next time he sleeps, no one may bother to wake him again.

Standard Dreadnought armament includes heavy lascannons, a power fist (with built-in bolter), missile launchers, and more. A single Dreadnought's firepower can independently engage light-to-medium tanks; in close combat it can lift an Ork battlewagon and smash it down on a pile of other Orks. The Dreadnought's pilots — those ancients who have endured a thousand, three thousand years of agony — are eccentric, irascible, and profound. Their shouts, broadcast from external vox-casters, sound like echoes from a deep stone tomb. The Chapter's younger generations fear the old monsters inside the Dreadnoughts. But every time they march to war, they must go and beg: Ancient One, it's time to wake up.

## III. Adeptus Custodes Wargear

The wargear of the Custodes surpasses even Astartes standards. Every Custodian's weapons and armor are individually crafted for him by the finest artisans of the Imperium, closer to art than to military manufacture. What follows is a brief catalog. **For the full construction, organization, and culture of the Custodes, see Book 08 (*The Adeptus Custodes and Sisters of Silence*),** and will not be expanded upon here.

**Guardian Spear**: The standard armament of the Custodes. Externally, a power spear with an integrated disruption field; internally, a barrel fires bolts — two weapons in one. A Custodian can simultaneously thrust with the spearhead and unleash a bolt salvo from the integral barrel, shredding the target from the front.

**Sentinel Blade**: A combat shield with a bolt pistol barrel integrated into its inner face. Wielding the Sentinel Blade allows the Custodian to block and fire simultaneously — the shield absorbs the heretic's melta attack while the barrel pours bolts from behind it. This is the classic stance of Custodes in the defense of the Imperial Palace's corridors.

**Allarus Terminator Armour**: Custodes-specific Terminator plate, more advanced than any Astartes pattern — its thickness, force fields, and machine-spirit intelligence all exceed by a full tier. Custodes Terminators can independently teleport into battle to execute precision strikes.

## IV. Rare Imperial Weapons and Relics

Some weapons fall outside standard issue. They are relics of the Dark Age of Technology, or astronomically expensive products locked away by the Mechanicus. Their numbers are tiny — but a single appearance on the battlefield can change the course of a war.

**Nemesis Force Weapon**

The dedicated armament of the Grey Knights, the daemon-hunting specialist Chapter of the Adeptus Astartes. Each Nemesis Force Weapon is a psychic focus — the disruption field on its blade is driven by the wielder's own psychic power. The stronger the wielder's mind, the sharper the blade and the greater the psychic shockwave it delivers. Against Warp entities, the Nemesis Force Weapon is one of the few instruments capable of severing spirit from the material realm.

**Conversion Beamer**

A remnant of the Dark Age of Technology, an antique the present-day Imperium can no longer replicate. The conversion beamer fires an energy beam that transmutes the atomic structure of matter along its path directly into antimatter — it does not burn, does not explode, but erases the target from the laws of physical existence. The further the range, the greater the destructive effect, because the beam has more matter to participate in the conversion reaction as it traverses space. These were already scarce in the 30K era; in 40K they are legendary finds only an archaeologist could hope to unearth.

**Volcano Cannon**

A weapon mounted on Emperor-class Titans — a titanic laser cannon. The Volcano Cannon is powerful enough that, even through void shields, it can melt an entire defended city to slag. Its name is not metaphor: after firing, the ground beneath the muzzle vitrifies from thermal radiation, looking from afar as though volcanic lava has flowed across it. The Astra Militarum and Space Marines have no means of fielding a Volcano Cannon — it belongs to the Titan Legions. A weapon of the gods.

**Vortex Grenade**

A rare and extraordinarily dangerous single-man grenade. Upon detonation it does not explode in realspace — it tears the boundary between the material universe and the Warp, creating a localized micro-Warp rift. The rift pulls everything within its radius — people, armored vehicles, entire walls, the air itself — into the other dimension. No one has ever returned from a vortex rift, and no one knows what lies on the other side. The soldier who uses a vortex grenade and the target are, to an equal degree, "presumed dead."

**Rad Weapons and Phosphex Weapons**

Rad weapons burn living tissue with invisible gamma radiation, destroying all organic matter within without damaging armor — specifically designed against enemies like the Tyranids: thick-skinned, cheaply lived. Phosphex weapons represent the extreme of incendiary technology: a phosphex round burns for a prolonged duration after impact, white-hot, the flame impervious to sand or water. The Imperium rarely deploys these two weapon classes, because the environmental and ethical consequences, even by Imperial standards, fall under the category of "too ugly." But when a war deteriorates far enough, "ugly" becomes synonymous with "effective."

## V. Summary

The essence of Imperial wargear can be expressed in twelve words:

**Reliability over advancement. Quantity over quality. Works for ten thousand years — better than innovating once.**

The lasgun is not the most powerful infantry weapon, but a hundred lasgunners firing simultaneously will make any species in the galaxy duck. The Leman Russ is not the most advanced tank, but the Imperium can put ten thousand of them on the battlefield at the same time. The bolter is the honor of the Space Marine; the lasgun is the forever of the Guardsman. Can't manufacture new Terminator armour? Then polish the old suit and use it for another five thousand years. The conversion beamer cannot be replicated? Then lock the remaining handful in a Mechanicus vault with six hundred verification protocols, only to be used on the day even the Emperor himself can no longer bear to watch.

The Imperium's philosophy of war does not pursue a single "best weapon." It pursues having "enough weapons, reliable enough" that war will never stop. In the darkness of the 41st Millennium, human civilization does not know what victory looks like. It knows only one thing: so long as a single lasgun is still firing, the Imperium is still alive.
