# 06 · Tyranids

## Overview

Every power in the galaxy — the Imperium of Man, the Aeldari Craftworlds, the Necron Dynasties, the warbands of Chaos — shares at least one trait: they all came from this universe, whether they admit it or not. The Tyranids alone do not. They come from the void beyond the galaxy, belonging to none of this realm's lineages of life, following none of its logics of war. They seize no territory, negotiate no treaties, build no temples, raise no monuments — they simply eat. Before them, the blood-soaked millennia of human and Ork, the eternal war of Chaos and Imperium, the aeons-old grudges of Aeldari and Necron — all of it is merely different dishes on the same buffet.

If the Warhammer 40K galaxy is an epic of "who shall rule," the Tyranids are the thing that swallows the entire book in one gulp. They do not participate in the game; they are the termination condition of the game.

## Origin

As to where the Tyranids truly come from, the Ordo Xenos of the Inquisition has studied the question for millennia. The answer still amounts to eight words: beyond the galaxy, in the deep void. The first unambiguous signal was recorded at Tyran — a monitoring station world on the Imperial frontier. When interstellar communication suddenly ceased and the investigation fleet arrived, they found the entire planet devoid of any sign of life. Atmosphere, oceans, soil, even the planetary biosphere itself — all gone. Tyran had become a bare stone sphere.

This was humanity's first glimpse of what a "world eaten by the Tyranids" looked like. The Imperium named them after that dead world: Tyranids. But Tyran was only the messenger. The signal originated somewhere beyond the galaxy, in the interstellar void where humanity had never observed any life.

Regarding their true origin, only two plausible theories exist. The first is the "flight theory": the Tyranids may have already devoured countless galaxies in the void beyond, but something even more terrifying — a greater predator, some cosmic-scale event — is driving them forward. They are not attacking the galaxy. They are fleeing. The second is connected to Szarekh, the Silent King. During his self-imposed exile in the void beyond the galaxy, this last ruler of the Necrons came face to face with the main body of the Tyranid swarm. It was this encounter that convinced him the Tyranids are the greatest threat facing the entire galaxy, compelling him to break tens of millions of years of silence and return to awaken the Dynasties. If something can make the undead king of a robot race feel terror, the sheer mass of the Tyranids speaks for itself.

Whichever theory is correct, the fact remains: the Tyranids have arrived, and they are endless.

## Deeds

### Biology and the Hive Mind

The Tyranids are not, in their essence, an army, a species, or a civilisation. They are a single organic whole. The Hive Mind is the one consciousness shared by every Tyranid organism. An individual Tyranid bioform has no concept of "I." When a Gaunt charges into a storm of gunfire and is torn to shreds, it feels no fear, no anger, no thought — it is simply a cell the Hive Mind is currently moving. Being killed does not matter; biomass can be reclaimed, genetic memory is never lost.

The Hive Mind is the most terrifying, and least comprehensible, aspect of the Tyranids. It is not a "brain" located somewhere; it is a trans-stellar collective consciousness field. The more Tyranids there are, the stronger the signal, and the surrounding Warp becomes blanketed by the Shadow in the Warp. Within this shadow, the light of the Astronomican dims, psychic communications are crushed into an endless shriek of madness, and psykers either suffer mental collapse or spontaneously combust. For the Imperium — which depends on the Astronomican for navigation and psykers for communication — this means every Tyranid invasion is simultaneously an information war. Before you even know the enemy has arrived, your communications, navigation, and faster-than-light transmissions have already been neutralised.

A Hive Fleet is the form in which the Tyranids move through space — millions of organic bioships form living clouds that advance slowly but inexorably between star systems. The Tyranids possess a colossal organism called the Narvhal, which can tear open gravity wells at the edge of a system and drag an entire Hive Fleet into the low orbit of its target world in one jump.

Once arrived, the invasion unfolds systematically. First, capillary towers and spore chimneys carpet the land, transforming the biosphere into a Tyranid digestion pool. Genestealers, having long since infiltrated the target world and spawned Genestealer Cults underground, now rise from within to sever the planet's defences. Then come the Norn-Queens, endlessly birthing new bioforms; the Hive Tyrants, directing the front lines; the Swarmlord — the only individual the Hive Mind can recall from death — iterating and evolving after each defeat, returning more lethal the next time. In the final phase, every gram of organic matter is absorbed — humans, animals, trees, micro-organisms, oceans, atmosphere — drawn through capillary towers into the Hive Fleet. When the fleet departs, what remains is a planet stripped to its core, devoid of even a single bacterium. The Hive Mind feeds the genetic information it has gained back into the entire swarm; the next wave of Tyranids will carry new resistances adapted precisely to the weapons that stopped them last time.

Before the Tyranids, the Imperium's foundational weakness is laid bare. The Imperium's logic of war is to hold positions, expend resources, and wait for reinforcements. The Tyranids' logic of war is: you are the resources.

### The Tyrannic Wars

The Imperium's most famous engagements with the Tyranids are concentrated in the mid-to-late 41st Millennium, marked by the massive incursions of three Hive Fleets.

The First Tyrannic War — Hive Fleet Behemoth. Behemoth attacked head-on, driving straight for the Ultramarines' homeworld of Macragge in mid-M41. The Ultramarines, with their Primarch asleep and their fleet dispersed, shattered the swarm's synapse creatures at a brutal cost, causing the remaining bioforms to lose cohesion and scatter. After this war, the Imperium finally understood what it was facing and elevated the Tyranids to the highest priority for xenos Exterminatus.

The Second Tyrannic War — Hive Fleet Kraken. Kraken did not walk through the front door. It struck from beneath the galactic plane in late M41, attacking across multiple systems simultaneously, making it impossible for the Imperium to concentrate its forces. Kraken tore through Craftworld Iyanden, inflicting the most devastating wound in Aeldari history. Ultimately, the Imperium and the Aeldari — enemies of a thousand years — were forced into an alliance just to halt Kraken's main thrust.

The Third Tyrannic War — Hive Fleet Leviathan. Leviathan's mass exceeded the first two Hive Fleets combined. It struck at the end of M41, punching up directly from beneath the galactic disc. One of its primary targets was Baal, the homeworld of the Blood Angels. The Defence of Baal stands as the largest recorded siege of a single planet by the Tyranids — Commander Dante, at over 1,500 years of age, led the defence for sixty days of relentless assault, before Guilliman finally arrived with the Indomitus Crusade fleet to break the siege.

Since then, Hive Fleets Jormungandr, Gorgon, and others have followed, along with the ongoing Fourth Tyrannic War. The war has never stopped.

## Fate & Present Status

Every veteran who has fought in the Tyrannic Wars knows the truth: what has arrived are the scouts. Behemoth, Kraken, Leviathan — extrapolating from their overall scale, they represent only the tendrils of the true Tyranid swarm. The main body is still out there, in the darkness known only as the Void.

Within the Inquisition, there is a grim calculation: the total biomass lost from a fully-consumed world is beyond comparison to even a century of conventional war. The Tyranids are not fighting a war of attrition; they are conducting extinction. If the known Hive Fleets really are only the vanguard tendrils, then when the main body arrives, the question will not be whether we can win. The question will be: how many planets will be left? The Necrons have already understood this. The Silent King Szarekh has returned from the void precisely to mobilise all Necrons against the swarm.

## Verdict

The Imperium of Man calls the Tyranids "the xenos evil," "the plague of the galaxy," "the ultimate enemy." But strip away the moral rhetoric and one fact stands clear: the Tyranids are not evil. They are simply hungry.

Evil requires choice. Khorne chooses violence. Tzeentch chooses manipulation. They are beings with the capacity to choose. The Imperium's "abhor the xenos" is also a choice. But the Tyranids have never "chosen" anything. The Hive Mind is hunger itself, and nothing more.

They attach no ideology to their wars — no "For the Emperor," no "Blood for the Blood God." They found no church. They write no history. Someone in the Inquisition once wrote: "They are not an enemy. An enemy can be dealt with by winning. They are a law of nature. A law of nature can only be accepted."

You cannot negotiate with the Tyranids. You cannot declare a truce. You cannot deceive them. You can only live, or be digested.

And what truly chills the blood is not the known Hive Fleets, but the main body the Hive Mind has yet to reveal. If these tendrils have already nearly destroyed half the Imperium, nearly annihilated the Aeldari's most ancient Craftworld, and forced the last Phaeron of the Necrons to hurry back in alarm — then when the true "trunk" of the Tyranids arrives, what will the galaxy have to stop it? That is a question the historians of the 71st Millennium may well know the answer to. Assuming anyone is still alive.
