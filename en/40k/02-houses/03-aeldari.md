# 03 · Aeldari

## Overview

The Aeldari — whom humanity calls the Eldar — are among the most ancient intelligent civilisations in the galaxy. When humanity was still apelike, the Aeldari empire already spanned the stars, its attainments in technology, art, and psychic mastery reaching heights scarcely imaginable. Today, this race has shrunk to scattered, fleeing remnants. The depravity they themselves created gave birth to Slaanesh, the fourth Chaos God, who consumed the entire Aeldari empire into the Warp, leaving only fragments of survivors clinging to life in the corners of the galaxy. A race that ruled the galaxy for sixty million years now has one overriding wish: not to be devoured by its own god.

## Origin

The origins of the Aeldari trace back to the War in Heaven — the most ancient conflict in the galaxy. The Old Ones, in their war against the C'tan and their Necrontyr servants, created the Aeldari as a psychic weapon-race. The Old Ones granted them highly developed psychic gifts, extraordinary intelligence, and vastly extended lifespans. When the War in Heaven ended and the Old Ones vanished from the galaxy, the Aeldari inherited the greater part of their legacy.

For tens of millions of years thereafter, Aeldari civilisation flourished to an unimaginable extreme. Their technology did not depend on machinery, but fused psychic power with artistry — they shaped matter with will and built cities with pure thought. The star-region containing their homeworld and core territories was sculpted into an eternal paradise.

The Aeldari Pantheon formed the spiritual backbone of their civilisation — Kaela Mensha Khaine, the war-god; Isha, the goddess of life; Cegorach, the Laughing God; and others. With the gods' protection and lifespans that verged on immortality, death in the golden age barely existed. Upon death, an Aeldari soul automatically returned to the Warp and was reborn. It was a closed loop.

It was precisely within this environment — no external threats, no pressure to survive — that the Aeldari walked a path no other civilisation had ever taken. They were not conquered by enemies; they were devoured by their own desires.

## Deeds

### The Fall

The Aeldari were immortal and innately, exquisitely sensitive to sensation. This proved an irreversible evolutionary trajectory. Across millions of years of unchallenged peace and prosperity, Aeldari society fragmented into pleasure-cults that pursued ever-more-extreme sensory experiences. Their art became blood-spectacle. Their rituals became sadistic feasts beyond the imagination of the universe's darkest beings. This pursuit of extremes was amplified to an extraordinarily dangerous threshold.

The collective soul of a civilisation leaves an imprint in the Warp. The Aeldari's hedonism — all the extremes of violence, excess, and perfectionism — coalesced in the Warp into a gradually-forming entity. At first it was merely an unconscious vortex, but as Aeldari depravity deepened, it began to stir, to hunger, to crave more.

By the 30th Millennium, the critical point was breached. The entity formed from millennia of Aeldari sin fully awoke — Slaanesh, the fourth Chaos God, god of desire and excess and agony. The moment of Slaanesh's birth triggered a Warp-cataclysm of staggering magnitude. The core systems of the Aeldari empire were ripped into the Warp, forming an eternal vortex — what is now the Eye of Terror.

The Aeldari civilisation of tens of millions of years was annihilated in an instant. Trillions of souls were devoured by Slaanesh in a single gulp. The gods were slaughtered or imprisoned — Khaine's body was shattered and scattered across the galaxy; Isha was abducted by Nurgle and imprisoned within his garden; almost all other deities were consumed by Slaanesh. The sole survivor was Cegorach the Laughing God, who escaped into the depths of the Webway through trickery and laughter.

From that moment, the Aeldari lost the cycle of rebirth. Every Aeldari soul, upon death, is devoured by Slaanesh — unless a way can be found to evade her. This curse is inscribed upon every survivor.

### The Craftworld Aeldari (Asuryani)

Not all Aeldari participated in the Fall. A portion foresaw the catastrophe and boarded the Craftworlds — self-sustaining, planet-sized mobile cities — fleeing the core systems before the collapse. They drift through the galaxy to this day.

Craftworld life is structured around the Paths. Every adult Aeldari chooses a specific Path — the Path of the Warrior, the Path of the Artist, the Path of the Seer — focusing the mind on a single discipline to contain the dangerously extreme emotions that come naturally to their species. When one Path is completed, they switch to another, thus avoiding being consumed by a single obsession.

When a Craftworld Aeldari dies, their soul is captured in a Spirit Stone, which is then interred within the Infinity Circuit of their Craftworld. The Infinity Circuit is a collective pool of consciousness from countless dead, protecting their souls from Slaanesh while accruing wisdom across tens of thousands of years.

The major Craftworlds include: Biel-Tan, whose militant inhabitants dream of rebuilding the empire; Ulthwé, perched on the edge of the Eye of Terror, renowned for its Farseers; Iyanden, which after being besieged by the Tyranids has become a ghost-ship of the dead; Saim-Hann, preserving a wild, primal rider-culture; and Alaitoc, known for its rigid conservatism.

### The Drukhari (Dark Eldar)

If the Craftworld Aeldari chose self-discipline to survive, the Drukhari chose a different path: keep enjoying themselves.

The Drukhari never renounced hedonism. They retreated into the Webway — the ancient Aeldari-built network of Warp-tunnels — and built within it the dark city of Commorragh. Slaanesh siphons their souls with every passing moment. The Drukhari's solution is to feed Slaanesh with the suffering of others — so long as they keep tormenting others, Slaanesh will defer consuming them.

Drukhari society is a thoroughgoing culture of sadism — pleasure derives from inflicting pain, and power derives from being more cruel than the next. Periodically, they launch raiding fleets from the Webway, sweeping through human and xenos colonies and hauling captives back to Commorragh.

### The Harlequins

The Harlequins are the most enigmatic of the Aeldari. They serve the sole surviving Aeldari deity — Cegorach the Laughing God. The Harlequins travel through the Webway and the galaxy in troupes, conveying hidden messages to all sides through battle-dances and theatrical performances. They owe allegiance to no Craftworld, recognise no factional boundaries — in the Harlequins' eyes, all the Aeldari's divisions are merely different acts of the same tragedy.

The Harlequins possess the deepest secrets of the Webway and the lost knowledge of the Aeldari. They are renowned for their extraordinary speed and spectacular combat-dances. Whenever the Aeldari factions need a messenger to communicate, it is always the Harlequins who arrive.

### The Ynnari

The Ynnari are the youngest and most radical Aeldari faction. They worship Ynnead — the Aeldari god of the dead. According to ancient prophecy, when all the Aeldari's Spirit Stones are gathered and unified within the Infinity Circuit, Ynnead will awaken from the collective soul and use the power of death to kill Slaanesh outright, liberating the Aeldari.

The Ynnari rose to prominence in the late 41st Millennium. The prophet Yvraine, after undergoing multiple near-death experiences, became the emissary of Ynnead. She and the avatar of Ynnead — the Yncarne — travel the galaxy rallying more and more Aeldari to their cause. The Ynnari are one of the few factions to cooperate on a limited basis with the Imperium of Man — it was Yvraine who, at the end of M41, assisted in the resurrection of Roboute Guilliman, an act that sparked enormous controversy among both the Imperium and the Aeldari.

## Fate & Present Status

In the 42nd Millennium, the Aeldari still teeter on the brink of extinction. The civilisation that once spanned the galaxy has devolved into Craftworlds, Webway enclaves, and a handful of Maiden Worlds. The Drukhari continue to trade slaughter for a few more days of borrowed existence. The Craftworld Aeldari dwindle upon their ancient ships. The Ynnari still search for the final path to awaken Ynnead.

The Aeldari's role in the galaxy is a tragic one. They possess ancient technology and psychic mastery capable of matching any power, along with profound understanding of the Warp and the Webway — yet all of it has been transmuted into a curse by their own fall. Other races regard them either as pirates and predators or as arrogant schemers. The Aeldari have almost no allies within the Imperium of Man.

## Verdict

The Aeldari are the most quintessentially tragic race in the Warhammer 40K universe.

They were not conquered by an external enemy — they were destroyed by their own nature. The Old Ones gave the Aeldari perfect bodies and psychic power, but overlooked their extreme sensory needs. Perfection led to unrestrained depravity. In the 40K universe, a civilisation without external constraints will ultimately devour itself.

The greater tragedy is that the Aeldari know this with crystalline clarity. They remember the golden age. They know they destroyed everything with their own hands. Every survivor bears the constant draining hunger of Slaanesh. This "lucid agony" is unique in 40K — the Orks do not care what they are; the Imperium is a machine that does not know what it is doing; only the Aeldari know exactly where they went wrong, yet are powerless to turn back.

The emergence of the Ynnari is the sole, faint hope. But whether Ynnead can truly awaken, and whether, once awakened, he can actually defeat Slaanesh — in the pessimistic galaxy of the 42nd Millennium, this can only be considered a dubious fairy tale.
