# 04 · Orks

## Overview

The Orks — Greenskins, as they are commonly called — are the most purely war-driven race in the galaxy. They are not victims caught in a war they did not start, not cannon fodder driven by tyranny, not fanatics enslaved by faith — they fight because they enjoy it. To the Orks, war is not a means to an end, not a pathway to peace; it is what life itself is supposed to be.

Orks are not the product of natural evolution. They are a fungal bio-weapon. Every Ork carries within its body the spores of the entire Orkoid ecosystem. When an Ork dies, its corpse releases spores; the spores take root and spawn a new generation of Orks along with the entire accompanying ecosystem. This means Orks can never be eradicated. You can burn their camps, blow apart their warbosses, drench a planet in disinfectant for three years — but if a single spore survives, a generation or two later the hills are green again.

Their very existence is a form of satire directed at every other race in the galaxy: you compete for power, worship gods, build empires, fear extinction — and the Orks just want a good fight.

## Origin

The origin of the Orks traces back roughly sixty million years to the War in Heaven. That war was fought between the Old Ones and the C'tan along with their Necrontyr allies. The Old Ones, the galaxy's most ancient intelligent race, commanded Webway technology and unparalleled psychic knowledge. Faced with the overwhelming strength of the C'tan, the Old Ones needed a warrior race that could self-replicate infinitely on the battlefield, growing more numerous the more it fought, and would never, ever surrender. So they created the Orks — or more precisely, they created their predecessors: the Krorks.

The Krorks were the ancient prototype of the Orks. According to surviving lore, the Krorks were taller, more intelligent, and better equipped than today's Orks — likely gigantic greenskin warriors clad in power armour. After the War in Heaven ended and the Old Ones were gone, the Krorks degenerated over sixty million years into today's Orks. But what degenerated was not ferocity — it was discipline. Today's Orks still carry all the genetic instincts of the Krorks, including an innate combat sense, linguistic encoding, technological intuition, and hierarchical obedience. Their genetic expression is simply locked inside a more chaotic, more fragmented social form.

The Imperium's official research on Orks is pitifully thin. The Adeptus Mechanicus still cannot figure out why an Ork gun is capable of firing. The Inquisition tends to classify any inquiry into Ork origins as "excessive curiosity." But one point is acknowledged by all sides: Orks are not the primitives you think they are. Their genes carry the design blueprint of the Old Ones — and those were the creations of a race that once defeated the C'tan.

## Deeds

### Biology and the WAAAGH!

Orks are the most bizarre race in the galaxy because their biology and their psychic nature are fundamentally inseparable.

Start with the basics: Orks are a fungal-symbiotic organism. An individual Ork is composed of green skin, muscle, bone, and a fungus network spread throughout its entire body. Their skin is green precisely because of internal fungal chlorophyll-analogues performing photosynthesis — Orks do not survive purely on eating; they can just stand in the sun. Their muscle density far exceeds that of a human, and they can sustain damage that by human physiological standards should have killed them a dozen times over. Orks do not care. Their pain receptors may not even be wired correctly.

When an Ork dies, its remains release millions of spores. These spores grow in the soil into a complete subterranean fungal network: first come the Gretchin (Grots) and Squigs — Gretchin being small slave-labourers and emergency rations, Squigs being symbiotic beasts of varying sizes; then comes the fungal network itself, which conditions the soil; last of all, Ork Boyz emerge. The entire process takes only a few seasons. A planet that has been "cleansed," if spores remain in the soil, will see Ork settlements sprout again decades later. After countless lessons, the Imperium has finally learned: fighting Greenskins cannot rely on conventional military occupation — after battle, the entire planet must undergo long-term surface incineration and fungal isolation. Even then, no one can guarantee they got everything.

Then there is the Orks' most famous psychic phenomenon: the WAAAGH!

WAAAGH! is not a battle-cry, not an army, not a tactical slogan. It is a collective psychic field. When large numbers of Orks gather together, their collective belief generates an overwhelming psychic resonance in the Warp — and that resonance, in turn, produces physical effects in reality. The core mechanism is: what Orks believe becomes more likely to be true.

Red truly does make vehicles go faster — not because red is faster, but because every Ork believes red is the fastest colour, and that collective belief turns into a real acceleration effect within the WAAAGH! field. Similarly, purple things are harder to spot (because you've never seen a purple Ork, have you?), blue gear is luckier, yellow bombs explode louder. Tech-Priests of the Adeptus Mechanicus suffer repeated mental collapse when studying captured Ork weapons, because they open up an Ork gun and find nothing inside but a lump of iron, a few pipes, and a jumble of scrap that could not possibly constitute a firing mechanism — but the gun, when held by an Ork, does indeed kill people.

This is the truth of greenskin technology: it is not technology. It is collective will made physical. The stronger the WAAAGH! — the more Orks participating, the higher their emotional charge — the greater this "will-makes-reality" effect becomes.

WAAAGH! has another function: it caps the power of Ork psykers. Ork psykers are the Weirdboyz. Their power is drawn from WAAAGH! energy, but too much causes their heads to explode — literally. So Weirdboyz in battle are simultaneously the most dangerous weapon and the most unstable bomb.

Lastly: Orks grow bigger the more they fight. This is not hyperbole. An Ork's body grows continuously through combat and victory — adrenaline from battle, the infusion of WAAAGH! energy, and the Ork's own physiology all combine so that a victorious Ork becomes taller, broader, and more terrifying. An Ork on a long winning streak can swell from the size of an ordinary Boy to more than twice the size of a standard Space Marine. This is the basis of Ork social hierarchy: whoever is biggest is boss. No voting process. Just a punch.

### Social Structure

Ork society is simple to the point of elegance. There is no constitution, no parliament, no legal documents, no property registry — whoever can beat everyone else up is in charge.

The basic combat unit is the Warband. A Warband consists of a mob of Ork Boyz and the Nobz who lead them, supplemented by Gretchin as menial labour and Squigs as food and pets. Warbands frequently fight each other — sometimes over territory, sometimes over weapons, sometimes simply because there are no other enemies today, so they might as well fight themselves.

When the boss of one Warband beats the bosses of all the other Warbands, he becomes the Warboss. A Warboss is not appointed — he is fought into existence. A true Warboss must simultaneously possess savage strength, a certain level of instinctive tactical sense, and a voice loud enough to bellow orders that get the Orks for kilometres around frothing with battle-lust.

Occupational division within a Warband is entirely determined by genetics — Orks are born carrying specific gene-templates that decide whether they will fight, fix things, heal things, or go insane while fighting.

The Mekboy is the Ork equivalent of an engineer. Mekboyz have an instinctive feel for machinery rooted in their bones. They require no learning, no blueprints, no mathematics of any kind — give them scrap and they will weld a functioning tank. A Mekboy cannot explain what he built, but hand him a crate of parts and he will assemble a doomsday walker. If the Adeptus Mechanicus permitted itself to feel rage, it might cause every Magos to suffer an inferiority complex before an ordinary Mekboy.

The Painboy is the Ork surgeon-dentist. The Painboy's concept of "medicine" is: attach strange things — nails, iron plates, buzz-saws — to an injured Ork, and he will become stronger. This method is surprisingly effective — not because it makes sense, but because the modified Ork sincerely believes he has been healed and made stronger, and so the WAAAGH! genuinely makes him stronger. A Painboy's most commonly used surgical tools are a large pair of pliers and a chainsaw. The anaesthetic method is a headbutt.

Finally, the Weirdboyz — Ork psykers. They do not actively cultivate psychic power; psychic power pours into them on its own. In a large WAAAGH!, Weirdboyz are forced to absorb colossal amounts of WAAAGH! field energy. If it is not released in time, their head explodes; but if they successfully release it, they can spew green lightning from their mouths and upend an entire line of enemy tanks.

Ork spiritual belief revolves around two twin gods: Gork and Mork. Gork is "cunningly brutal," and Mork is "brutally cunning." Which is which, and what the difference is — no scholar in the entire galaxy has ever figured it out, including the Orks themselves. This does not prevent Orks from shouting both deities' names in their war-cries. They do not need a theological treatise; they just need two names to increase the volume of the roar.

### Famous WAAAGH!

Ork history has witnessed countless WAAAGH!, several of which were so vast they altered the shape of the galaxy.

The Ullanor Empire — At the height of the Great Crusade, the Emperor personally led a massive campaign against a gigantic Ork empire in the Ullanor system. The battle was so immense that the Emperor committed everything: nearly all the loyalist Primarchs and their Legions poured into the fight to bring down the Ork Warboss. It was after this campaign that the Emperor named Horus Warmaster — in a sense, this victory against the Orks indirectly paved the road to the Horus Heresy. After Ullanor was cleansed, the Adeptus Mechanicus spent decades terraforming the planet. Years later it became Armageddon — and that name would echo through many WAAAGH! yet to come.

The War of the Beast (M32) — This was the largest Ork invasion in Imperial history, and one of the moments the Imperium came closest to annihilation. Among the Orks appeared an extraordinarily rare subspecies: the Beast Orks, whose physiques dwarfed even ordinary Warbosses by several multiples, and who could fight a Primarch one-on-one. The entire Ork WAAAGH! fleet directly attacked the Sol System. The Imperium lost half the galaxy in two months. The loyalist Primarch Vulkan returned at this moment and led the counter-offensive. In the final battle, Vulkan and the Ork "Beast" destroyed each other — Vulkan used his Perpetual physiology to detonate his internal energies, blowing himself, the Beast, and the entire Ork battle-moon to pieces.

The Wars for Armageddon — Armageddon is the most famous war-world in the galaxy. Three great campaigns have been fought there. The first (early M41) saw the Daemon Primarch Angron lead the World Eaters in an invasion, repelled by the combined forces of the Space Wolves and the Grey Knights. The second (late M41) was the colossal Ork WAAAGH! led by Ghazghkull Mag Uruk Thraka. Ghazghkull is the most powerful Ork Warboss in the contemporary galaxy. After being struck in the head by a mysterious event, he began calling himself the "Prophet of Gork and Mork." His WAAAGH! swept through dozens of star systems until it was halted at Armageddon by Commissar Sebastian Yarrick — an old human commissar — using the Orks' own methods. Yarrick fought on with a severed arm, replaced his lost limb with a captured Ork power klaw, and generated something rare among Orks: fear. Ghazghkull personally honoured Yarrick as "Ol' Bale Eye" and deliberately let him go — because, by Ork logic, a fight without a strong enemy is no fight at all.

Ghazghkull remains active in the galaxy. His body has been destroyed and rebuilt multiple times; he is now less a single Ork individual and more a humanoid war-machine driven by rage and the WAAAGH!. Every major power is watching him: he is walking a path of the legendary "Ork Prophet," and if he succeeds, the next WAAAGH! may not be smaller than the War of the Beast.

## Fate & Present Status

After the War of the Beast and countless WAAAGH! large and small, the Orks remain one of the Imperium's most intractable threats — not because a single decisive battle can settle things, but because you can never fully cleanse them.

After the Great Rift tore the galaxy apart in M42, the Orks entered another golden age: everywhere there is war, everywhere there are enemies, which means everywhere there is fun. New Warbands and Warbosses have emerged on both sides — the Imperium Nihilus and the Imperium Sanctus. Some Ork Warbosses have even gone to fight Chaos — not out of loyalty to the Emperor, but because Chaos Space Marines also wear armour, also carry guns, and also fight back. Perfect.

The T'au Empire encountered Orks during its Fourth Sphere Expansion, and the result was catastrophic. A T'au diplomatic team, bearing the message of the Greater Good, attempted to negotiate with the Orks. They were tied to the front of a battlewagon as new decoration.

The Orks' greatest contradiction is this: they fight divided, but their nature dictates that sooner or later they will gather under a Warboss large enough, forming an unstoppable green tide. Ghazghkull was not the first, and will not be the last. What the Imperium can do is not "eliminate the Greenskins" — that is a joke — but to constantly, ceaselessly stamp out every Ork gathering before it grows too large to control. Year after year, millennium after millennium, without end.

## Verdict

Of all the galactic races, the Orks may be the only one for whom you feel no sorrow. Because Orks do not need your sorrow. They do not think they are miserable. They do not feel abandoned by the gods. They care nothing for Imperial propaganda, are unmoved by the temptations of Chaos, dismiss Aeldari elegance, and ignore Necron antiquity. They just wake up every morning — or pop out of a spore-sac — and find something to have a fight with.

If an Ork is blown to pieces on the battlefield, the expression on his face in the final second is probably not terror, but a toothy grin — because that explosion was damn exciting.

In this sense, the Orks may be the happiest intelligent race in the galaxy. The Emperor has sat on the Golden Throne for ten thousand years; Guilliman has processed paperwork for ten thousand years; the Aeldari cling to life in the shadow of Slaanesh; the Necrons search for lost souls within metal shells; the Tyranids are eternally hungry; Chaos wars against itself — and the Orks are just fighting. Fighting is their job, their religion, their art, their love. Orks have no existential anxiety, because they do not fear extinction. Orks have no fear of death, because they do not care who dies — themselves included.

Every civilisation in the galaxy chases meaning; the Orks are the only race that has already found it. Their meaning is very simple: fight. Roar WAAAGH! at the top of your lungs, and smash everything in front of you.

Perhaps the truth of the universe is exactly this: all complex philosophy, profound theology, and intricate politics will ultimately lose their meaning before a WAAAGH!. Because meaning is man-made, and fighting is written into the genes. The Orks were simply stripped of all pretences by the Old Ones, revealing life's most primal kernel.
