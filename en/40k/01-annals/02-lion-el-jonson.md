# 02 · Lion El'Jonson

## Overview

Lion El'Jonson, Primarch of the First Legion — the Dark Angels — was recovered among the last of the twenty brothers, yet commands the first and most secretive Space Marine Legion the Emperor ever created. He is called the Knight-Primarch and also the Double-Edged One, for two layers always coexist within him: absolute loyalty and absolute secrecy. His Legion wields forbidden technologies no other Legion possesses, and executes the darkest tasks the Emperor entrusts to no one else. His homeworld of Caliban has been destroyed, his foster-father became a traitor, and he himself slept for ten thousand years in a hidden chamber deep within the Rock — until the galaxy needed his sword once more.

## Origin

Of all the Primarchs, Lion had the most primal childhood.

Other Primarchs landed on civilized human worlds, were adopted by consuls or scholars, and grew up in palaces or academies. Lion landed on Caliban — a Death World blanketed in endless forests teeming with titanic beasts twisted by the touch of Chaos. No cities. No government. Only knightly strongholds scattered among forest clearings. Lion was found and adopted by a knightly Order in those forests, and his childhood was the hunt.

This determined everything about him. The beasts of Caliban's forests were not ordinary predators — they were Chaos-tainted monsters large enough to tear open a tank. A boy who survived alone in such a place learned two things very early: first, never show weakness; second, kill the threat before you speak to it. Lion's talent in these matters was overwhelming. Before reaching adulthood, he had become Caliban's greatest knight, surpassing every predecessor in the speed and scale of his hunts. Upon adulthood, he unified every knightly order on Caliban into a single army, cleared the deepest monsters from the forests, and made Caliban a world where one could walk the roads in safety for the first time.

Then the Emperor of Mankind arrived. Among the Primarchs, Lion was the second-to-last to be recovered. When the Emperor set foot on Caliban, Lion was already its king. There was no warm father-son recognition — to Lion, the Emperor was not "the father coming to take him home" but "a man stronger than himself whom he could choose to follow." He chose to follow. It was the most important decision of his life: he acknowledged the Emperor as his liege, and never spoke a second word of doubt.

## Deeds

### The First Legion and the Exterminators

The First Legion was no ordinary Legion.

Of the twenty Legions of the Great Crusade, the First was the earliest the Emperor assembled. It had been waging war across the galaxy long before the Emperor found any of the other Primarchs. So while the other Legions were still learning the basics of frontal assault, the First had already mastered methods of war unseen anywhere else in the galaxy. They possessed forbidden weapons left over from the Dark Age of Technology, including war machines linked to artificial intelligence — unthinkable under the framework of the Imperial Truth, yet the Emperor never ordered them destroyed. Instead, he sent them forth with those weapons to carry out the darkest of missions.

After Lion took command of the First Legion, it gained a name unknown to outsiders: the Exterminators. When the Emperor needed a world to be "resolved" rather than conquered — when a civilization could not be peacefully incorporated, could not be left as a prize of war, could not even be permitted to retain its historical record — he sent the First. Other Legions won victories. The First Legion made sure certain things ceased ever to have existed.

It was not glorious work. The Emperor did not send other Primarchs to do it because they were either incapable, unwilling, or would harbor secret resentment while obeying outwardly. Lion harbored no resentment. He never asked why — he only asked where. The Emperor said destroy, and he destroyed, clean and swift, with no internal friction whatsoever.

This also made Lion the least understood of all his Primarch brothers. The others had quarrels, alliances, grudges, and friendships — Lion participated in almost none of it. It was not coldness; it was that the habit of keeping everything inside had become instinct. He did the Emperor's work in the shadows. He neither needed explanation, nor ever offered it.

### The Horus Heresy: A Distant Vengeance

When the Horus Heresy erupted, Lion was leading the Dark Angels fleet on campaign in a remote region of the galaxy. This was the result of Horus Lupercal's careful planning — the Warmaster knew well the threat posed by the First Legion, and had dispatched Lion far from the Imperium's heartland, then used Warp Storms to delay his return. Lion received word of the betrayal in those distant stars, and took far longer than anyone else to fight his way back to Terra.

By the time he finally returned, the Siege of Terra was over. The Emperor lay mortally wounded. Sanguinius had been martyred. Horus had been erased from existence. Lion had missed it all. Like Guilliman, his greatest strength had gone unused at the most critical moment.

But Lion's vengeance did not arrive late. He wheeled his fleet and fell upon the Traitors' rear worlds, burning one Traitor homeworld after another to cinders. His counterattack was so ferocious that for thousands of years after the war, Traitor forces did not dare show themselves in the patrol sectors of the Dark Angels. He did not make it to Terra in time, but he ensured the Traitors held onto nothing after Terra.

### The Fall of Caliban

When Lion returned to Caliban with his fleet, waiting for him was the most lethal blow of all — a blade from his own side.

During his long campaign, his second-in-command Luther, left behind to garrison Caliban, had turned traitor. Luther had been Lion's foster-father. In Caliban's knightly tradition, Luther had been the one in the Order who most respected Lion, and the one Lion trusted most deeply. But when Lion was elevated by the Emperor to Primarch and led the Legion on campaign across the stars, Luther was left behind on Caliban — nominally to hold the world, in truth abandoned. The fissure in this man's heart had widened over decades into an abyss. Chaos seized that crack. When word of Horus's rebellion reached Caliban, Luther chose the Traitor side, and the entire garrison force followed him.

Lion's fleet was ambushed on descent. Primarch against foster-father. Loyalty against betrayal. The battle escalated into a duel between Lion and Luther. The psychic shock of that duel, combined with orbital bombardment, shattered the entire planet of Caliban into fragments. Luther was defeated, but Lion had suffered fatal wounds in the fighting.

The Dark Angels' Loyalists carried their dying Primarch into the largest fragment of Caliban — the asteroid that would later be converted into the fortress-monastery called the Rock. In a secret chamber deep within the Rock, they laid down the grievously wounded, unconscious Primarch. No one declared him dead, and no one knew when he would wake. Bound by oaths upon the Emperor's blade, by the traditions of the First Legion, they swore: this secret would never be revealed.

### Ten Thousand Years

Lion slept in the depths of the Rock for ten thousand years.

During this age, the galaxy passed through countless disasters and wars. The Imperium transformed from a rational empire into a theocratic hell: the Emperor was worshipped as a god, the Dark Angels were split from a Legion into countless Chapters, and every Primarch was either dead, lost, or vanished. By the close of the 41st Millennium, almost no one within the Imperium even remembered the name Lion El'Jonson — among the High Lords of Terra, no more than three knew of the existence of that hidden chamber.

But the Dark Angels themselves never forgot. For ten millennia, the Inner Circle — the highest-ranking few within the Chapter — guarded this secret generation after generation, and generation after generation hunted the Fallen: those Dark Angels who had followed Luther into betrayal. Scattered by Warp Storms across the galaxy and across different points in time, these renegades were the quarry of a hunt that became the Dark Angels' most important internal tradition. Every Chapter Master, upon elevation, was told in private: your Chapter has a secret. The Imperium must not know. The Inquisition must not know. No outsider can ever know.

Hunting the Fallen was at once faith and curse. For the sake of this secret, the Dark Angels have, over ten thousand years, even killed Imperial allies. Their loyalty is real. But their secret is real, too. The two are symbiotic, indivisible.

### M42: The Forest Walker

At the end of M41, the galaxy was torn in two by the Great Rift. The Imperium Nihilus sank into the long night of Warp Storm isolation.

Not long after the Thirteenth Black Crusade, the door in the deepest part of the Rock opened. Lion El'Jonson walked out.

Upon awakening, he did not immediately return to Terra, nor did he rush to announce his return. He began to wander the Imperium Nihilus, traveling from one world to the next, rallying scattered Loyalist forces and hunting Chaos warbands. Wherever he passed, a vague legend was left behind: a towering warrior called the Forest Walker, cloaked in dark green, bearing a greatsword that shimmered with starlight. He spoke to no one, left no name, but in the ruins after battle there always lay dozens of Traitor corpses.

Eventually, word reached Terra from the Imperium Nihilus. While Guilliman fought the forces of Nurgle in Ultramar, Lion arrived in the realm of the Ultramarines. For the first time in ten thousand years, two Primarchs stood face to face. It is said they spoke much, and remained silent much. They are not the best of friends — their personalities are each other's inverse on nearly every dimension — but they are the only beings of their kind in a mad age. Guilliman needed a blade in the Imperium Nihilus, and Lion was that blade. He was granted the title of Lord Regent, sharing rule of the Imperium with Guilliman: Guilliman managing administration and diplomacy, Lion overseeing military reconstruction in the Imperium Nihilus.

## Verdict

Of all the Primarchs, Lion El'Jonson is the hardest to define. He is not Sanguinius, the angel beloved by all; not Guilliman, the bureaucrat who saves the Imperium through institutions; not Rogal Dorn, the defender who turns himself into a living wall. He is the blade the Emperor kept for no one else to see. He does not need to be understood — he does not care. He works only in the dark, and when the work is done, he does not tell you.

But he is also a man who was stabbed in the back by the one person he trusted. Luther's betrayal left a wound deeper than any beast's claw ever could — because Lion spent his entire life trusting no one, and trusted Luther alone. The destruction of Caliban was not merely the physical death of a world; it was the collapse of Lion's entire worldview. The secrets he had hidden all his life exploded in his own home.

The Dark Angels are a mirror of Lion. They have hunted the Fallen for ten thousand years, not because those renegades pose some immense military threat, but because they must prove loyalty — with violence, with secrecy, sometimes with cruelty, proving inwardly and outwardly that they are not traitors. A Legion defined by secrets, living in a universe defined by secrets, forged by a Primarch who keeps half the truth hidden forever. This is the mode of existence of the Dark Angels.

Now Lion has awakened. The Imperium Nihilus needs him. Guilliman needs him. The Emperor, too, may have sensed from the Golden Throne that this silent son has risen once more. He did not return to the halls of glory. He walked once again into the depths of darkness. Ten thousand years, and he has done the same thing all along: in the places where no one sees, he has kept the oath he spoke to the Emperor all those years ago — "I follow you." He has never wavered.
