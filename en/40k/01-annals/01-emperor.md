# 01 · The Emperor of Mankind

## Overview

The Emperor, titled Master of Mankind, is the founder and sole ruler of the Imperium of Man in the 41st Millennium. He is the most powerful psyker in human history — an immortal Perpetual who has lived for over forty thousand years. Today he sits, neither truly alive nor dead, upon the Golden Throne on Terra, his broken body sustaining the Astronomican, the only beacon that guides human fleets through the Warp.

## Origin

8,000 BC. The Anatolian plateau — what is now central Turkey. Humanity still dwelt in the dawn of the Neolithic age, scattered in tribes whose spiritual anchors were their shamans. But the Warp was growing turbulent, and the shamans discovered that after death their souls could no longer reincarnate as before — they were being devoured by malevolent entities lurking in that psychic dimension.

And so an unprecedented collective sacrifice took place. Thousands of human shamans gathered together and simultaneously ended their own lives, fusing all their souls into a single vessel: the body of a newborn infant. That infant would become the Emperor.

From childhood he knew his purpose: to guide human civilization and shield the species from the Warp's corruption. Having inherited the souls and psychic might of thousands of shamans, he was born immortal — not merely ageless, but capable of resurrection even if slain. This constitution would later be called a Perpetual.

## Deeds

### Prehistory to the Dark Age of Technology

Throughout the long childhood of human civilization, the Emperor appeared in history under countless guises. Some have speculated he may have been Alexander the Great, perhaps Jesus, perhaps Leonardo da Vinci — no one can confirm it, and he himself never spoke of it. His principle remained constant: push from the shadows, never mount the throne in the open. He watched, waited, and at critical moments applied a gentle nudge.

During the Dark Age of Technology (roughly M15–M25), human civilization reached its first zenith — interstellar colonization, artificial intelligence, and genetic engineering all flourished. The Emperor's activities during this era are almost entirely absent from the record, but one thing is certain: when the AI rebellion known as the Men of Iron War destroyed interstellar civilization, and the Warp Storms that followed isolated every human world from every other, the Emperor began to realize that the age of waiting was over. He would have to act personally.

### The Unification Wars

Between M25 and M30, Terra was a shattered planet. Techno-barbarian warlords carved it into fiefdoms; radiation-scoured wastelands, mutants, and deranged cults sprawled across every continent. The Emperor no longer hid. Emerging from his secret bases beneath the Himalayas, he gene-forged the first super-soldiers — the Thunder Warriors — and led them in the Unification Wars.

It was a brutal lightning campaign. The Thunder Warriors were stronger and more ferocious than the Space Marines who would come after them, but also more unstable and shorter-lived. One warlord after another fell at the Emperor's feet, and at last Terra was unified beneath his banner. When the wars ended, the Emperor did something that posterity would regard as ruthless: he purged every last Thunder Warrior. They were too unstable for the Great Crusade that lay ahead.

### The Great Crusade

With Terra unified, the Emperor turned his gaze to the galaxy. In the long aftermath of the Warp Storms, countless human colony worlds had degenerated into barbarism in their isolation, or been enslaved by xenos. The Emperor launched the Great Crusade, circa M30 — the grandest military undertaking in human history.

His trump cards were twenty Primarchs — transcendent beings created from his own genetic template fused with Warp-derived techniques. Each Primarch embodied the ultimate expression of some human trait: war, governance, construction, intellect, mystery... But the Chaos Gods, without the Emperor's knowledge, snatched all twenty Primarch incubation capsules from the Terran laboratories and scattered them across the galaxy.

The Emperor did not relent. Using the genetic samples the Primarchs had left behind, he created the Space Marine Legions, then set out to find his twenty sons. Among those recovered, Horus Lupercal was the first, and the one who spent the longest at the Emperor's side. The Great Crusade advanced with gathering momentum — one human world after another was brought into the Imperial fold, xenos civilizations were crushed, and lost technologies were recovered by the Adeptus Mechanicus.

Toward the end of the Great Crusade, the Emperor made a decision that stunned the galaxy: he handed command of the entire crusade to his most trusted son Horus, naming him Warmaster, and returned to Terra. He announced publicly that he was undertaking secret research, but in truth, the Emperor was driving forward beneath the Imperial Palace the grandest project of his life — the Webway Project. He sought to open humanity's access into the ancient Aeldari Webway, so that human fleets would no longer depend on the peril of Warp travel.

### The Horus Heresy

But the Emperor's departure plunged Horus into an abyss. On the world of Davin, Horus was wounded by a xenos-corrupted blade called the Anathame. Through the machinations of the traitor Erebus, the festering wound drew Horus's soul into a Chaotic vision within the Warp. The Chaos Gods showed him a twisted future in which the Emperor became a god and the Primarchs were forgotten in the corners of history, their names and deeds erased. The Chaos Gods did not directly command him to rebel — they planted a seed: doubt. Why did the Emperor return to Terra? What was he doing there? Had he never intended to let the Primarchs share in the Imperium's future? Once these thoughts took root, they grew wild inside Horus. By the time he awoke, he was no longer the Horus who had been.

The Horus Heresy erupted. Nine Space Marine Legions followed Horus into rebellion, and the galaxy burned. The most savage engagement was fought at Isstvan V, where four Loyalist Legions were betrayed mid-drop by supposed allies and slaughtered almost to annihilation. Ferrus Manus, Primarch of the Iron Hands, was slain; his head was presented to Horus.

Meanwhile on Terra, Magnus the Red — the Emperor's fifteenth son, the most powerful psyker among the Primarchs — foresaw the rebellion and attempted to warn the Emperor through forbidden psychic sorcery. His spell shattered the seals on the Emperor's Webway Project, unleashing a flood of Warp entities that rendered the project a permanent, irrecoverable failure. The Emperor's response was to send Leman Russ to "bring Magnus back." But Horus secretly altered the order to "destroy." Prospero burned to ash, and the Thousand Sons were driven into the arms of Chaos.

The final confrontation unfolded above Terra. Horus led the Traitor forces in the siege of the Imperial Palace, and the Loyalists held the walls in blood and fire. Sanguinius — Primarch of the Ninth Legion, the Emperor's most beloved son — stood alone at the Eternity Gate, then was slain by Horus himself during the ultimate duel between the Emperor and the Warmaster. The death of Sanguinius shattered the Emperor's last hesitation. Unleashing the full measure of his psychic power, the Emperor erased Horus from every level of existence — not merely killed, but obliterated, so that the soul of this son could never exist again.

### The Golden Throne

After the final battle, the Emperor lay at the edge of death. He bore mortal wounds dealt by Horus; his soul was on the verge of collapse. Malcador the Sigilite, the Emperor's closest companion and Regent of the Imperium, had taken the Emperor's place upon the Golden Throne for the duration of the duel, sustaining the Astronomican with his own life force. When the Emperor returned, Malcador had already crumbled to ash on the Throne. His last words were "I do not regret."

The Emperor's ruined body was installed upon the Golden Throne — a device originally intended for the Webway Project, now transformed into an eternal life-support engine of torment. From M31 to the present day, ten thousand years have passed.

### Ten Thousand Years of Vigil

For ten millennia the Emperor's physical form has withered upon the Throne to little more than a skeleton, yet his psychic will has never flickered out. The light of the Astronomican burns on in the Warp, guiding every human fleet. His psychic presence reaches deep into the Warp, locked in an invisible spiritual struggle against the Chaos Gods.

Every day, thousands of psykers are sacrificed to the Golden Throne, their soul-fuel sustaining the Emperor's life-fire. This is the darkest and most sacred daily rite of the Imperium — the Black Ships patrol the galaxy, hunting down every psyker, the vast majority of whom are fed into the Throne chamber and reduced to ash.

## Fate & Present Status

At the close of M41 and the dawn of M42 — the so-called Era Indomitus — the Emperor's power appears to be undergoing some form of transformation. Living Saints such as Saint Celestine have begun appearing across the galaxy, resurrected and endowed with supernatural power directly by the Emperor's will. His interventions have grown more direct, more forceful.

When the resurrected Roboute Guilliman came before his father on the Golden Throne, he found that the Emperor was no longer the father who had pursued the Imperial Truth of atheistic rationalism — but a cold, almost inhuman godlike entity. The Emperor told Guilliman that the Primarchs had been merely tools, not sons. That coldness struck Guilliman with a grief from which he has not recovered.

Is the Emperor's power increasing, or is a more fundamental metamorphosis underway? Some theorize he is becoming a fifth Chaos God within the Warp — the entity the Chaos Gods call the "Anathema," at once their greatest enemy and something growing ever more akin to them in its essence.

## Verdict

The Emperor is the greatest and most contested being in human history.

He is the savior. Without him, human civilization would have perished in the chaos following the Dark Age of Technology. He unified humanity's cradle, launched the Great Crusade, and reconnected the countless scattered human worlds across the galaxy. That he has sustained the Astronomican with his broken body for ten thousand years is itself a feat beyond the limits of all comprehension.

He is also the cruelest tyrant. He purged the Thunder Warriors; he suppressed all religious belief with the Imperial Truth only to be worshipped as a god by billions of believers; his Imperium is a theocratic totalitarian state whose fundamental policies are "hate the alien, hate the mutant, hate the heretic." He created the Primarchs but treated them more as instruments than as sons — this coldness drove, directly or indirectly, the betrayal of at least nine of them.

Perhaps the deepest question is this: did the Emperor ever truly love his children? Horus saw in Chaotic visions a future in which the Emperor regarded all the Primarchs as chess pieces. Guilliman, after meeting his broken father upon the Throne, could not determine the truth either. Ten thousand years of agony, of solitude, of psychic fire — they have made "the Emperor of M30" and "the God-Emperor of M42" into two fundamentally different beings.

One thing is certain: so long as the Astronomican burns, the Imperium of Man still lives. And so long as the Emperor still sits upon the Golden Throne, the darkest age of the galaxy has not yet truly arrived.

> The Emperor is not a god. He is the greatest man who ever lived.

This was the Emperor's own conviction. It is also the truth the Imperium has long since forgotten.
