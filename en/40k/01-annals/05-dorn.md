# 05 · Rogal Dorn

## Overview

Rogal Dorn, Primarch of the Seventh Legion — the Imperial Fists. Among the twenty Primarchs, he is the least skilled at fine words, and the one who gave the Emperor the greatest peace of mind.

## Origin

Dorn landed on the ice world of Inwit, where he was adopted by the local ruling clan. Inwit had no forests and no beasts, only endless ice plains, subterranean caverns, and hard bedrock. Raised in such an environment, Dorn's character grew to resemble his homeworld — cold, hard, silent, but carrying a great inner heat.

Even as a youth he displayed a prodigious talent for calculation and organization, not only unifying Inwit itself but expanding outward to bring the many worlds of the Inwit Cluster into an orderly ice-kingdom. When the Emperor's fleet reached Inwit, Dorn was no longer a lost child waiting to be found, but a monarch who already had his own empire. He neither resisted nor knelt. According to the record, the gist of the first thing he said to the Emperor was: "I have been waiting for you."

The Emperor placed extraordinary trust in this taciturn son. He gave Dorn a task no other Primarch could have shouldered: build the ultimate defensive system for Terra — the Imperial Palace. That colossal fortress, stretching across the entire Himalayan plateau, was not only the Emperor's residence but humanity's final bastion. Every wall, every gun battery, every level of subterranean labyrinth came from Dorn's hand. When the Horus Heresy later erupted and the Traitor host reached the walls of Terra, it was precisely this defense network that held to the last hour.

## Deeds

### The Builder of the Great Crusade

During the Great Crusade, the Seventh Legion — the Imperial Fists — consistently drew the most thankless assignments. Other Legions sought glory on the field of battle; the Imperial Fists built walls and fortresses in the rear. Other Primarchs received cheers at victory banquets; Dorn commanded fleets guarding supply lines.

But this did not mean the Seventh Legion could not fight. Quite the opposite: in siege warfare and defensive warfare, the Imperial Fists were peerless among all Legions. Dorn's own architectural expertise allowed him to perceive structural weaknesses in enemy fortifications that others could not see, and his understanding of the garrison commander's psychology made his own defensive arrangements watertight. In ship-to-ship void warfare, the Seventh was equally supreme — Dorn regarded space fleets as mobile fortresses, every formation a solvable geometric problem.

One can put it this way: the Emperor's other sons were responsible for pushing the Imperium outward; Dorn was responsible for making sure that nothing pushed outward ever fell back.

### Nemesis: The Builder and the Destroyer

Of all his brothers, Dorn's relationship with Perturabo, Primarch of the Fourth Legion — the Iron Warriors — was the worst. Both excelled at siegecraft and defense, but their essences were opposites. Dorn built to protect. Perturabo built to prove himself superior to all others. The Emperor's regard for Dorn and his coldness toward Perturabo planted an eternal seed of jealousy and resentment in the latter.

This opposition reached its zenith in the Horus Heresy. Perturabo joined the Traitors and became the mastermind of the siege at the Siege of Terra. The sharpest spear in the world and the strongest shield now met — builder against builder, brother against brother. Dorn's walls held. Perturabo could not breach them. The enmity between the Iron Warriors and the Imperial Fists has thus endured for ten thousand years, evolving into the eternal war that later ages would call the Iron Cage.

### The Siege of Terra: The Longest Watch

During the Horus Heresy, Dorn was the Loyalists' supreme commander on Terra. It was perhaps the most agonizing role of his life — because he had to decide who lived and who died, had to allocate forces to the most desperate sectors, had to watch his soldiers and brothers fall one after another while he himself could only stand at the command post and keep calculating.

Throughout the Siege of Terra, Dorn barely closed his eyes. He moved ceaselessly along the walls, repairing every breach, redeploying every reserve. The enemy attacked simultaneously from the sky, from the ground, and from beneath the walls; every stone of the Imperial Palace was burning. Dorn was not beyond despair — but he never showed it. His soldiers, seeing their Primarch still standing on the battlements, knew the line had not broken.

There was another episode during the siege: Alpharius, Primarch of the Twentieth Legion, attempted to infiltrate Terra. In the battle on Pluto, Dorn slew one of the twin brothers with his own hand. Dorn would barely speak of this thereafter, but all who knew him understood that killing a brother had cut far deeper than it appeared.

### The Heaviest Gold

The Horus Heresy ended with the final duel between the Emperor and Horus. When Dorn led his men into the Throne Room, the fighting was already over — and he saw the most heartbreaking sight of his life.

His closest brother, Sanguinius, Primarch of the Ninth Legion — the angel, the Great Angel — lay on the floor in a pool of blood, slain by Horus's own hand.

The Emperor — his father, his liege, the one for whom he had built all of this — was barely alive, his body shattered, a hair's breadth from death.

It was Rogal Dorn who lifted the dying Emperor's body from the floor, carried it through the wreckage of the Palace, step by step toward the Golden Throne. It was his hands that installed the Emperor upon that machine, which was at once life support and eternal prison. Before the installation, the Emperor, in his last moment of lucidity, instructed Dorn on how to maintain the Golden Throne.

Then the Emperor closed his eyes, and never opened them again.

Dorn never walked out of that moment. He built the Imperial Palace but could not protect the man inside it. He defeated the besieging enemy but lost the battle that mattered most. This self-reproach was beyond anyone's power to console. He was too silent — silent to the point of being unable to confide and unable to be saved.

### The Iron Cage: A Penance by Blood

After the Horus Heresy, Dorn did something no one could understand. He led the Imperial Fists in a mass assault upon the defensive positions Perturabo had prepared in the Eternal Fortress star system, launching the Iron Cage campaign. In essence, this was a suicidal charge — Dorn was not trying to defeat Perturabo tactically. He was punishing himself in the cruelest trench warfare possible, purging himself with blood.

The Imperial Fists suffered catastrophic casualties in that campaign, nearly to the point of annihilation. Perturabo, deep in his fortress, watched Dorn's warriors charge wave after wave to their deaths and felt a twisted satisfaction. In the end, when both sides had exhausted themselves to the breaking point, Dorn ordered a withdrawal. He had not won. He had never meant to win. What he had wanted was to bleed. To bleed it all out.

### Disappearance and the Severed Hand

After that, Dorn continued to serve the Imperium for many years. He oversaw the breaking of the Legions, dividing the Imperial Fists into multiple successor Chapters in the Second Founding. He fought in the Ork Wars. He guarded the Emperor's legacy, silently fulfilling the duties of a Primarch who still lived.

Later, during a Chaos Black Crusade incursion, Dorn led a small fleet into battle against a vast Chaos armada. The fighting was unimaginably savage. When reinforcements arrived, Dorn was no longer on the battlefield — no body, no last words. Only a severed hand remained, the skeletal structure still recognizable as his signature bionic hand.

The Imperial Fists took that hand back and enshrined it in the deepest sanctum of their Chapter. For ten thousand years, they have refused to acknowledge that Dorn is dead. Their official position: Dorn is missing, awaiting the moment of his return somewhere. Every Imperial Fists Chapter Master, upon taking office, carves his name into the bones of that severed hand, as a sign that he inherits Dorn's will.

No one knows whether he is alive or dead. Perhaps he truly wanders somewhere. Perhaps he has been consumed by Chaos. Perhaps he was simply tired and chose a corner where no one could find him, sitting quietly, continuing to calculate those defense plans that can never be finished.

## Fate & Present Status

See above. Missing in action; the severed hand is enshrined by the Imperial Fists.

## Verdict

Rogal Dorn left behind no resplendent speeches, no autobiography or philosophical treatise, no legendary tales for posterity to savor. What he left behind was humbler.

He left behind the walls of Terra. Ten thousand years later, the Imperial Palace defensive system that Dorn built still operates, still the most formidable fortress of the Imperium of Man. The gun emplacements where he stood during the siege, patching walls while shouting orders to his soldiers — Imperial Guardsmen still stand guard at those positions today.

He left behind the Imperial Fists. This Chapter, renowned for siegecraft and defense, remains to this day the warfare force most skilled at defensive operations. Their gene-seed carries the imprint of Dorn's tenacity — not flashy, not flexible, no clever tricks, just hold the line, hold until the last moment.

Even the hardest stone is worn away by time. But what Dorn built, brick by brick, still protects Terra. For a silent architect, this is perhaps the finest epitaph there is.

Or perhaps he is not dead yet. The Imperial Fists have believed this for ten thousand years.
